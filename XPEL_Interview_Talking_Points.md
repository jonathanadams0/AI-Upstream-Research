# Research Vault — Talking Points (XPEL FP&A Interview)

*One-line pitch:* "I've built and run a disciplined, driver-based forecasting process across ~58 companies — modeling, scenario analysis, and variance tracking. FP&A is that same process pointed inward at one business I can know deeply."

---

## What it is (the 10-second version)
- **AI Upstream Research Vault** — maps the full AI infrastructure stack: power generation → chip manufacturing → data-center real estate.
- **58 rated companies · 8 sectors · 16 investment theses · DCF models · live dashboards.**
- Built and maintained solo, end-to-end: sourcing data, building models, forming a view, tracking it, and grading myself.

## Structure (if asked to walk through it)
- **Theses** — 16 written investment cases tying a driver to an outcome (e.g., gas-turbine backlog pricing power, HBM supply constraint).
- **Sectors** — 8 primers with competitive maps.
- **Companies** — 58 rated notes + earnings prep/briefs.
- **Models** — DCF models (GEV, VST, CEG, NRG) + estimates tracker + valuation snapshots.
- **Dashboard** — coverage tracker, model portfolio, **Decisions Log**, catalyst playbooks, **position-sizing framework**, verification worklist.
- **Monitors** — dated portfolio snapshots + thesis stress-tests.

---

## Translate it to FP&A language (most important)
| What I call it | What it is in FP&A terms |
|---|---|
| Driver-based revenue models | Driver-based forecasting / the planning model |
| Earnings previews + estimate/guidance tracking | **Variance analysis** — forecast vs. actual, then diagnose the gap |
| Bull / base / bear cases | **Scenario & sensitivity planning** with explicit assumptions |
| Model portfolio + Decisions Log | **Forecast accountability** — post-mortems and lessons-learned |
| Thesis stress-tests / invalidation checks | Risk identification and re-forecasting triggers |
| Synthesizing filings, transcripts, data feeds | Turning messy inputs into a defensible number |

## Proof points to drop naturally
- "I track my estimates against what companies actually report and diagnose *why* I was off — that variance loop is the core habit."
- "Every position has a written thesis and a sizing rationale, and I log the decision so I can grade it later."
- "I keep dated monitors and stress-tests, so the forecast is a living thing, not a one-time spreadsheet."

## Handling the sector gap (be proactive)
- My coverage is AI/semis/power, **not** auto aftermarket — name it first, then neutralize:
- "The companies differ; the process transfers — decompose the P&L into drivers, build the forecast, track the variance. I'd love to point that at XPEL's channel mix and margin bridge."
- Signal I've already started: China direct-distribution shift, OEM/dealer channel mix, the manufacturing/supply-chain investment, premium ~23x valuation = forecasting accuracy matters.

## Watch-outs
- **Lead with forecasting, variance, and business-partnering — not stock-picking or price targets.** Valuation is the least FP&A-relevant part.
- Keep it about *how I think*, not *what I picked*.
- Have **one** note ready to walk through end-to-end (a clean initiation or earnings prep) if they want depth.
