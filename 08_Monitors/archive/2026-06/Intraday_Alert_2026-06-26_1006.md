## Catalyst Analysis

### ON Semiconductor (ON) — ⚠️ THESIS REVIEW REQUIRED

**Catalyst:** ON announced an all-stock acquisition of Synaptics (SYNA) for ~$7 billion. Shares fell ~21%.

**Why it matters:** The thesis for ON was SiC power electronics for AI data center UPS systems and EV drivetrains — a focused, high-margin business with design-win visibility. Synaptics is an IoT/HMI/touchscreen controller company with no SiC exposure, no AI data center exposure, and no EV traction. This acquisition does not extend the core thesis; it dilutes it. Multiple analyst comments characterize the deal as "diluting the data center focus."

**Mechanics:** All-stock transaction means existing ON shareholders absorb dilution. At ~$93/share, the stock is now 16.4% above the $80 bear case — the smallest margin of safety since initiating coverage.

**Thesis trigger check:** The three formal invalidation triggers in the ON note (EV volume decline at top-5 OEMs, automotive guidance cut >15%, competitive displacement by Wolfspeed/STMicro) have not fired. However, a $7B acquisition into an unrelated vertical is a capital allocation event that the thesis did not contemplate. The note's stated bear scenario assumed thesis breaks via EV/SiC competitive dynamics — not a strategic pivot. **This warrants a formal re-underwrite ahead of the Q2 call (July 28).**

**Current vs. bear:** $93.13 / $80 bear = +16.4% cushion. If the deal signals a management pivot away from SiC focus, or if integration risk pressures margins, the bear case narrows.
