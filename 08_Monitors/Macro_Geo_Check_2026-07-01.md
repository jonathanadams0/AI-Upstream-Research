## Vector 1 — Korea

**Samsung Electronics:** No Q2 print yet (prelim flash due ~Jul 7–8, full report ~Jul 30–31 per calendar). However, Samsung has already guided full-year 2026 semiconductor capex to **>₩110 trillion**, versus ₩47.5T in 2025 — a **+128% YoY increase**, described as the largest single-year semiconductor capex commitment in history. Driven by HBM4 scale-up, closing the foundry yield gap with TSMC, and advanced packaging buildout. This is a massive capex **raise**, not a cut — clears the >10% raise bullish-confirm threshold by a wide margin. Q1 2026 results (reported) showed chip profit up ~48x YoY on HBM demand. First HBM4E samples targeted for Q2.

**SK Hynix:** Next earnings ~Jul 24 (calendar). Guidance so far: DRAM shipments +high-single-digit% QoQ, NAND +mid-teens% QoQ in Q2. 2026 capex "expected to increase significantly" vs 2025, with ₩45.5T earmarked for new Korea fab capacity (Yongin Cluster/M15X) and ₩11.9T for EUV scanner procurement. HBM4 ramp is proceeding to the agreed customer schedule; 7th-gen HBM4E samples targeted for H2 2026, mass production 2027 — no delay language found. Net: capex raise, no HBM4 delay signal — clears bullish-confirm / no-breach.

**TrendForce (NAND/DRAM contract pricing):** Q2 2026 DRAM contract prices guided **+58–63% QoQ**; NAND **+70–75% QoQ** — both accelerating off Q1's +90–95% (DRAM) and ~60% (NAND) prints. This is an extreme supercycle pricing signal, the opposite of the >10%/>20% decline thresholds in the playbook. Driver: AI server demand, CSPs locking in long-term supply agreements, capacity reallocation toward HBM/enterprise SSD away from commodity NAND/DRAM.

**Read:** All three inputs are firmly bullish-confirm. No capex cuts, no HBM4 delay, no NAND/DRAM price erosion — the opposite condition (severe supply tightness/price surge) is in effect.

## Vector 3 — US-China Export Controls

**BIS / Federal Register:** No new rule expanding equipment controls to DUV or additional etch/dep categories found in the past 30 days. Background context: a January 2026 rule already softened the licensing posture for advanced computing semiconductors to China (presumption of denial → case-by-case review) — this predates the window but remains the operative policy backdrop.

**Dutch government / ASML — new development this window:** On Jun 24, 2026, Dutch officials confirmed they are "irritated" by a **new US proposal** to impose further export restrictions on ASML's China business; Dutch cabinet members traveled to Washington for consultations. This is a US-driven push for tighter restrictions, not a Dutch license non-renewal — the Netherlands is actively resisting the US proposal rather than acquiescing. Status: **proposal stage, not enacted.** This does not meet the "Dutch license non-renewal" breach threshold, but it is a live risk to track — flagging as WARNING.

**Japan METI / Tokyo Electron:** The MATCH Act (introduced in the US House in April 2026, outside this 30-day window) would force Japan to align its export controls with the US within 150 days of enactment or lose access to "country of concern" markets for its equipment makers. No new June development found — Japan has not yet issued a formal response; the bill has not been reported as passed. Separately, China added 40 Japanese companies to its own export-control/unreliable-entity list back in April, also outside this window. No new US-China-Japan escalation specifically in June.

**US-China diplomacy (past 30 days):** Net tone is de-escalatory. Xi Jinping called 2026 a potential "historic, landmark year" for US-China ties during talks with Trump in Beijing. Under the evolving trade agreement, China ended antitrust/anti-dumping/anti-monopoly investigations targeting US semiconductor firms, and the US reduced certain fentanyl-related tariffs by 10pp with Section 301 exclusions extended through November 2026. Countervailing note: on Jun 9, the US added Alibaba, Baidu, and BYD to a list of Chinese military-linked companies (restricts US defense contracting exposure to these names, not semiconductor equipment export licensing) — limited direct relevance to LRCX/AMAT/ASML/ENTG/TOELY. A Jun 2 USTR Section 301 proposal for a 12.5% tariff relates to forced-labor issues, not semi equipment.

**Read:** No enacted BIS/Entity List/Dutch breach in the window. The standout new risk is the US proposal to tighten ASML restrictions further (Dutch resisting) — genuine tail risk to track but not yet a triggering event. Broader trade-diplomacy trend is constructive. Net: WARNING, not BREACH.
