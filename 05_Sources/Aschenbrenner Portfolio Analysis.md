## Core Thesis

> "The real bottleneck to AGI is not algorithmic but physical. Electricity generation and computing capacity will be the most valuable assets of the coming decade."

Aschenbrenner's insight, derived from his deep knowledge of AI training infrastructure, is that the AI buildout will be ultimately constrained by:
1. **Electricity** — not enough dispatchable, firm power near where data centers need to be built
2. **Compute capacity** — GPU clusters and the physical infrastructure to house/cool/power them

This led him to invest in power companies and infrastructure companies rather than the obvious AI chip names.

## Where We Diverge

**Primary disagreement: AVGO (Broadcom)**

We rate Broadcom **Buy/$560**. Aschenbrenner has **$1.01B in put exposure** against AVGO.

His thesis: AVGO's valuation at ~35-40x forward earnings discounts too much of the custom silicon opportunity; if hyperscaler AI capex digests or moderates, Broadcom's custom silicon revenue could disappoint.

Our counter: Broadcom's custom silicon programs (Google TPU, Meta MTIA, Apple Neural Engine) are multi-year design commitments with high switching costs. Even if new custom silicon wins slow, the existing programs provide 3-5 year revenue visibility. The networking ASICs business provides additional diversification. We believe 35x is defensible for this earnings quality and visibility profile.

**Resolution:** This is a genuine professional disagreement. Aschenbrenner's short is a *pairs trade* — he's long infrastructure, short chips, so his AVGO short is hedging his portfolio rather than being an outright bear call. Our Buy is outright constructive. Both can be right simultaneously if infrastructure outperforms chips (which it has).

## Portfolio Construction Lessons

**What the Aschenbrenner fund teaches:**

1. **The biggest alpha is in the re-rating, not the ongoing growth.** Bloom Energy's extraordinary returns came not from fundamental earnings growth but from the *multiple re-rating* as the market recognized it as AI infrastructure rather than industrial equipment.

2. **The paired trade structure is disciplined.** By going long infrastructure and short expensive chip names, Aschenbrenner captures the *relative value* between the two. Even if both go up, the infrastructure outperforms. Even if both go down, the chips fall more.

3. **The "old-economy price, new-economy thesis" is repeatable.** Bloom, CAT, GNRC, STX, WDC — all priced as old-economy businesses with new-economy demand. The pattern is the basis of the [[AI Bottleneck Screener]].

4. **Concentration in conviction.** The fund holds ~24-30 positions in a $13.7B book — extremely concentrated. This is not a diversification strategy; it's a concentrated bet on a single macro thesis.
