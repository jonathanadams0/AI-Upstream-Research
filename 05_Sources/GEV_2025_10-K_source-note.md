## Capital Structure — Critical for WACC

### Debt
> "Total debt, excluding finance leases, was less than $0.1 billion and $0.1 billion as of December 31, 2025 and December 31, 2024, respectively."

**GEV essentially has zero debt.** This is material.

### Credit Facilities (Undrawn)
- **Revolving Credit Facility:** $3.0B, five-year unsecured, matures April 2, 2029. Available in USD and EUR. $500M sub-limit for letters of credit. **$0 drawn** as of 12/31/25.
- **Trade Finance Facility:** $3.0B, for standby LCs and bank guarantees. **Not utilized.**

### Prolec Acquisition — Pending Debt Issuance
- Announced Oct 21, 2025; expected close **February 2026**
- Purchase price: **~$5.3B at closing**
- **Funding: ~50% cash, ~50% debt** → approximately $2.65B of new debt issuance
- Prolec brings: ~10,000 employees, 7 manufacturing sites (5 in US), $4B of standalone backlog
- Grid equipment supplier → transformers across all voltages

**Analyst note:** This fundamentally changes the 2026 capital structure. Build your WACC using **target** capital structure, not 2025 actual. Suggested target: $2.5B debt / $260B market cap ≈ 1% D/(D+E), still trivial. **WACC will be essentially Ke even post-Prolec.**

- 2025: $53M — this represents LC fees + finance lease interest, not corporate debt
- Do NOT use this to back into Kd for WACC

## Tax & Pension — Critical Modeling Inputs

### Normalized Tax Rate
The 2025 effective tax rate of **(72.5%)** is meaningless due to the $2.9B valuation allowance release. Reference the deferred tax reconciliation:
- Pre-tax income 2025: $2,828M
- Tax provision at 21% US statutory: $594M
- **Actual tax provision: ($2,051M)** including $2,907M valuation allowance release
- Without the VA release, implied normalized tax expense: ~$856M → **normalized ETR ~30%**

For DCF, use **~25-28% forward tax rate** (slightly below US statutory + state due to mix of foreign earnings, but GEV's foreign mix is now taxed in US too per disclosure footnote).

### Pension — Underappreciated Tailwind
From Note 13:
- **Net periodic benefit income 2026E: $0.5B** (principal pension plan, estimate)
- Principal pension plan 2025: net income of $(327)M (i.e., pension is contributing $327M of non-operating income)
- Other pension: $(30)M
- Principal retiree benefit: $(55)M
- **Combined 2025 non-operating benefit income: $459M** (recorded below the operating line)
- Contribution required: $0 to principal pension in 2025; ~$91M expected to other pension plans in 2026

**Analyst note:** This $459M of annual non-operating income is real and recurring. It flows through to net income and FCF. Many analysts mis-model this by either:
1. Including it in operating income (overstates operating margin)
2. Excluding it from net income projections (understates EPS and FCF)

The correct treatment: non-operating income in the P&L; include in DCF as non-operating cash flow or via its net income contribution.

Pension is **underfunded** (principal pension deficit: $1,272M) but no required contributions until 2028.

## Legal & Contingent Issues

### Vineyard Wind — Material Risk
> "On December 22, 2025, the United States Department of Interior announced that it is pausing the leases for all large-scale offshore wind projects under construction in the United States, which had a direct impact on the Vineyard Wind project completion timeline."

**2025 Offshore Wind contract losses: $637M**
**2024: $1,005M**
**2023: $379M**

Losses include blade removal/replacement, schedule extension, quality remediation. Wind segment goodwill is $3.3B with fair value only 27% above carrying value — this is an impairment watch item. If offshore issues escalate, could trigger a Wind goodwill impairment.

### GE Indemnification Overhang
- ~$8B of RPO still covered by GE parent company guarantees (down 77% since Spin-Off)
- $347M of restricted cash from GE related to legacy GE legal matters
- Tax Matters Agreement restricts strategic transactions that could jeopardize Spin-Off tax-free treatment (2-yr lookback for Section 355(e))

### Restructuring
- July 2025 restructuring plan announced ("the Plan") — targeting $225-250M in costs, $250M in annual savings
- Substantially complete by mid-2026
- 2025 restructuring charges: $285M (Workforce $193M + plant closures $53M + other $38M)

## What I'd Flag for Investment Committee

1. **Valuation vs. historical margins.** GEV at 56x P/E and growing ~10% organic. Even with Electrification segment at 15% EBITDA margin, consolidated Adj EBITDA margin is 8.4%. Total EBITDA of $3.2B on $267B market cap = ~83x EV/EBITDA. You need to believe in significant margin expansion (to 12-15% consolidated) and the durability of the order cycle to justify the multiple.

2. **Offshore wind is a known risk, Vineyard pause is new.** Wind segment has had >$2B in cumulative contract losses over 3 years. Goodwill buffer only 27%. Watch Q4 2026 annual impairment test.

3. **Zero debt + $8.8B cash + $3.7B FCF + pending $2.65B Prolec debt** means GEV enters 2027 with massive financial flexibility for either M&A or accelerated buybacks. Capital allocation optionality is real.

4. **Pension income is $459M of "free" earnings** not in segment EBITDA. This creates a material gap between Adj EBITDA and net income that analysts often miss.

5. **Tax normalization in 2026.** The reported ETR will jump from -72.5% to +25-28% in 2026 — any analyst model showing 2026 earnings growth needs to normalize 2025 net income before computing YoY.

6. **Hyperscaler customer concentration & credit risk.** 10-K explicitly flags unique risk from "nontraditional customers... with limited operating histories, different contracting practices, or weaker credit profiles." Not a near-term issue but a structural one.
