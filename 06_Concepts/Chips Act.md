# CHIPS and Science Act

The CHIPS and Science Act (2022) is a US federal law providing $52.7B in subsidies and $24B in investment tax credits to incentivize domestic semiconductor manufacturing. The Act was a direct response to US dependence on Taiwan (TSMC) for advanced chip production.

**Key programs:**
- **CHIPS for America Fund ($39B):** Construction grants for semiconductor fabs in the US
- **CHIPS Manufacturing Tax Credit (25%):** Investment tax credit for semiconductor equipment purchases
- **National Semiconductor Technology Center:** R&D facility for advanced chip research

**Who benefits in the portfolio:**
- [[ENTG]] — specialty materials for chip manufacturing qualify for the domestic supply chain buildout
- [[MU]] — Micron's Idaho DRAM/HBM fab is CHIPS Act-supported
- [[AMAT]], [[LRCX]], [[KLAC]] — US-made equipment qualifies for the 25% tax credit, incentivizing customer purchases
- [[ON]] — domestic SiC manufacturing benefits from related critical infrastructure provisions

**The strategic logic:** Advanced chip manufacturing in the US reduces vulnerability to Taiwan Strait geopolitical risk. TSMC Arizona, Samsung Austin, and Intel Ohio fabs are all CHIPS Act beneficiaries — each requiring domestic equipment, materials, and gases from the companies in this portfolio.
