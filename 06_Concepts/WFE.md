# WFE — Wafer Fab Equipment

Wafer fab equipment (WFE) is the collective term for all tools used to manufacture semiconductor chips: lithography systems (ASML), etch tools (LRCX, TEL), deposition equipment (AMAT, LRCX), and process control/metrology (KLAC). WFE spending is the leading indicator of future chip capacity, running 18-24 months ahead of production.

**2026 WFE market:** ~$140B+ (Lam Research CEO, Bernstein May 2026) with "bias to the upside." KLA's 2030 target: $215B ±$20B. Prior cycle peak: ~$100B (2022).

**Why WFE matters for the portfolio:** Every dollar of WFE spending flows through [[LRCX]], [[AMAT]], [[KLAC]], [[ASML]], and [[TOELY]]. WFE is the indirect demand signal for AI — if hyperscalers order more GPUs, TSMC orders more WFE to build them.
