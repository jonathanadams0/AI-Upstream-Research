## Candidate 1: Babcock & Wilcox (BW) ⭐⭐⭐ HIGH CONVICTION

**One-line:** 160-year-old industrial boiler company that signed a $2.4B definitive contract to build four 300 MW natural gas plants for Applied Digital's AI factory — and then saw its bookings increase 1,900% YoY. Priced as an industrial manufacturer. Should be priced as AI power infrastructure.

### 5-Criteria Screen

| Criterion | Assessment | Pass? |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Physical bottleneck** | Designs and manufactures industrial boilers, heat recovery steam generators, and steam turbines — the core equipment inside every natural gas-fired power plant. No AI data center can use a gas power plant without BW's equipment. | ✅ PASS |
| **2. Old-economy multiple** | Industrial boiler manufacturer, 160 years old. Historically priced as a cyclical industrial with commodity-like contract margins. EV/EBITDA ~10-15x appropriate for old BW. | ✅ PASS |
| **3. Hyperscaler contract signal** | February 26, 2026: definitive agreement signed with Base Electron (backed by Applied Digital) for four 300 MW plants = 1.2 GW total. Contract value: $2.4B. Revenue recognition already begun ($31M in Q1 2026). In active discussions with "other hyperscaler customers" for additional contracts. Pipeline: $14B+ total. | ✅ PASS |
| **4. Supply can't be replicated quickly** | Industrial boiler engineering and manufacturing requires decades of specialized metallurgical, thermal, and fabrication expertise. BW has 160 years of this. A new entrant can't replicate this in 3-5 years. | ✅ PASS |
| **5. Backlog growing faster than revenue** | Q1 2026 bookings: $2.5B (+1,900% YoY). Backlog: $2.7B (+483% YoY). Revenue: +44% YoY. The backlog/revenue ratio is expanding rapidly — exactly the Bloom Energy pattern signal. | ✅ PASS |

### Key Data Points

- **Revenue Q1 2026:** $214.4M (+44% YoY); $31M from Base Electron contract already recognized
- **Bookings Q1 2026:** $2.5B — up 1,900% from Q1 2025
- **Backlog Q1 2026:** $2.7B — up 483% from Q1 2025
- **Total pipeline:** $14B+ including additional hyperscaler opportunities
- **Contract:** Applied Digital/Base Electron — four 300 MW natural gas plants, total $2.4B, completion targeted 2029
- **Balance sheet:** Net debt reduced 87% in Q1 2026 alone. Net debt now below 1x TTM EBITDA.
- **Connection to existing coverage:** Base Electron is backed by [[APLD]] (Applied Digital, Buy in vault). BW builds the power plants that APLD then operates to supply AI workloads.

### Why the Market Hasn't Priced This In

BW has a complicated history — serial restructuring, legacy pension liabilities, debt refinancing cycles. The market still prices BW as a distressed industrial recovering from balance sheet issues. The $2.4B AI contract is transformative but the stock ran 23% on the day of Q1 earnings (May 11) and is still not widely covered as an AI infrastructure name. The pipeline ($14B) is 5x+ current revenue — the repricing hasn't happened.

## Candidate 2: Williams Companies (WMB) ⭐⭐ MEDIUM-HIGH

**One-line:** The largest US natural gas pipeline company has quietly become a hyperscaler power developer. Five commercialized behind-the-meter power projects including "Neo" (682 MW, $2.3B, 12.5-year contract with a top-tier hyperscaler). Building at 5x EBITDA multiples; guiding 10%+ earnings CAGR through decade-end.

### 5-Criteria Screen

| Criterion | Assessment | Pass? |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Physical bottleneck** | Pipeline infrastructure = fuel delivery for every natural gas AI power plant. Pipeline permits take 3-7 years; WMB has 78,000 miles already built. Data centers can't get gas without pipelines. | ✅ PASS |
| **2. Old-economy multiple** | Midstream pipeline company, EV/EBITDA ~11-13x — utility-like but still industrial multiple. Not priced as a power developer. | ✅ PASS |
| **3. Hyperscaler contract signal** | 5 signed contracts: Socrates (400 MW/Meta/$1.6B, online H2 2026), Apollo ($1.5B, H1 2027), Aquila ($1.6B, H1 2027), a 4th project, and Neo (682 MW/$2.3B, 12.5-year, H2 2028). Plus announced Transco Power Express pipeline. | ✅ PASS |
| **4. Supply can't be replicated** | Pipeline right-of-way acquisition + FERC permitting takes 5-10 years. WMB's Transco system is irreplaceable east coast gas infrastructure. | ✅ PASS |
| **5. Backlog growing faster than revenue** | Power Innovation projects represent $7B+ incremental investment. Moderate — growth is real but more gradual than BW's step-change. | ⚠️ PARTIAL |

### Key Data Points

- **Socrates project:** 400 MW, $1.6B, 10-year fixed PPA with Meta. In-service H2 2026.
- **Apollo + Aquila:** Two more Ohio projects, ~$3.1B combined, H1 2027 in-service.
- **Neo (Q1 2026 announcement):** 682 MW — largest project to date. 12.5-year contract, 5th commercialized power innovation project. ~$2.3B investment. H2 2028 in-service.
- **Build multiple:** 5x EBITDA on all projects — attractive for midstream
- **Gas demand 2026 guidance:** 17% total US gas demand growth through 2030 (company estimate)
- **Current issue:** WMB is a large-cap (~$60B market cap). Less "under the radar" than BW. The market is aware of the Power Innovation business.

### Why This Is Different From Just Being a Pipeline Company

WMB is now a power developer that provides the full stack: gas supply + pipeline + power generation + 10-year PPA. This is structurally different from just transporting gas. The "Power Innovation" business unit is a new revenue model that earns power generation returns (5x build multiple) rather than just pipeline throughput fees. If this business scales to $5-10B of investment by 2028, it's worth 2-3x pipeline multiples — a meaningful re-rating.

## Structural Observations from Screener

**1. Copper wire/cable as an emerging bottleneck (no listed pure-play found)**
Multiple sources confirm: Goldman forecasts 640,000-tonne copper deficit; Wood Mackenzie confirms hyperscalers "outbidding grid suppliers on transformers"; each Microsoft data center requires ~2.2 Kt of copper. US-listed pure-plays in electrical wire for infrastructure are limited. Encore Wire (WIRE) is the closest but is primarily residential/commercial construction. Watch: Prysmian ADR, Nexans ADR for international cable exposure.

**2. GOES steel — one US producer, all import-dependent**
Wood Mackenzie confirms: US relies on single domestic GOES producer (AK Steel, now Cleveland-Cliffs CLF). 30% shortage of CRGO steel hitting transformer manufacturers. CLF is a diversified steel company — data center GOES is one product line. Not a clean pure-play but worth monitoring.

**3. Williams/Kinder Morgan "Power Innovation" model is new**
Both WMB and KMI are pivoting from pure gas transport to integrated power development for hyperscalers. WMB's 5x EBITDA build multiple is attractive. This is structurally different from just benefiting from higher gas throughput. The model is: midstream company builds and owns behind-the-meter gas power plants + provides gas supply under one bundled contract. This competes directly with GEV turbines, BE fuel cells, and CAT generators for the same customer dollar.

**4. Cooling — no new public pure-plays, but 2 acquisitions signal trend**
Johnson Controls (JCI) acquired Alloy Enterprises (thermal management) in May 2026. Daikin acquired Chilldyne (negative-pressure liquid cooling) in November 2025. Large HVAC companies are buying into AI data center cooling — consistent with VRT/MOD thesis but no new names to add.
