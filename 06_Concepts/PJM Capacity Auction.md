# PJM Capacity Auction (RPM)

## What It Is

The PJM Reliability Pricing Model (RPM) is a forward capacity market operated by PJM Interconnection — the grid operator covering 13 states from Illinois to New Jersey (including major data center markets: Virginia, Pennsylvania, Ohio, Illinois). The RPM runs auctions approximately 3 years ahead of delivery, procuring commitments from generators to be available to produce power during peak demand periods.

Capacity auctions are distinct from energy markets. In energy markets, generators are paid for actual electricity produced ($/MWh). In capacity markets, generators are paid simply to be *available* — a fixed annual payment per MW of committed capacity regardless of whether the plant actually runs, analogous to an "option premium" paid by the grid operator.

## Why It Matters for the Portfolio

Capacity payments are a significant and predictable revenue stream for [[VST]] and [[CEG]] — both of which hold large generation fleets in PJM. Changes in cleared capacity prices directly affect East segment earnings, which are otherwise subject to energy price volatility.

The July 2026 PJM capacity auction will set capacity payments for the 2027/2028 delivery year — a meaningful forward earnings signal for VST and CEG. Prior auction (2025/2026 delivery): cleared at $269.92/MW-day. The next auction (2027/2028) is expected to clear significantly higher given tightening reserve margins driven by data center load growth.

## How the Auction Works

1. **Base Residual Auction (BRA):** Held ~3 years before the delivery year. Generators submit capacity offers; PJM clears the auction at the price needed to procure the required MW of reserve margin.
2. **Incremental Auctions:** Held closer to delivery to adjust for changes in load or resource availability.
3. **Clearing price determination:** PJM uses a demand curve (the Variable Resource Requirement, or VRR curve) that sets the maximum price PJM is willing to pay for capacity. If supply is tight (fewer MW offered than needed), price clears higher on the VRR curve.

## Reserve Margin Tightening

PJM's reserve margin — the buffer between available generation capacity and peak demand — has been tightening due to:
- **Retirement of fossil plants:** Coal retirements in PJM (EPA regulations, economics)
- **Renewable intermittency:** Wind and solar count as partial capacity (their capacity value = average output during peak hours, not nameplate capacity)
- **Data center load growth:** PJM is experiencing 2-3% annual weather-normalized load growth, driven by Northern Virginia data centers (the largest data center market in the world by volume)
- **New generation delays:** New gas and nuclear plants take years to permit and build

Result: PJM reserve margins are declining toward the minimum threshold, which mechanically drives higher capacity auction prices.

## Historical Capacity Prices

| Delivery Year | Clearing Price ($/MW-day) | Key Context |
|---|---|---|
| 2018/2019 | ~$160 | Oversupply; gas glut |
| 2020/2021 | ~$100 | COVID demand reduction |
| 2022/2023 | ~$50 | Low (oversupply persisted) |
| 2023/2024 | ~$35 | Near record low |
| 2024/2025 | ~$50 | Beginning of recovery |
| 2025/2026 | **$269.92** | Significant step-up; tightening reserves |
| 2027/2028 (next auction, July 2026) | **Expected materially higher** | Data center load growth; coal retirements |

The 2025/2026 jump to $269.92 (from ~$50) was a significant positive surprise for VST and CEG, and represents a structural recognition that PJM's reserve margins are tightening. Management at both companies has noted the next auction could clear even higher.

## VST and CEG's PJM Exposure

**[[VST]] East segment:** Perry (1,240 MW), Davis-Besse (894 MW), Beaver Valley (1,710 MW), Braidwood (2,386 MW nuclear) + gas generation in PJM. Total PJM economic capacity ~6,500+ MW. At $269/MW-day × 365 days × 6.5 GW = ~$640M annual capacity revenue. If next auction clears at $329/MW-day (a scenario cited in Q3 2025 earnings call commentary): ~$780M — an incremental ~$140M.

**[[CEG]] (Constellation):** ~22 GW nuclear fleet heavily concentrated in PJM. Constellation is the largest single participant in the PJM capacity market by MW. Each $50/MW-day increase in clearing price adds ~$400M to annual Constellation capacity revenues.

**[[NRG]] East segment:** Gas fleet in PJM; benefits from higher clearing prices.

## Why July 2026 Auction Is a Catalyst

The July 2026 auction clearing price for the 2027/2028 delivery year will be disclosed publicly. If it clears above $300/MW-day (as Fitch and management commentary suggest is possible), it would:
1. Immediately provide multi-year earnings visibility for PJM-exposed generators
2. Likely trigger analyst estimate upgrades for VST and CEG
3. Confirm the tightening reserve margin narrative that underpins VST's Buy thesis

Fitch (October 2025) cited PJM as proposing a collar of $175-$325/MW-day for the next two auctions, which implies the upper end would be a positive outcome. The exact clearing price depends on how many MW of capacity is offered at what prices.
