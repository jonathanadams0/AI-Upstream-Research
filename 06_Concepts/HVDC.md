# HVDC — High Voltage Direct Current

## What It Is

High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) transmission is a method of transmitting large amounts of electricity over long distances using direct current (DC) rather than conventional alternating current (AC). HVDC lines carry electricity at voltages typically ranging from ±200 kV to ±1,100 kV.

## Why HVDC Matters

**Long-distance efficiency:** AC transmission loses energy to resistance at roughly 3% per 1,000 km. HVDC losses are approximately 50-70% lower over equivalent distances. For transmission lines spanning hundreds to thousands of kilometers, HVDC is the economically dominant choice.

**Offshore wind connection:** Every offshore wind farm beyond ~50 km from shore uses HVDC cables to transmit power to shore. The rapid growth of offshore wind (North Sea, US East Coast) is a major HVDC demand driver.

**Subsea interconnects:** Cross-border or cross-sea electricity trading (UK-Norway, Nordic-Continental Europe, future Atlantic cable concepts) requires HVDC cables.

**Grid stability:** HVDC can instantly and precisely control power flow, helping stabilize AC grids that would otherwise be vulnerable to cascading failures.

**Data center relevance:** Hyperscalers building large campuses in renewable-rich but population-sparse areas (West Texas wind, Appalachian solar) need HVDC to efficiently deliver that clean power to load centers. Several proposed long-distance HVDC projects in the US target exactly this use case.

The global HVDC market is a duopoly at the high end:

| Company | Coverage | Notes |

## GEV's HVDC Position

GE Vernova's Electrification segment includes HVDC converter stations — the AC-to-DC and DC-to-AC conversion stations at each end of an HVDC line. These are among the highest-value components in an HVDC project.

GEV has disclosed significant HVDC backlog growth as part of the Electrification segment's acceleration. The Q1'26 Electrification data center order of $2.4B included HVDC systems alongside transformers and switchgear.

- US SunZia HVDC line: 3,000 MW of wind from New Mexico to Arizona/California (Pattern Energy, using GEV equipment)
- North Sea offshore wind HVDC connections: Major European projects using Siemens Energy and Hitachi Energy
- Atlantic Wind Connection: Proposed offshore HVDC "backbone" for US East Coast offshore wind

## Market Size and Growth

The global HVDC market is estimated at $15-20B annually, growing at 8-12% CAGR through 2030, driven by:
- Offshore wind (largest single driver)
- Long-distance renewable integration
- Grid resilience and interconnection investment
- Data center-adjacent transmission infrastructure
