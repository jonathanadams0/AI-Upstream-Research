# Interconnect Queue

**Category:** Power Markets / Regulation

---

## What It Is

The backlog of generation and load projects waiting for grid interconnection studies and approvals from regional transmission operators (RTOs) like PJM, ERCOT, MISO, CAISO. Each project requires impact studies before it can connect, and queues have ballooned as renewables, storage, and data center loads overwhelm RTO processing capacity.

---

## Why It Matters

- PJM queue has years of backlog
- Data centers that can't interconnect can't operate — this is the binding constraint on hyperscaler site selection
- Drives premium for sites with existing interconnection rights (e.g., co-location at existing power plants — see [[CEG]] TMI deal)
- FERC Order 2023 attempts to reform the process (cluster studies, commercial readiness requirements)

---

## Implications for Coverage

- Incumbents with existing interconnection rights are re-rated upward
- Grid EPCs ([[PWR]]) benefit from buildout to clear queues
- Behind-the-meter generation deals bypass the queue entirely
