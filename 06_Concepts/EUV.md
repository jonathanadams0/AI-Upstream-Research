# EUV — Extreme Ultraviolet Lithography

EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet Lithography) uses 13.5nm wavelength light (vs. 193nm for conventional DUV) to pattern features below 7nm on semiconductor wafers. Only one company makes EUV tools: **ASML** (Netherlands). Every leading-edge AI chip (Nvidia H100, A100; Apple M-series; Google TPU) is manufactured using EUV.

**Why it's a monopoly:** EUV requires a plasma light source that generates 13.5nm photons, tin droplet targets, extremely precise mirrors operating in high vacuum, and alignment systems accurate to atomic-scale tolerances. ASML has been developing EUV for 30+ years and no competitor has come close.

**High-NA EUV:** The next generation of EUV with a higher numerical aperture (0.55 vs. 0.33 for current Low-NA). Enables patterning below 2nm. First systems being tested at customer fabs in 2025-2026.

**Portfolio link:** [[ASML]] is the only public company with EUV exposure. Every leading-edge logic chip (TSMC N3/N2) requires multiple EUV passes per wafer.
