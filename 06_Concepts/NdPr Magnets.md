# NdPr Magnets — Neodymium-Praseodymium Permanent Magnets

NdPr (Neodymium-Praseodymium) permanent magnets are the strongest permanent magnets available and are used in motors, generators, speakers, and hard drives. NdPr oxides are extracted from rare earth ore (primarily monazite and bastnäsite) and processed into metal for magnet manufacturing.

**Why NdPr matters for AI:**
- **EV motors:** Each EV requires ~1 kg of NdPr in the motor's permanent magnets
- **Wind turbine generators:** Direct-drive offshore turbines use ~600 kg NdPr per turbine
- **Data center cooling fans and drives:** HDD write heads and cooling motors both use NdPr
- **AI robot motors:** Physical AI (robotics) will dramatically increase NdPr demand as it scales

**Supply concentration:** China processes ~85% of global NdPr. Western governments (US, EU, Japan, Australia) are actively building alternative supply chains. [[MP Materials]] Mountain Pass is the only US mine and separation facility at scale.

**Price volatility:** NdPr prices are set primarily in China (domestic quotas affect global supply). High prices historically attracted Chinese export restrictions and market manipulation.
