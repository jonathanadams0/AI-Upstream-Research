# AI Bottleneck Screener Run — June 2026 (Q3 Quarterly)

> Second run of the quarterly cadence (the inaugural run was [[Screener Run - June 2026]], dated 2026-06-04, which surfaced BW). This Q3-cadence refresh re-scans Q1/Q2 2026 earnings, 8-Ks, and trade press for industrial/old-economy names quietly becoming AI-infrastructure plays and NOT already in vault coverage. Seven candidates screened. **No 5/5 this run.** The headline finding: the obvious data-center-contractor cohort (MEP, switchgear, site-dev, cooling) has *largely re-rated* since the June 4 run — so the alpha gap (Criterion 2) has closed on most. The remaining edge sits in names the multiple has *not* caught up to: **EMCOR (EME)** and the deep-value materials/lodging names **Cleveland-Cliffs (CLF)** and **Target Hospitality (TH)**.

## Candidate 1: EMCOR Group (EME) ⭐⭐ HIGH (4/5)

**One-line:** The same hyperscaler data-center backlog story driving Comfort Systems (FIX, ~44x) and Sterling (STRL, ~50x), but EMCOR still trades at ~24x forward — the one name in the data-center contractor cohort where the industrial-to-AI multiple gap has *not* fully closed. Record remaining performance obligations of $15.6B (+33% YoY), with the network & communications (data-center) book at ~$4.3B.

### 5-Criteria Screen

| Criterion | Assessment | Pass? |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Physical bottleneck** | Mechanical, electrical & plumbing (MEP) construction and field services — the skilled-trade execution layer every hyperscale data center must buy. Skilled electrical/mechanical labor at scale is a genuine throughput constraint on how fast campuses energize. | ✅ PASS |
| **2. Old-economy multiple** | ~24x forward P/E (2026 EPS est ~$27.41). Historically EME traded 12–18x; it has re-rated *partially* but remains below the framework's 25x bar — and far below FIX (~44x) / STRL (~50x) running the identical playbook. This is the surviving gap. | ✅ PASS (the cheapest of the cohort) |
| **3. Hyperscaler contract signal** | Record RPO $15.62B (+32.9% YoY); network & communications segment ~$4.3B, explicitly data-center-driven. Demand described as sustained across data centers, manufacturing, healthcare. Type 2/3 evidence (segment-level disclosure rather than a single named 8-K contract). | ✅ PASS |
| **4. Supply can't be replicated quickly** | Skilled-trade labor pools, multi-region branch footprint, and bonding/safety track records are a real but *not insurmountable* barrier (competitors FIX/EME/API compete head-to-head). Moderate. | ⚠️ PARTIAL |
| **5. Backlog growing faster than revenue** | RPO +33% YoY vs. 2026 revenue guide $18.5–19.25B (~12–15% growth). Ratio ≈ 2.2–2.5x → clears the ≥2x bar. | ✅ PASS |

### Why This Is the Find of the Run
The June 4 run flagged the *power* side (BW, WMB, KMI). This run, the screenable edge has rotated to the *construction/execution* side — and the market has already paid up for FIX, STRL, POWL and AAON. EMCOR is the anomaly: identical data-center backlog tailwind, larger absolute RPO than any peer, yet still a ~24x multiple because it is perceived as a sprawling diversified MEP/industrial-services conglomerate rather than an "AI infrastructure" pure-play. That perception gap *is* the trade. Anti-pattern check: this is not "exposed to AI" hand-waving — the $4.3B network/comms RPO is a hard, disclosed number.

## Candidate 2: Powell Industries (POWL) ⭐⭐ Medium-High (4/5)

**One-line:** Custom-engineered electrical switchgear maker that just booked the largest order in company history — a $400M+ data-center mega-order post-Q2-FY26 — lifting backlog to $1.8B at a 1.7x book-to-bill. Passes four criteria cleanly; the only miss is that the market already knows (stock +265% since June 2024; ~30x forward).

### 5-Criteria Screen

| Criterion | Assessment | Pass? |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Physical bottleneck** | Custom medium-voltage switchgear and integrated electrical distribution — the gear that steps and distributes power inside the data center. Long-lead, custom-engineered, capacity-constrained. | ✅ PASS |
| **2. Old-economy multiple** | ~30x forward P/E (vs. ~17x electrical-industry average). Already re-rated through the framework's 25x ceiling — the repricing the screener hunts for has substantially happened. | ❌ FAIL |
| **3. Hyperscaler contract signal** | Q2 FY26: booked a mega data-center order >$75M *and* a mega utility order; post-quarter awarded a >$400M data-center order (largest in Powell history). New orders $490M in Q2; total DC orders >$100M booked in Q1. Type 1/2. | ✅ PASS |
| **4. Supply can't be replicated quickly** | Custom switchgear engineering, UL/spec qualification, and constrained skilled assembly capacity = multi-quarter lead times competitors can't compress quickly. | ✅ PASS |
| **5. Backlog growing faster than revenue** | Backlog $1.8B with 1.7x book-to-bill; orders growing well ahead of revenue recognition. | ✅ PASS |

### Why Medium-High (Not High)
POWL is a textbook bottleneck name on Criteria 1/3/4/5, but it fails the criterion that *generates the alpha*: the multiple. At ~30x forward the market has reclassified POWL as an AI-electrical play. Per the framework's anti-pattern #3 ("if analysts have already re-rated the stock to AI multiples, the trade is likely over"), the asymmetric entry has narrowed. Keep on the bench as a pullback candidate and a read-through for the broader switchgear bottleneck (also benefits ETN, HUBB, NVT already in coverage).

## Candidate 3: Cleveland-Cliffs (CLF) ⭐ Medium / Watch (3/5)

**One-line:** The *sole* domestic producer of grain-oriented electrical steel (GOES) — the irreplaceable core material in every grid and data-center transformer — wrapped inside an out-of-favor, deeply cyclical steelmaker trading at a distressed multiple. Just committed $150M to a new Weirton, WV distribution-transformer plant to move downstream into the bottleneck it already feeds.

### 5-Criteria Screen

| Criterion | Assessment | Pass? |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Physical bottleneck** | GOES is the magnetic steel at the heart of transformer cores. The US transformer shortage is acute; CLF (Butler Works, PA) is the only domestic GOES source — "no real #2." Directly addresses the [[Transformer Supply]] / [[NdPr Magnets]]-adjacent materials bottleneck. | ✅ PASS (strong) |
| **2. Old-economy multiple** | Diversified steelmaker; cyclical, frequently near break-even, depressed EV/EBITDA and ambiguous/negative P/E. Trades nowhere near AI multiples — passes the "priced as old-economy" test trivially. | ✅ PASS |
| **3. Hyperscaler contract signal** | No direct hyperscaler contract. GOES sells to transformer OEMs, not to MSFT/AMZN directly. The new $150M transformer plant + record GOES/NOES demand commentary is Type 3 (new product line / end-market narrative). | ⚠️ PARTIAL |
| **4. Supply can't be replicated quickly** | Sole US GOES producer; GOES production is metallurgically hard and import-dependent elsewhere. Replication is multi-year and capital-intensive. Among the strongest moats in the screen. | ✅ PASS (strong) |
| **5. Backlog growing faster than revenue** | CLF is a spot/contract steel seller, not a backlog discloser; GOES is a small slice of a large diversified steel P&L. No clean backlog>revenue signal. | ❌ FAIL |

### Why Watch (Not Higher)
CLF is the inverse of POWL: it *passes* the cheapness and irreplaceability tests but the AI-bottleneck product (GOES) is a minority of a noisy, commodity-cyclical, debt-heavy steel business. You'd be underwriting the whole steel cycle to own the GOES optionality. That's a thesis dilution, not a pure-play. Monitor whether management carves out / discloses electrical-steel economics separately — that disclosure would be the re-rating catalyst. Cross-ref the prior run's structural note on GOES (single domestic producer / CRGO shortage).

## Candidate 4: Target Hospitality (TH) ⭐ Medium / Watch (3/5)

**One-line:** Workforce-lodging operator that signed a $550M+ multi-year committed-revenue contract with a top-five hyperscaler to house ~4,000 workers building a North Texas data-center campus (8-K, April 2026). Cheap (~22x EV/EBITDA), a hard named-hyperscaler signal, and a step-change versus a ~$365M revenue base — but the "bottleneck" is soft.

### 5-Criteria Screen

| Criterion | Assessment | Pass? |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Physical bottleneck** | Houses the construction labor force at remote mega-campus sites. Real adjacency, but per anti-pattern #1, lodging is *exposure to* the build-out, not a *physical constraint on compute*. The campus gets built with or without TH (other lodging providers exist). | ⚠️ PARTIAL |
| **2. Old-economy multiple** | ~22x EV/EBITDA; historically a low-multiple workforce-housing/lodging name tied to oil & gas man-camps. Not priced as AI infrastructure. | ✅ PASS |
| **3. Hyperscaler contract signal** | $550M+ committed-revenue, multi-year contract with a *named-tier* (top-5) hyperscaler for a Data Center Hub in North Texas; +$20–40M/yr variable upside. Disclosed via 8-K Apr 2026. Type 1 (strongest evidence class). | ✅ PASS (strong) |
| **4. Supply can't be replicated quickly** | Owns deployable modular lodging assets and turnkey hospitality ops at scale, but modular housing is comparatively quick to replicate; barrier is speed/existing fleet, not technology. | ⚠️ PARTIAL |
| **5. Backlog growing faster than revenue** | The $550M committed contract is large versus FY26 revenue guide of ~$365M (raised from $325M on this win) — a genuine step-change in contracted revenue. | ✅ PASS |

### Why Watch (Not Higher)
TH is the most *Bloom-shaped* on the signal mechanics — cheap multiple + discontinuous named-hyperscaler 8-K + step-change to a small base. But it fails the screen's spirit on Criterion 1: housing crews is one degree removed from a physical compute bottleneck, and the moat (Criterion 4) is thin. This is a credible event-driven trade, but a *softer* structural thesis than a true materials/power bottleneck. Worth a tearsheet to test whether the committed-revenue annuity + variable occupancy upside justifies a re-rate, and whether a second hyperscaler hub follows.

## Structural Observations from This Run

**1. The contractor/switchgear/cooling cohort has re-rated — the screener worked, then the market caught up.** In ~7 weeks since the June 4 run, FIX, STRL, POWL and AAON all pushed through the 25x ceiling. The framework's anti-pattern #3 is the operative lesson: once the AI multiple arrives, the screen should *stop* flagging the name as a fresh idea. The surviving edge is the laggard in an otherwise-re-rated group — here, **EME**.

**2. "Materials moat, commodity wrapper" names (CLF) are a recurring screen artifact.** Like the June run's copper/GOES notes (Encore Wire, CLF), the genuine irreplaceable-supply story keeps showing up *inside* diversified cyclicals with no clean pure-play. The re-rating catalyst for these is *segment disclosure*, not a contract. Flag to watch: any CLF move to separately report electrical-steel economics.

**3. Event-driven "AI-adjacent services" (TH) are appearing.** Workforce housing, on-site catering, security, water trucking — the services layer around mega-campus construction is starting to sign named-hyperscaler contracts. These screen cheap with strong Type-1 signals but weak Criterion-1 bottleneck purity. Treat as event/catalyst trades, not structural bottleneck theses.

**4. No 5/5 this quarter.** Unlike the inaugural run (BW, 5/5), nothing cleared all five. That is the honest read: the easy industrial-multiple-to-AI-multiple gaps in the data-center *build* cohort have mostly been arbitraged in the last quarter. Per Step 5 of the loop, **no Dashboard initiation To-Do is appended** (that trigger is reserved for 5/5 names, as BW was).
