# Uranium Long-Term Contracting

Nuclear utilities purchase uranium fuel through long-term contracts (typically 5-15 years) rather than spot market purchases. Long-term contract prices are negotiated privately between utilities and miners, and carry a premium above spot in supply-constrained environments.

**Why it matters more than spot:** Cameco's realized uranium price reflects its long-term contracted book — currently $66.21/lb (Q1'26) vs. a volatile spot market. Long-term contracts provide predictable revenue regardless of short-term spot price swings.

**The current contracting cycle:** Utilities that deferred uranium procurement after Fukushima (2011) are now urgently re-contracting as reactor lifetimes extend and new builds advance. Competition for Cameco's limited supply has pushed long-term contract prices substantially above the historical average.

**Enrichment vs. uranium:** Uranium contracts cover mined concentrate (U3O8 or "yellowcake"). Separately, utilities must also contract for conversion (UF6) and enrichment (SWU). The full fuel cycle from mine to reactor requires 3-5 years, meaning utilities must plan far ahead.
