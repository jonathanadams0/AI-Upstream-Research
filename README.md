## Research Approach

The vault is organized around a top-down, thematic framework. Each position begins with a macro investment thesis — identifying a structural supply/demand imbalance or secular tailwind — then narrows to fundamental company analysis, competitive positioning, and valuation. The process is designed to be repeatable and falsifiable.

**Thesis → Sector → Company → Valuation → Monitoring**

1. **Thesis construction** — Identify a structural imbalance (e.g., transformer lead times hitting 3.5 years, HBM capacity constrained by etch intensity). Document the mechanism, the beneficiaries, and the conditions that would invalidate the thesis.
2. **Sector primers** — Competitive landscape maps, key KPIs, and company positioning within the sector before initiating individual coverage.
3. **Company notes** — Business overview, revenue model, competitive moat assessment, risk factors, rating, price target, and conviction level. Structured frontmatter enables cross-portfolio data queries.
4. **Valuation** — DCF models for highest-conviction names; relative valuation snapshots across the broader universe.
5. **Active monitoring** — Catalyst playbooks track upcoming events (earnings, auctions, regulatory decisions) with pre-defined price targets and asymmetry ratios. Portfolio monitors capture intraday and close-of-day reactions.

## Investment Theses

Each thesis documents a structural mechanism, the primary and derivative beneficiaries, the bear case, and the conditions for thesis invalidation.

1. **Nuclear Renaissance via Hyperscaler PPAs** — 20-year contracts re-pricing nuclear from commodity to contracted asset; hyperscalers paying for carbon-free baseload at scale
2. **Transformer Supply Crunch** — 120–180 week lead times driven by AI/grid/electrification demand surge; GEV Prolec the primary beneficiary
3. **Gas Turbine Backlog Pricing Power** — Oligopoly (GEV/Siemens/MHI) sold out through 2029; new-price orders running 10–20pts above backlog average
4. **Liquid Cooling Mass Adoption** — Physics constraint: next-gen GPU racks exceed air cooling limits, driving mandatory liquid/direct-liquid cooling adoption
5. **WFE Supercycle** — $140B+ wafer fabrication equipment in 2026; multi-vector demand from HBM, NAND, leading-edge logic, and advanced packaging
6. **HBM Supply Constraint** — HBM etch intensity compounds with each GPU generation; Lam Research dominant in the critical process steps
7. **Advanced Packaging Bottleneck** — CoWoS capacity constrains AI GPU supply; every TSMC expansion creates proportional equipment demand
8. **Copper Structural Deficit** — 10–15 year mine-to-production lag vs. AI/EV/grid demand growing 3–4% annually; long-dated structural imbalance
9. **Rare Earth Decoupling from China** — Bipartisan US policy + EU Critical Raw Materials Act creating structural demand for MP Materials and domestic processing
10. **Fuel Cells and On-Site Power** — Bloom Energy, Caterpillar, Generac providing power outside grid interconnection queues for data center operators
11. **Miners to AI Hosts** — Bitcoin miners (Core Scientific, Applied Digital, IREN) repurposing stranded power infrastructure for GPU hosting
12. **AI Networking and Custom Silicon** — Broadcom/Marvell custom ASICs displacing merchant silicon; AI Ethernet ecosystem (Arista/Coherent/Credo) growing alongside InfiniBand
13. **Memory Bottleneck** — HBM, NAND, and HDD represent distinct constrained layers of the AI data hierarchy, each with different supply dynamics
14. **SiC Power Conversion** — Silicon carbide enables AI-density power conversion efficiency; onsemi positioned as the transitional winner vs. GaN
15. **Uranium Supply-Demand Inflection** — Mine supply recovering from decade of underinvestment against structurally growing nuclear demand; Cameco as the primary liquid vehicle
16. **Vertiv as the AI Cooling Pure-Play** — Vertiv's thermal management and power infrastructure positioned as the most direct beneficiary of rack density growth
17. **AI Cluster Networking** — High-speed interconnect (800G/1.6T) as the emerging bottleneck in AI training clusters
18. **Custom Silicon** — Hyperscaler in-house ASIC programs (Google TPU, Microsoft Maia, Meta MTIA) as a structural shift in semiconductor demand composition
