# FCEL — Q2 FY2026 Earnings Preview

**Reports:** June 8, 2026
**Price at report:** $21.40 (~$1.1B market cap)
**Coverage:** 4 Buy / 13 Hold / 2 Sell | Consensus PT $8.73 (mean)

→ Full report: [[FCEL_Earnings_Preview_Q2FY2026.docx]]

---

## Key Debate

The most binary event in the AI power sector: **does FCEL announce a signed hyperscaler contract?** The stock at $21.40 is pricing in a contract win that hasn't happened. Consensus PT of $8.73 represents 59% downside; bull case PT of $12 is still 44% downside. The thesis is validated — but by Bloom Energy (BE), not FCEL.

---

## The Bull Case

- 1.5 GW of pricing proposals submitted Q1 FY26 alone; 80%+ tied to data centers
- Grid interconnection advantage: 18–24 months from contract to power vs. 5+ year queue for traditional grid
- Torrington manufacturing scale-up: 100 MW/yr → 350 MW/yr target
- Inuverse MOU: 100 MW for AI data center in South Korea (2027)

## The Bear Case

- No signed hyperscaler contract as of Q1 FY2026 report (March 9, 2026)
- Bloom Energy is the dominant player — 2 GW/yr capacity, $2.52B cash, signed Oracle (2.45 GW) / Nebius ($2.6B) / AEP+AWS (1 GW)
- FCEL has **negative gross margins** — can't scale profitably without large contracted volume
- Manufacturing constraint: a single 360 MW hyperscaler contract would require 3–4x current capacity
