## MRVL — Marvell Technology

**One-line variant:** Consensus values Marvell as a semiconductor company with an AI growth kicker. We value it as a dedicated AI infrastructure designer with an expanding TAM — custom silicon, co-packaged optics, and PAM4 DSPs are not cyclical chip businesses.

**Consensus says:** MRVL is a semiconductor company with $6–8B forward revenue at 12–15x revenue = $72–120B market cap → ~$150–250/share.

**We say:** Custom silicon design wins don't stop at Amazon and Microsoft. The 50+ new opportunities management disclosed in Q2 FY26 represent the next wave. Each win is a 5–7 year committed revenue stream. At $370 and our $11B revenue estimate: 17x NTM revenue. For a business growing 40%+ with 5-year locked revenue, that's reasonable — not expensive.

**Revenue trajectory:**
- FY2025 (Jan 2025): ~$5.5B run-rate
- FY2026 (Jan 2026): ~$8–9B run-rate
- FY2027 (Jan 2027) guidance: ~$11–12B run-rate

**Key sources:** MRVL Q1–Q3 FY2026 earnings; MRVL 10-Q Q3 FY2026

## BE — Bloom Energy

**One-line variant:** Consensus is valuing post-Oracle Bloom Energy using pre-Oracle metrics — a miss particularly severe because the 2026 guidance raise (to $3.4–3.8B, +80% YoY) and the manufacturing capacity buildout to 2 GW/yr represent a structurally different company than 2024.

**What changed in 2025–2026:**
- Oracle: 2.45 GW signed contract
- Nebius: $2.6B contract
- AEP/AWS: 1 GW
- Manufacturing: ~500 MW/yr → 2 GW/yr capacity
- 2026 revenue guidance: $3.4–3.8B (+80% YoY) with positive EBITDA

**Key distinction from FCEL:** Bloom has *signed contracts*, *manufacturing capacity*, and *positive margins guidance*. FCEL has proposals and MOUs. The AI fuel cell thesis is validated — the beneficiary is BE, not FCEL.
