Executive Summary

The core issue: MU prints Q3 FY2026 on June 24 AMC against guidance of $33.5B revenue / $19.15 EPS — but its last three quarters beat guidance by +21%, +28%, and +28% on revenue and +68%, +39% respectively on EPS. The market is not trading against the guidance number; it is trading against a whisper of ~$36–38B revenue and ~$22–25 EPS. The single most important number on the call is Q4 FY2026 guidance: the Street needs ≥$42B to hold the stock at current levels. With MU at ~$1,142 vs. a vault PT of $1,100 (stock now above PT), this is a binary event with options implying an 11–18% move.

Story of last quarter in 5 bullets:
  • Revenue beat guidance by $5.16B (+27.6%). This was not a guidance beat — it was a guidance demolition. AI datacenter DRAM demand and HBM ASP expansion drove both volume and price simultaneously. The $18.7B midpoint proved to be a floor.
  • Gross margin hit 75%, +300bps above guided 72%. HBM carries materially higher margins than commodity DRAM, and the mix is shifting rapidly. The Q3 guide of 81% implies yet another +600bps sequential expansion — the sustainability of this trajectory is the critical question for this print.
  • HBM revenue annualized run rate exceeded $8B. Volume shipments of HBM4 for NVIDIA Vera Rubin began in Q2, ramping at 2x the pace of HBM3E. Entire CY2026 HBM supply is locked at pre-agreed pricing and volumes — unprecedented visibility for a memory company.
  • Stock fell 3.8% the next day despite the massive beat. Classic buy-the-rumor / sell-the-news. The stock had already run +70% in 2026. Over the subsequent 83 days, however, MU drifted +113.8% as the earnings power magnitude became evident to the market.
  • Dividend raised 30%; board signaling sustained strength. Capital return via dividends from a cyclical memory company is a qualitative signal of management confidence — but also warrants scrutiny on reinvestment opportunity cost at peak cycle.

8. Macro & Sector Backdrop

  • POSITIVE DRAM contract prices surged 90–95% QoQ in Q1 CY2026 (TrendForce, May 2026). NAND prices up 55–60%. The pricing environment is the strongest in memory history — structural, not cyclical, driven by HBM demand cannibalizing DRAM commodity supply.
  • POSITIVE WFE forecast raised from >20% to >30% growth for CY2026 (AMAT, May 15). Most constructive WFE guidance in a decade. Implies capacity investments being pulled forward across all memory segments.
  • POSITIVE HBM4 production ramp running 2x faster than HBM3E. Volume shipments to NVIDIA Vera Rubin begun. MU is the only confirmed HBM4 supplier at volume for the next-generation GPU platform — a competitive moat not present in HBM3E.
  • POSITIVE AI infrastructure capex not decelerating. All major hyperscalers raised CY2026 capex guidance (AWS, Azure, Google). Each incremental AI server contains ~5–10x more DRAM content than a traditional server.
  • POSITIVE LRCX confirmed NAND conversion investments accelerating. If NAND etch spending is rising, NAND ASPs are inflecting — a catalyst for MU H2 margin improvement in NAND, the 21% revenue laggard.
  • NEUTRAL Samsung HBM market entry delayed but not absent. Samsung struggling with HBM3E yield issues. Creates near-term HBM supply tightness benefiting MU and Hynix, but Samsung will eventually qualify and compress long-term HBM pricing.
  • WATCH China export restrictions constrain NAND addressable market. NAND flash demand from Chinese data centers limited by export controls. Ongoing drag on NAND volume and limits MU's fastest-growing geographic exposure.
  • WATCH MU is now trading above vault PT ($1,142 vs. $1,100). Stock has outrun our target. A Q4 guidance disappointment from elevated levels could produce a sharp de-rating — asymmetry favors caution on incremental sizing.
