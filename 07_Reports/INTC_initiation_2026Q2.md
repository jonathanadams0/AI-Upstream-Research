> **Live Event Note:** We are initiating on the day Trump announced an Intel-Apple foundry deal using Intel's 18A process. Neither company has formally confirmed. Our analysis reflects both the strategic significance and the current valuation context.

---

## One-Line Verdict

Intel is executing the most credible industrial turnaround in semiconductor history — 6 straight beats, a historic strategic coalition ($8.9B government + $5B NVIDIA + Apple foundry deal) — but at $121 and ~$590B market cap, the stock is already pricing in the full thesis at >100x FY2026E EPS. Hold: the turnaround is real, the valuation reflects it.

---

## Five Key Tensions

1. **Apple deal = historic 18A validation vs. 15–20M units is economically immaterial** — $500M–$1B revenue on a $590B market cap
2. **DCAI +22% CPU renaissance vs. AMD Epyc continues structural share gains** — Xeon 6 is competitive; AMD still has architectural advantages
3. **NVIDIA + US Government = extraordinary strategic floor vs. FCF −$3.1B TTM** — Coalition changes downside calculus; doesn't fix cash burn
4. **Lip-Bu Tan 6-straight-beat execution vs. stock already tripled** — Best CEO execution in semis right now; priced in
5. **18A "ahead of expectations" vs. industry-standard yields not until 2027** — Apple chose Intel for second-source, not TSMC displacement

## Apple Deal Summary (June 18, 2026)

- **Technology:** Intel 18A process node
- **Scope:** Entry-level M-series chips, 15–20 million units/year, starting 2027
- **Revenue:** ~$500M–$1B/year by 2028
- **Confirmed?** No — announced by Trump, unconfirmed by Apple or Intel
- **Significance:** First major external 18A validation; sets template for 14A hyperscaler conversations
- **Limitation:** Apple keeps TSMC for 80%+ of production, including all premium iPhone/Mac chips
