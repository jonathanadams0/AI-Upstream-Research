## What Caused the -12.59% Post-Print Drop

The stock was at $477 going into Q2 and fell to ~$416 the next session, then continued sliding to $392 by June 9 (market scan date). By June 18, it has partially recovered to $408.

**Two factors:**
1. **VMware miss** ($7.18B vs. $7.32B est.): Software revenue came in $140M light. Enough for algos to sell.
2. **Buy-the-rumor, sell-the-news**: AVGO had run +21% into the print from ~$393 at the start of May to $477 on earnings day. Classic setup for a post-beat selloff even with clean fundamentals.

**What this is NOT:** An invalidation event. The Aschenbrenner short thesis (hyperscalers build custom silicon in-house and reduce AVGO dependency) has been empirically inverted — see below.

## Key Non-Consensus Finding: Aschenbrenner Thesis Inverted

The bear case on AVGO entering Q2 was the Aschenbrenner view: Google, Meta, and Apple would gradually bring XPU design in-house and reduce Broadcom's custom silicon revenue. The Q1 2026 13F showed that Aschenbrenner's own fund — Situational Awareness LP — took a **new long position in AVGO** during Q1 2026.

The man who wrote the short thesis is now long. The 6 XPU customer confirmation (+200% AI revenue Q3 guide) further suggests the custom silicon market is expanding, not fragmenting.
