# FCEL — Q2 FY2026 Post-Earnings Scoring

**Date:** June 18, 2026 | **Print date:** June 8, 2026 | **Rating:** Hold | **PT:** $18 | **Price:** $16.60

## Binary Event Check

The single most important question going into Q2: **Did FCEL announce a major hyperscaler fuel cell contract?**

**Answer: No.** No contract announced. This was the upgrade trigger. Without it, the thesis remains speculative. The rating cannot be Buy.

---

## What the Results Tell Us

**The bad:** Revenue is declining (-5% YoY at $35.6M). Operating losses are heavy at ~$78M/quarter. The Groton impairment ($42.57M) raises questions about legacy asset quality. This is a company burning significant cash with no near-term path to profitability on current revenue.

**The good:** Pipeline growth of +267% to 4 GW, with 90% data center mix and average proposal size doubling to 130 MW, suggests FCEL is having serious conversations at serious scale with hyperscalers. A single 130 MW fuel cell installation at $X/watt would represent hundreds of millions in revenue. The manufacturing expansion to 500 MW signals internal conviction that these proposals will convert.

**The interpretation:** FCEL is in the "late pipeline, pre-contract" phase. Every proposal that doesn't convert is wasted pipeline. Every one that does is a step-function re-rating event.

## Rating Decision: Hold/$18

**Why not Buy:** The binary event (hyperscaler contract) has not occurred. Buying without a signed contract is buying pure optionality on a cash-burning speculative play. Conviction is too low.

**Why not Pass:** The pipeline expansion is real and extraordinary — 267% growth to 4 GW is not noise. Average proposal size doubling to 130 MW signals hyperscaler-scale conversations. The stock has already de-rated 40% from its $27.69 52-week high. A Pass assumes the thesis is dead; it isn't.

**PT $18:** Represents ~8% upside from $16.60. This is not a high-conviction upside target — it reflects that the stock is fairly valued at current pipeline optionality but has modest upside if pipeline progress continues without a contract.
