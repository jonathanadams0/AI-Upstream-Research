# Note Walk-Through — GE Vernova (GEV)

*Use this if they ask "walk me through a piece of your work." Pick GEV because it shows forecasting, normalizing judgment, scenario work, AND a documented case where I changed my own view.*

---

## The 2-minute spoken version

"The note I'd point to is GE Vernova — they make the gas turbines and grid equipment that power data centers.

**The driver:** they're effectively a three-player oligopoly in heavy-duty gas turbines, and the orderbook is sold out through 2029. So I didn't forecast revenue top-down off a growth rate — I built it off the actual mechanics: backlog (now ~100 GW of turbines), the rate that backlog converts to revenue, and pricing. New order slots are pricing 10–20 points above the old backlog, so as old-price work ships and new-price work replaces it, margin expands almost mechanically. That's the core of the model.

**The judgment part:** the reported numbers were misleading. 2025 net income was up 215%, but ~$2.9B of that was a one-time tax benefit. I normalized that out — used a 25% tax rate, stripped non-operating pension income, and adjusted working capital because their customer deposits had been a big cash tailwind that fades as backlog converts. The headline number and the number you forecast off of were very different.

**The honest part:** I originally had this as a Hold. My DCF said the stock was expensive and the comps said fairly valued. After the Q1 print — backlog jumped to 100 GW with the target raised — I upgraded to Buy and roughly tripled my target. I kept the old analysis in the note so I have a record of what I missed and why I changed. The thing the DCF couldn't capture was the optionality in a sold-out orderbook.

That loop — build the forecast off real drivers, normalize the reported numbers, then hold myself accountable when I'm wrong — is exactly what I'd bring to FP&A."

## If they want mechanics (have ready, don't volunteer)
- **Revenue build:** backlog (~$150B total, ~100 GW turbines) → conversion schedule → segment revenue (Power / Electrification / Wind). Orders (+34% organic) are the leading indicator of 2026–27 revenue.
- **Margin bridge:** old-price backlog converting to new-price reservations (+10–20 pts) drives Power EBITDA margin toward 12–14%.
- **Normalizing adjustments:** removed $2.9B tax-allowance release; 25% normalized tax rate; pension income excluded from operating FCF; working-capital tailwind from contract liabilities faded out as backlog ships.
- **Scenario + sensitivity:** explicit Bear/Base/Bull on 2030 EBITDA and margin; WACC × terminal-growth sensitivity grid; each incremental GW of backlog above target ≈ +$15/share.
- **Valuation triangulation:** DCF (conservative floor) vs. EV/EBITDA and P/E comps vs. sum-of-parts — and a clear written reason for weighting comps over DCF.
- **Invalidation triggers:** written rules for downgrading — backlog net cancellations, two+ hyperscalers cutting capex >20%, Power margin stalling below 11% for two quarters.
