# Miners to AI Hosts — Repurposing Bitcoin Infrastructure for AI

> Bitcoin miners have spent the last decade building exactly what AI data centers need: cheap power contracts, large industrial buildings, and power-dense infrastructure. As Bitcoin mining margins have compressed, these companies are pivoting to GPU hosting — renting their facilities and power to AI cloud providers. Aschenbrenner's Situational Awareness held CleanSpark, Riot Platforms, IREN, Applied Digital, Hut 8, BitDeer, and Cipher Mining as a basket. Core Scientific took it furthest: 590 MW contracted to CoreWeave, $10B+ over 12 years, and ultimately acquired by CoreWeave.

---

## Why Miners Make Natural AI Hosts

Bitcoin miners and AI data centers have remarkably similar infrastructure requirements:

| Requirement | Bitcoin Mining | AI GPU Hosting |
|---|---|---|
| Power density | 30-50 kW/rack | 30-120 kW/rack |
| Cooling | Air + immersion | Air + liquid |
| Location | Low-cost power markets | Low-cost power markets |
| Power contracts | Long-term, MW-scale | Same |
| Building type | Industrial, high clearance | Same |
| Connectivity | Fiber (for pool connections) | Fiber (for AI inference) |
| 24/7 operations | Yes | Yes |

The key insight: **the power contract is the asset.** Miners spent years building relationships with utilities and securing long-term power purchase agreements at below-market rates in locations with abundant renewable or natural gas generation (Texas, North Dakota, Wyoming, Alberta, Quebec). These power contracts have enormous value for AI hosting because data center operators desperately need reliable, cheap power.

---

## The Conversion Economics

**Bitcoin mining (2025-2026):** Bitcoin halving reduced mining rewards. At moderate BTC prices, many miners operate near breakeven. Revenue = BTC price × hash rate share. Highly volatile.

**GPU hosting:** CoreWeave pays Core Scientific $700M+ annually for 590 MW of contracted capacity. That's ~$1.19/watt-year for power + real estate + operations. Revenue = contracted rate × uptime. Predictable, 12-year terms.

**Margin comparison:**
- Bitcoin mining: 30-60% gross margin when BTC is strong; near zero in bear markets
- GPU hosting: 50-70% gross margin; locked in long-term contracts with investment-grade counterparties (CoreWeave has Microsoft as anchor tenant)

For miners with large power contracts and facilities, GPU hosting is a structurally superior business model.

---

## The Core Scientific Proof of Concept

Core Scientific (CORZ) is the canonical example of the miner-to-AI-host pivot:
- Emerged from bankruptcy in 2023 after BTC crash
- Signed initial 200 MW CoreWeave contract in 2023 → expanded to 590 MW
- $10B+ projected revenue over 12 years at full capacity
- Currently 350 MW energized, 185 MW billing ($700M+ annual run-rate)
- CoreWeave signing a definitive agreement to acquire CORZ validates the asset value

The $10B figure for 590 MW of contracted AI hosting capacity implies a value of ~$17M per contracted MW — compared to the market's previous valuation of miners at $2-5M per MW of power infrastructure.

## Key Risks

1. **BTC optionality gives up:** Converting facilities to AI hosting means losing the Bitcoin upside if BTC spikes; some miners prefer to keep optionality
2. **CoreWeave concentration:** Several miners have one anchor tenant — if CoreWeave hits financial difficulty, the whole AI hosting thesis unwinds
3. **Commodity power contracts expire:** Long-term power agreements eventually reprice; high power prices could erode margins
4. **Competition from purpose-built data centers:** EQIX, DLR, IRM are building AI-spec facilities; miners' advantage (existing power) erodes as new grid capacity comes online

**CoreWeave (CRWV) is down ~11% today** as part of the broader AI infrastructure selloff, trading ~$114. This is a direct read on the Miners thesis: if the anchor tenant faces financial stress or reduces contracted capacity, the hosting revenue streams at APLD, IREN, and any remaining miners with CoreWeave exposure are at risk. The positive offset: CoreWeave recently added $32B to its AI backlog and joined the Nasdaq-100, suggesting the business itself remains on solid footing — today's selloff looks like market sentiment, not fundamental deterioration.

**CORZ acquisition is already agreed** — the CoreWeave acquisition of Core Scientific is a done deal contractually; near-term price swings at CRWV don't affect the transaction economics.

**The power contract asset remains the most insulated element.** Long-term power PPAs signed with utilities don't unwind because AI sentiment softens for a quarter. If new hosting contracts slow, miners retain the optionality to run BTC mining on the same infrastructure — the asset doesn't go to zero.

**Near-term risk: new contract signing pace.** APLD and IREN are most exposed if hyperscalers slow their data center expansion commitments. Neither has the locked-in backlog that BE or CAT have.
