# Transformer Supply Crunch

---

## The Pitch

Large power transformer lead times have extended from ~50 weeks (pre-2022) to 120+ weeks. Data center load growth, grid reliability upgrades post-Winter Storm Uri, and EV/electrification demand have collided with a decades-old supply base. Grain-oriented electrical steel (GOES) is the upstream bottleneck. This structurally supports pricing and backlog at T&D equipment makers through at least 2027.

---

## Why Now

- AI data center interconnects require LPTs (345kV+) which are the most supply-constrained tier
- Aging US grid — average transformer age is ~40 years, past design life
- GOES capacity hasn't been added meaningfully in decades; Cleveland-Cliffs is the sole US producer

---

## Supporting Evidence

- Utility IRPs consistently cite transformer availability as the binding constraint on grid buildout
- Listed-company backlogs at [[ETN]], [[HUBB]] have extended in duration and pricing
- Hitachi Energy, Siemens Energy backlogs at multi-year highs

---

## Companies Expressing This Thesis

- [[ETN]] — electrical equipment, direct
- [[HUBB]] — T&D components
- [[SMNEY]] — Siemens Energy, HVDC + transformers
- [[PWR]] — grid EPC, benefits indirectly from buildout

---

## What Would Invalidate This

1. Meaningful GOES capacity additions (multi-year lead time to invalidate)
2. Demand destruction from recession
3. Modular/alternative grid architectures (DERs, microgrids) reducing LPT demand

---

## Time Horizon

- Near-term (0-12mo): Backlog conversion, pricing flowing through P&L
- Medium-term (1-3yr): Peak pricing power, margin expansion
- Long-term (3-5yr): Supply response likely catches up
