# Core Scientific (CORZ)

> Core Scientific is being acquired by CoreWeave in an all-stock deal. It has 590 MW of contracted HPC infrastructure with CoreWeave across six sites, with $10B+ in projected 12-year revenue ($700M+ annually at full capacity). As of June 3, 350 MW is energized and 185 MW is billing. This is now an acquisition/merger arbitrage situation.

## 2. Situation

Core Scientific began as a Bitcoin mining company, went through bankruptcy in 2022, and emerged to pivot aggressively to AI/HPC hosting. The pivot thesis: existing power infrastructure + real estate + cooling can be repurposed for GPU hosting faster than building new data centers.

**CoreWeave acquisition:** CoreWeave signed a definitive agreement to acquire Core Scientific in an all-stock transaction. This validates the GPU hosting thesis but converts CORZ from an equity play into an acquisition target.

**Current status:** ~350 MW energized, 185 MW actively billing at $700M+ annual run-rate. Core Scientific is also raising $3.3B in high-yield bonds to fund further site buildout.

**The thesis that worked:** Aschenbrenner held CORZ before the CoreWeave deal validated the thesis. The 590 MW / $10B contract over 12 years is one of the largest data center hosting agreements ever signed.

## 3. Investment View

Under Review pending deal completion and assessment of merger terms. The acquisition by CoreWeave means CORZ value depends on CoreWeave equity value — which is itself a new public company. Monitor deal closing.
