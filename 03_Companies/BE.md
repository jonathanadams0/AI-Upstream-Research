## 2. Why Bloom Solves the AI Power Problem

Data centers need power. The US grid interconnection queue has multi-year backlogs. A new data center that applies for grid connection today may wait 3-7 years. Bloom's solid oxide fuel cells bypass this problem entirely:

1. **On-site generation:** Bloom Energy Servers generate electricity directly at the data center site from pipeline natural gas, eliminating the grid connection requirement
2. **Fast deployment:** A Bloom installation can go from contract to power in 12-18 months vs. 3-7 years for grid connection
3. **Dispatchable and reliable:** Unlike solar/wind, Bloom generates power on demand, 24/7 — exactly what AI training clusters need
4. **Clean credentials:** Bloom produces electricity with no combustion — lower NOx/SOx than generators; can run on hydrogen or biogas for zero-carbon option
5. **Modular:** Can deploy 1 MW to hundreds of MW in stages as data center demand grows

## 3. The AI Data Center Contracts

**Oracle / Project Jupiter:** Up to 2.8 GW of Bloom fuel cells for Oracle's AI data center buildout. Project Jupiter is Oracle's initiative to build the world's largest AI data center infrastructure. This is Bloom's largest-ever contract and validates the fuel cell-as-AI-power thesis at scale.

**Nebius Group (NBIS):** 10-year, $2.6B agreement for 328 MW of solid oxide fuel cell capacity. Nebius is a European AI cloud company (Aschenbrenner's other large holding). The combination of Bloom + Nebius in Situational Awareness's portfolio reflects a conviction that AI cloud providers will use fuel cells to solve the power access problem.

**Pipeline:** Bloom has disclosed "discussions with multiple hyperscale customers" beyond Oracle and Nebius. Given the interconnection queue problem is universal, every major hyperscaler faces the same issue.

## 4. Key Risks

1. **Natural gas price risk:** Bloom's economics depend on natural gas prices remaining affordable; price spikes reduce competitiveness vs. grid power
2. **Electrolyzer competition:** For green hydrogen applications, Bloom faces competition from PEM electrolyzers (Nel, Plug Power, ITM Power)
3. **Permitting:** Even without grid interconnection, Bloom still needs local air permits and utility interconnection agreements for export
4. **Valuation:** At $291 and $25B market cap, 177% YTD, much of the near-term thesis is priced. PT $330 reflects 13% upside.
5. **Execution at scale:** 2.8 GW for Oracle is unprecedented; manufacturing capacity must scale
