## 10. Bear Case

**Scenario:** China removes export restrictions on rare earth processing, flooding the global market with NdPr supply and collapsing prices. Tesla or another major EV OEM delays or reduces its rare earth magnet procurement program. Mountain Pass mine faces operational challenges.

**Implied Bear Price:** $35 (~47% downside from ~$66 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **NdPr oxide price falls below $55/kg for two consecutive quarters.** Mountain Pass's economics are directly tied to NdPr pricing. Below $55/kg, the margin contribution from Stage 2 processing becomes marginal and the investment in magnets manufacturing looks premature.
2. **Tesla magnet supply program is delayed more than 12 months or reduced in scope.** MP's entire strategic pivot toward fully integrated rare earth magnets is anchored by the Tesla relationship. Any public reduction in that program's timeline or volume would impair the revenue bridge narrative.
3. **China announces unilateral relaxation of rare earth export controls.** This is the structural bear scenario — the entire thesis depends on Western supply chain decoupling from China. If China reverses course and allows unrestricted rare earth exports, the scarcity premium evaporates.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter processing yield variability, temporary NdPr price softness, or normal permitting delays on non-critical mine expansion activities.
