## 3. Corning's AI Angle

**Optical fiber for AI clusters:** Every GPU in an AI training cluster is connected to switches via optical cable, and every rack-to-rack connection uses fiber. At 100,000 GPU clusters, fiber miles are measured in thousands. Corning is the largest US optical fiber manufacturer.

**US manufacturing premium:** The NVIDIA partnership specifically targets US optical connectivity manufacturing. This reflects supply chain security concerns (similar to CHIPS Act logic) — hyperscalers want US-made fiber infrastructure, especially for sensitive AI programs.

**Display glass headwind:** Corning's largest segment historically is display glass (TV, laptop, smartphone screens). This business is in secular decline as device sales slow. The market has priced Corning at a discounted multiple reflecting this overhang. The AI optical thesis is fighting against the display glass headwind.

## 4. The Aschenbrenner Short — Was It Wrong?

Aschenbrenner had puts against Corning (GLW) — his thesis was likely that: (1) display glass was a structural decline, (2) Chinese fiber manufacturers (ZTT, Hengtong) were pricing Corning out of the fiber market, (3) valuation was stretched.

The NVIDIA $500M partnership for US optical connectivity potentially invalidates thesis point (2). If NVDA is paying a premium for US-made optical infrastructure, the China competition concern is partially offset by the "made in America" sourcing mandate.

**Our view:** Hold at $200 (5% upside to $210). The thesis is real but largely priced after the 13% surge. Would Buy below $175 where the AI optical thesis offers more compelling entry with display glass as the downside already known.
