# Sandisk Corporation (SNDK)

> Pure-play NAND flash manufacturer spun off from Western Digital on February 24, 2025. Experiencing a NAND supercycle driven by AI storage demand and a novel "New Business Model" (NBM) — multi-year hyperscaler supply agreements with floor-ceiling pricing and financial guarantees. Hold at $2,000 PT; the thesis is real but the stock at $2,182 prices in a very optimistic scenario.

## 1. Investment Thesis

SNDK is a structural AI storage beneficiary with a genuine business model innovation (NBM contracts). The company has gone from 22.5% gross margins (Q3 FY2025) to 78.4% (Q3 FY2026), from net debt to $3.7B net cash, and is guiding Q4 FY2026 to $31.50 EPS (midpoint). Five NBM agreements representing a $42B+ multi-year backlog are signed with hyperscaler customers.

**Hold (not Buy) because:**
1. The DCF doesn't support $2,182 under any but the most aggressive assumptions (~$380 base case, ~$1,000 bull case)
2. Probability-weighted expected value ~$1,612 (26% below current price)
3. Risk/reward asymmetry is unfavorable: +37% bull upside vs. −82% bear downside
4. Stock is already 18.5% above the sell-side consensus PT of $1,843

**Upgrade to Buy if:** Q4 FY2026 EPS ≥$38 AND 6th NBM agreement announced AND Q1 FY2027 guide ≥$35 EPS — OR stock pulls back to $1,600–1,800 range. *Note (6/25): MU beat Q4 EPS guide by 22%; applying the same beat to SNDK's $31.50 guide = ~$38.43 — the trigger is now in play. Next gate: Aug 13 earnings.*

- **Spinoff date:** February 24, 2025 from Western Digital

## 5. Key Risks

1. **NAND cycle reversal** — Samsung/SK Hynix capacity adds crash ASPs; GM reverts to 40–50%. High impact, medium likelihood.
2. **NBM contract failure** — Hyperscaler walkaway or renegotiation. Unproven in down-cycle. Low likelihood, very high impact.
3. **Kioxia JV disruption** — Geopolitical or operational. Low likelihood, extreme impact.
4. **Enterprise SSD share** — Samsung 34.6%, SK Hynix 26.7% vs SNDK ~10%. Structural weakness in the key growth segment.
