## 2. Thesis (3 bullets)

1. **The repricing trade: industrial boiler multiple on AI power infrastructure economics.** BW designs and manufactures the boilers, HRSGs, and steam turbines inside gas-fired power plants. The Feb 26, 2026 Base Electron contract ($2.4B, 1.2 GW for [[APLD]]'s AI factory) transforms the revenue base, but the market still prices BW as a restructuring story. Pipeline ($14B+) is 5x+ current annualized revenue.

2. **Bloom Energy pattern signal: backlog growing far faster than revenue.** Bookings +1,900% YoY vs revenue +44% YoY — the same divergence that preceded BE's repricing. Revenue recognition from Base Electron has only begun ($31M in Q1'26).

3. **Balance sheet de-risking removes the historical discount.** Net debt cut 87% in Q1'26 alone, now below 1x TTM EBITDA. The serial-restructuring/pension-liability overhang that justified the distressed multiple is shrinking just as the order book inflects.

## 5. Risks

1. **Single-contract concentration:** ~89% of backlog is one counterparty (Base Electron/APLD). APLD financing stress flows directly to BW. Note APLD's own sell trigger (refinancing failure) — correlated exposure.
2. **Execution at unprecedented scale:** Q1'26 bookings exceed any recent annual revenue. A 160-year-old company scaling 5-10x has real delivery risk.
3. **Legacy liabilities:** Pension and restructuring history; verify in latest 10-Q before sizing.
4. **Counterparty quality:** Base Electron is APLD-backed, not a hyperscaler directly — weaker credit than GEV/WMB counterparties.
