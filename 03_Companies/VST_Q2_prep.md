## Why Q2 Is the Most Important Quarter

VST's earnings are highly seasonal. The company's Texas segment (ERCOT) — its largest contributor — earns the vast majority of annual EBITDA in Q2 and Q3, driven by summer heat driving air conditioning load and power prices.

Q1'26 was explicitly called "one of the mildest first quarters in Texas history" by CEO Jim Burke, yet the company still delivered $1.49B in adj. EBITDA and beat estimates by 117% on EPS. That Q1 beat on a weak quarter de-risks the full-year guide — but Q2 weather is the swing factor.

**The math:** To hit the $7.2B FY midpoint, Q2–Q4 combined need ~$5.7B in adj. EBITDA. In prior years (2024), Q2+Q3 together contributed ~$4.9B (Q2 $1.5B, Q3 $1.9B). 2026 has the Cogentrix acquisition closing (adds ~$1B+ incremental EBITDA run-rate when integrated), Meta nuclear delivery starting, and ERCOT load growing 6% YoY — all supportive of achieving the guide.

## Key Drivers to Watch

**1. ERCOT summer weather (the dominant variable)**

Heat degree days vs. normal are the primary Q2 earnings driver. A hot June/July drives peak power prices in ERCOT that can easily add $100-300M to EBITDA vs. a mild period.

- ERCOT weather-normalized load grew ~6% YoY (per Q3 2025 earnings call commentary)
- Forward power curves in ERCOT have "more life" than a year ago but still below management's demand forecast (from Q3 2025 call)
- Temperature in Texas in June 2026 to date should be tracked via NOAA forecasts

**Bull setup:** If June and July are hot (above 100°F in Dallas/Houston for multiple weeks), Q2 adj. EBITDA could approach $2B or above, front-loading the FY toward the high end of guidance. This would also drive a significant positive EPS surprise vs. the $2.02 consensus.

**Bear setup:** A repeat of Q1 mild weather through June would limit upside. But even then, Q1 at $1.49B was a beat — management has built conservatism into the hedge book.

**2. Hedging book and mark-to-market**

Vistra typically hedges ~100% of near-year generation (95% of 2026 was hedged as of Q2 2025). The Q2 GAAP EPS will include significant unrealized derivative gains/losses that are excluded from adjusted results. Watch Q1 included $723M in unrealized hedge gains — Q2 will vary based on power price moves.

**3. Cogentrix acquisition update**
- Target close: H2 2026. Any regulatory update, closing date narrowing, or financing update is material.
- Cogentrix adds 5,500 MW of gas-fired generation across eastern US at mid-teens levered returns.
- Each month of delay pushes incremental EBITDA into 2027.

**4. Meta nuclear delivery commencement**
- Perry and Davis-Besse nuclear: delivery to Meta expected to begin "in late 2026" per the 10-Q.
- No Q2 revenue contribution expected yet, but management may provide delivery timeline specificity.

**5. PJM capacity auction (July 2026)**
- The next PJM RPM capacity auction clears in July 2026. Vistra's East segment (nuclear + gas in PJM) is directly affected by clearing prices.
- Prior auction cleared at $269.92/MW-day. If next auction clears higher (above $300/MW-day), VST East segment earnings increase for the 2027/28 delivery year.
- Fitch cited "collar of $175-$325/MW-day" being proposed. Higher clearing would be a positive catalyst.

**6. Investment grade confirmation and debt strategy**
- Both S&P and Fitch upgraded to investment grade in 2025-2026.
- Watch for commentary on refinancing plans — any announcement of IG-rate refinancing of high-coupon legacy debt would expand FCFbG.

## Q2 Seasonal Pattern

From prior year Q2 2025: Adj. EBITDA was $1.35B, which "fell ~4.5% YoY as Generation segment headwinds from unplanned coal outages offset higher wholesale electric pricing." That implies Q2 2026 base of $1.35B, and management's YoY growth trajectory from 2025 to 2026 (full-year guidance up significantly from ~$4.9B to $7.2B midpoint) implies Q2 EBITDA should be meaningfully above Q2 2025.

**2025 quarterly EBITDA trajectory:** Q1 $0.60B, Q2 $1.12B, Q3 $1.87B, Q4 $1.30B = ~$4.9B FY
**2026 guidance midpoint:** $7.2B — implies ~47% step-up
**If evenly distributed step-up:** Q2 2026 at ~$1.65B. Consensus $1.31B looks conservative if ERCOT has a normal summer.

## Investment Thesis Update

**Bull case for Q2:** Hot summer + Cogentrix approaching close + Meta nuclear delivery starting = all three thesis pillars advancing simultaneously. Each is a separate catalyst from the others.

**Bear case:** Mild ERCOT summer (low Q2 EBITDA), Cogentrix delayed beyond 2026, and/or PJM capacity auction disappointment.

**At $154, the stock is pricing in a mild scenario.** Sell-side mean PT is $228 — a 48% premium to current. The Q2 print is the clearest near-term catalyst to close this gap.
