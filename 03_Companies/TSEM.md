# Tower Semiconductor (TSEM)

> Israeli specialty analog/mixed-signal foundry. Aschenbrenner holds a position. Intel attempted to acquire Tower in 2021-2022 for $5.4B (deal blocked by China). Tower specializes in analog, RF, power management, MEMS, and photonics — all device types that AI infrastructure requires (power conversion, RF connectivity, sensors). ## 2. Why Tower Is in the Aschenbrenner Portfolio

Tower makes the analog/mixed-signal chips that don't get coverage but are essential:
- **Power management ICs** for data center servers and AI accelerators
- **RF front-ends** for 5G infrastructure that carries AI inference
- **Photonics** — silicon photonics for co-packaged optics (the next generation of optical transceivers)
- **MEMS** — sensors and actuators used in AI robotics and automotive AI

Tower's specialty processes aren't economically viable on leading-edge TSMC/Samsung nodes — the analog designs prefer older, more cost-effective processes. This creates a structural niche.

**The acquisition premium:** Intel's $5.4B failed bid values Tower at approximately $32-35/share (pre-split equivalent). At $275 today, the market has clearly repriced the strategic value significantly.
