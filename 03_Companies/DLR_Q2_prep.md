## 3. Three Questions This Print Must Answer

**Q1 — Pre-leased % on development pipeline (primary invalidation gate)**
DLR's core value proposition is building contracted capacity — "pre-leased before built." The thesis holds at ≥70% pre-leasing. A slide toward 65-70% is a warning; below 60% is the invalidation trigger. Management typically discloses this as a % of active development in the supplemental. Read this number first.

**Q2 — AI campus backlog and new signings: what's the total pipeline?**
[[Portfolio Summary]] catalyst calendar: "~Jul 24 — AI campus backlog; pre-leased %." This is the primary Q2 watch. DLR's signed bookings and pipeline disclosures are the forward indicator. A quarter with record new signings (net absorption) despite high deliveries would validate the AI-demand cycle thesis. A sequential decline in new signings would raise the concern that demand has peaked.

**Q3 — Core FFO delivery vs. $1.98 consensus and full-year guide trajectory**
DLR raised FY2026 core FFO guidance to $8.00–$8.10 per share (vs. $7.30 at initiation). The Q2 consensus is $1.98 core FFO/share (implied $1.87 × +5.9% YoY). Beating this confirms the thesis; missing — especially with a guide cut — would be the largest negative catalyst for the year. Also watch same-capital Cash NOI growth rate; below 3% is bear trigger #2.
