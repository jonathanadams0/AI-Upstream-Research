## Setup — One-Line Thesis

Pure-play AI HPC data center operator with $36B contracted backlog across five campuses, anchored by CoreWeave (400 MW, PF1) and an investment-grade hyperscaler with three separate long-term leases; trading at a meaningful discount to Core Scientific/CoreWeave comps despite comparable tenant quality.

## Thesis Invalidation Trigger (from [[Portfolio Summary]])

> **Sell trigger:** Balance sheet refinancing fails or prices at >300bps premium.

**Status as of June 9, 2026 — trigger did NOT fire:**
Applied Digital priced $1.59B in 7.000% senior secured notes due 2031 on June 9, 2026 (APLD ComputeCo 3 entity), used to fund ELN-04 (150 MW, fourth building at Polaris Forge 1) and repay the Goldman Sachs bridge facility. The "lower than expected" yield reflected strong investor demand. If the relevant benchmark is the 10Y Treasury (~4.3–4.5% range mid-June 2026), the effective spread is ~250–270 bps — below the 300 bps invalidation threshold. The balance sheet at quarter end: ~$2.3B total cash/equivalents/restricted cash; $5.2B total assets; majority of debt not maturing until 2030.

**What in the Q4 print would fire the trigger:**
1. Management discloses an additional refinancing need for PF2 or DF2 that is priced at >300 bps over the comparable Treasury — specifically any project financing closing at 8%+ given the current rate environment.
2. Covenant stress disclosure: any waiver, cure, or amendment discussion under existing credit agreements.
3. Liquidity guidance that implies APLD cannot fund current construction pipeline without near-term dilutive equity issuance.

*Note: ChronoScale (formerly Cloud) is a separate entity — financing constraints on ChronoScale do not trigger the APLD BS refinancing condition unless APLD is guaranteeing that debt.*

## 3 Questions This Print Must Answer

**1. What does standalone HPC revenue look like post-ChronoScale separation?**

Cloud contributed an uncertain portion of Q3's blended $126.6M. The Q4 print is the first clean read on the HPC/Data Center business in isolation (Cloud separated May 5, still partially in Q4). Management should break out (or restate) HPC segment revenue vs. Cloud for comparability. The key number: is standalone HPC segment revenue running at $100M+/quarter as a base before the PF1 new building ramp kicks in (July+ energizations)? This sets the floor for Q1 FY2027 models.

*Connects to: "(confirm from latest filings)" flags in [[APLD]] — note has no verification worklist entries, but the Cloud separation creates new comparability questions not in the original note.*

**2. Is ELN-02/ELN-03 energization on track for July–September 2026?**

Management guided staggered PF1 energizations starting July 2026. Q4 ends May 31 — so the energizations themselves are post-quarter, but the Q4 call (AMC ~Aug 20) will include management commentary on actual energy delivery commencement. If ELN-02 (150 MW) and ELN-03 (150 MW) are on track, this directly drives Q1 FY2027 revenue inflection. Any slip beyond September 2026 would delay the revenue ramp the bull case depends on. Watch for: MW delivered to CoreWeave, customer acceptance, grid interconnect status in North Dakota.

*Connects to Bear Case condition #3 (GPU utilization below 70% for two quarters) — can't hit utilization thresholds if capacity isn't energized on time.*

**3. What is the capital requirement for the construction pipeline, and does it require new financing?**

Five campuses now under development: PF1 (ELN-01 live, ELN-02/03 ramping, ELN-04 funded by June notes), PF2 (200 MW, inv-grade hyperscaler, ~$5B), DF2 (210 MW, inv-grade hyperscaler, ~$5.2B, ops Q1 2028). Total contracted $36B. These require significant project financing. Q4 10-K (FY2026 annual) will include full disclosure of capital commitments. The question: is the June $1.59B sufficient for PF1 Phase 2, or does PF2/DF2 construction require new project debt within the next 12 months? If yes, at what terms? This is the single most important indicator of whether the 300 bps refinancing trigger is a near-term vs. distant risk.
