# Celestica Inc. (CLS)

> Celestica is a contract electronics manufacturer — the company that physically builds AI networking hardware for Cisco, HPE, Juniper, Nokia, and Arista. As hyperscalers buy hundreds of thousands of AI switches and servers, Celestica's factories run at capacity. It's the Fabrinet of networking equipment: pure manufacturing leverage on AI infrastructure demand, priced at contract manufacturer multiples.

**Sector:** [[AI-Networking]] | **Rating:** Buy | **PT:** $530 | **Current:** ~$460 | **Upside:** +15%

---

## 2. Thesis

1. **AI networking hardware volumes are surging.** Every 100,000-GPU AI cluster requires thousands of 400G/800G switches, servers, and optical chassis. Celestica manufactures these for Cisco (Nexus), HPE (Aruba/ProLiant), Juniper (QFX), and others. As hyperscalers build AI clusters, they buy more of their OEM's products → Celestica's factories run harder.

2. **Priced at industrial multiple.** Contract manufacturing historically trades at 10-15x P/E. At $460 and ~17B market cap, Celestica is being valued on manufacturing fundamentals, not AI exposure. As AI networking revenue becomes a larger share and margins improve with volume, the multiple should expand.

3. **Factory qualification moat.** A new customer can't simply move manufacturing overnight — qualification for complex electronic assemblies takes 12-18 months. Celestica's existing relationship with its AI-exposed OEM customers is sticky.

## 3. The Screener Hit

Celestica passes the [[AI Bottleneck Screener]] on four of five criteria:
- ✓ Physical bottleneck: builds AI networking hardware
- ✓ Old-economy multiple: contract manufacturing P/E
- ✓ Supply constraint: 12-18 month factory qualification
- ✓ Backlog growing with AI networking OEM demand
- ⚠ Hyperscaler contract: indirect (through OEM customers, not direct)

The indirect nature is the key caveat. Celestica's AI exposure is real but mediated through Cisco, HPE, etc.

## 4. Bear Case

**Scenario:** Hyperscaler data center server and networking equipment capex cycle pauses as AI compute deployment matures from initial buildout to steady-state operation. Customer concentration risk materializes as one large hyperscaler (potentially >40% of CLS revenue) defers orders.

**Implied Bear Price:** $250 (~40% downside from ~$420 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Any single customer represents more than 40% of revenue AND reduces orders more than 20%.** Celestica's AI server manufacturing business is highly concentrated. A simultaneous large-customer revenue miss and forward order reduction would compress both earnings and multiple.
2. **Gross margin drops below 7% for two consecutive quarters.** Contract electronics manufacturing operates on thin margins (~10-11%). Any pricing concession to retain hyperscaler business or volume deleverage that compresses gross margin below 7% indicates the business is deteriorating.
3. **Revenue guidance cut more than 15% in a single quarter citing hyperscaler order delays.** Celestica's guidance visibility depends on customer order commitments. Any mid-year guidance reduction attributed to hyperscaler capex pauses would be the key signal.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter shipment timing differences, normal component supply constraints, or temporary margin compression from new program ramp.
