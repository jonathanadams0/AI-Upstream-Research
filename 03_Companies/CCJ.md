## 2. Thesis (3 Bullets)

1. **The nuclear renaissance is not a thesis — it is a done deal, and Cameco is the primary public equity expression of it.** Hyperscaler PPAs for nuclear power have created a durable new demand layer on top of the base case of existing fleet operation and life extensions. Confirmed deals include: Microsoft/Constellation Energy (20-year PPA to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1, announced Sep 2023, plant restarted Sep 2024), Google/Kairos Power (SMR fleet agreement, 2024), Amazon/X-energy (SMR agreement, 2023), and others. Uranium has a structural supply deficit: secondary supply (underfeeding, inventory drawdowns) that masked the deficit for ~a decade has been largely exhausted, while greenfield mine development requires 10–15 years from discovery to production. *(Verify latest hyperscaler nuclear PPA announcements for any additions since Q1 2026.)*

2. **McArthur River/Key Lake and Cigar Lake are among the world's highest-grade uranium deposits — Cameco's ore grades are 100–200x the global average for many in-situ recovery deposits.** McArthur River average ore grade ~15–20% U₃O₈ (peak zones higher); Cigar Lake ~15% average. World average for conventional underground/open-pit mines ~0.1–0.2%; global average including ISR operations is considerably lower (~0.01–0.05%). High grade = lower unit cost per pound = structural profitability advantage through down cycles. *(Source: Cameco corporate materials, World Nuclear Association; verify exact current reserve grades from most recent 40-F technical report.)*

3. **Westinghouse transforms the investment thesis from pure commodity exposure to vertically integrated nuclear infrastructure.** Westinghouse provides nuclear fuel fabrication, maintenance services, engineering, and SMR technology development (AP300, BWRX-300 participation). Every nuclear plant needs fuel, maintenance, and eventually upgrade — Westinghouse captures economics across the reactor lifecycle. The 49% stake total consideration was ~US$2.2B (Cameco's share of the ~$7.9B total enterprise value). *(Verify Cameco's exact acquisition cost from 40-F or original deal announcement.)* Westinghouse made a second cash distribution of US$350M gross in October 2025 (Cameco's 49% share = US$171.5M) related to its Dukovany construction project participation (confirmed). Our share of Westinghouse adj. EBITDA increased C$297M in FY2025 vs FY2024.

## 3. AI Exposure

**Classification: Direct**

### AI-Driven Demand Profile

Cameco's AI exposure is two steps removed from the AI stack but highly durable: AI data centers need electricity → hyperscalers want clean, firm, 24/7 power → nuclear is the only utility-scale source of 24/7 carbon-free baseload → nuclear plants need uranium → Cameco supplies uranium. The chain is long but the logic is iron.

**AI-linked demand vectors:**
- **Hyperscaler nuclear PPAs:** Microsoft's 20-year PPA with Constellation Energy to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1 (Crane Clean Energy Center) is the paradigm case — the plant restarted September 2024. Google signed an agreement with Kairos Power for a fleet of SMRs (2024). Amazon signed an agreement with X-energy for SMR deployment (2023) and also purchased a stake in nuclear projects via Energy Northwest. Each nuclear plant restart or life extension represents a 20-year+ uranium demand commitment.
- **Nuclear fleet life extensions:** The U.S. NRC grants initial 40-year licenses, extendable to 60 years (subsequent license renewal / SLR) and in some cases to 80 years. The NRC SLR pipeline includes multiple large plants. Each extension locks in uranium demand for decades without new construction — a low-marginal-cost demand extension. *(Verify count of pending/approved SLR applications from NRC public data.)*
- **SMR pipeline:** Westinghouse's AP300 (scaled-down AP1000), TerraPower Natrium, X-energy Xe-100, Kairos KP-FHR, and others are in various stages of licensing. Westinghouse also participates in the BWRX-300 program. If a fraction of announced SMR projects reach commercial operation by 2030–2035, it creates incremental uranium demand and direct Westinghouse engineering services revenue. *(Verify AP300 NRC Pre-Application review status.)*

## 5. Industry & Competitive Position

**Market Position:**
- Largest publicly traded uranium producer in the world. Kazatomprom (state-owned, Kazakhstan) produces more uranium by volume (~40% of global supply), but is not publicly traded on a major Western exchange in the same sense — CCJ is the largest pure-play publicly-traded Western uranium producer. Kazatomprom trades on the London Stock Exchange (KAP.LI) and AIX; the comparison depends on definition. *(Verify current production rankings from World Nuclear Association annual data.)*
- Controls two of the world's highest-grade uranium deposits (McArthur River, Cigar Lake) — irreplaceable assets
- Westinghouse: the dominant nuclear services and fuel technology company in the Western world, serving ~50% of global nuclear plants (per Westinghouse corporate materials).

**Moat:**
1. **Resource quality:** McArthur River/Key Lake ore grades (~15–20% U₃O₈) are unmatched globally among operating mines. High grade = lower unit cost per pound = structural profitability advantage.
2. **Westinghouse integration:** Vertical integration from mining through fuel fabrication to reactor services creates a unique, hard-to-replicate position.
3. **Long-term contract portfolio:** Utilities sign 10–20 year uranium supply contracts. Cameco's contracted book provides multi-year revenue visibility that competitors lack.
4. **Scale:** Largest Western producer with the operational expertise and balance sheet to maintain production through price downturns that idle smaller competitors.

**Key Competitors:**
- Kazatomprom (KAP.LI / AIX: KAZ) — Kazakh national uranium company, largest global uranium producer by volume (~40% of world supply per WNA data). State-owned. Lower cash costs given ISR (in-situ recovery) method, but geopolitical and logistics risk (sulfuric acid supply chain issues have intermittently constrained output). *(Verify most recent Kazatomprom production guidance.)*
- Uranium Energy Corp (UEC) — U.S.-focused, smaller-scale producer and developer with in-situ recovery operations (Texas, Wyoming). Not a direct peer on scale.
- NexGen Energy (NXE) — developing the Arrow deposit at Rook I project in Saskatchewan's Athabasca Basin. Arrow is one of the largest undeveloped high-grade uranium deposits globally. Pre-production; federal and provincial environmental review underway. *(Verify current EA/licensing status.)*
- Paladin Energy (PDN.AX) — Australian-listed; owns the Langer Heinrich mine (Namibia), which was placed on care and maintenance in 2018 and restarted in 2024. Lower grade than Canadian assets.
- Orano (French state) — vertically integrated mining + enrichment + fabrication; not publicly traded on Western exchanges.

**Porter's Five Forces:**
- Rivalry: **Low-Moderate** — oligopoly in high-grade uranium supply; Kazatomprom dominates volumes but Cameco dominates Western-facing supply
- New entrants: **Very Low** — new mines require 10–15 years, billions in capital, and social/regulatory license. Existing high-grade deposits are a non-renewable resource.
- Substitutes: **None for uranium** — nuclear fission requires uranium or thorium; no substitute fuel for existing light water reactors
- Buyer power: **Moderate** — utilities are sophisticated buyers with long-term contracting expertise, but they have limited alternatives for high-grade supply
- Supplier power: **Low** — mining inputs (labor, equipment, chemicals) are broadly available

## 9. Risks

1. **Uranium price cyclicality:** Despite structural demand drivers, uranium is a commodity and has experienced multi-year bear markets (2012–2018). A reversal from current elevated prices would compress margins even at Cameco's low-cost deposits.
2. **Kazatomprom supply:** Kazakhstan produces ~40% of global uranium. Any acceleration in Kazakh production (or resolution of current sulfuric acid supply constraints, which have intermittently limited ISR output) would pressure spot prices. *(Verify most recent Kazatomprom production guidance and sulfuric acid situation from their latest reporting.)*
3. **Production execution:** McArthur River/Key Lake is a complex underground mine. Operational challenges (flooding, equipment, worker safety incidents) have historically caused multi-year curtailments. 
4. **Westinghouse integration risk:** Cameco is a miner by heritage, and Westinghouse is a complex engineering and services business. Cultural and operational integration risk is real [VERIFY how Cameco is managing the Westinghouse investment alongside Brookfield].
5. **SMR delays:** SMR timelines have historically slipped. If the SMR wave is delayed by a decade, the near-term Westinghouse growth thesis is impaired.
6. **Geopolitical / Kazakhstan JV:** Inkai (Cameco's Kazakhstan JV with Kazatomprom) is subject to sovereign risk and currency risk [VERIFY Inkai operating conditions and recent performance].
7. **FX risk:** Cameco reports in CAD; mining costs are CAD-denominated; uranium is priced in USD. CAD/USD movements impact reported results [VERIFY FX hedging policy].

## 11. Bear Case

**Scenario:** Nuclear plant new build program faces multi-year delays due to regulatory, construction, or financing obstacles. Uranium spot price falls below $70/lb sustained as Kazakh and Russian supply returns to global markets. Westinghouse JV faces technical setbacks on SMR development.

**Implied Bear Price:** $85 (~21% downside from ~$107 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Uranium spot price falls below $70/lb and remains there for two consecutive quarters.** Cameco's long-term contract pricing is partially linked to spot. Below $70/lb sustained means new contracts are signed below cost-of-production-plus-margin thresholds, reducing the value of future commitments.
