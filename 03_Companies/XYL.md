## 2. The Water Bottleneck

AI data centers are water-intensive in ways most investors don't appreciate:

**Cooling tower evaporation:** Traditional air cooling uses water cooling towers that evaporate water to reject heat. A 100 MW data center running traditional cooling can evaporate 100-500 million gallons/year — equivalent to a small city's water consumption.

**Liquid cooling CDU systems:** Direct liquid cooling (DLC) uses closed-loop water circuits that require water treatment to prevent bacterial growth, scale, and corrosion. Xylem's water treatment chemicals, monitoring sensors, and purification systems are essential for CDU reliability.

**Regulatory risk:** Several states and municipalities are restricting data center water use permits. Companies like Vertiv (PurgeRite NearZero) and Xylem are directly addressing water conservation in data center commissioning.

**The Bloom Energy fuel cell angle:** Bloom Energy Servers also produce water as a byproduct of the electrochemical reaction. Water management is a key operational consideration for large-scale fuel cell deployments.

This is a classic screener hit: physical bottleneck (water), old-economy multiple, no hyperscaler contract announced yet (the gap), supply constraint (can't quickly expand water treatment capacity). The opportunity is in discovering this before a major data center water management contract is announced.

## 5. Bear Case

**Scenario:** Municipal water infrastructure spending is cut as budget pressures in local governments lead to project deferrals. Smart meter upgrade cycle ends before significant new replacement demand materializes. Xylem's AI/data center water cooling thesis does not convert to material orders.

**Implied Bear Price:** $85 (~23% downside from ~$110 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Infrastructure Solutions segment revenue declines more than 10% YoY for two consecutive quarters.** Municipal water infrastructure is Xylem's largest segment and the one most exposed to government spending cycles. Below 10% YoY growth signals the infrastructure investment supercycle is fading.
2. **Smart meter deployment contracts fail to ramp beyond current levels.** Xylem's Measurement & Control Solutions segment depends on utility capex for AMI (advanced metering) upgrades. If the utility sector delays smart meter deployment due to rate case uncertainty, this segment stalls.
3. **Applied Water segment gross margin declines below 30%.** Applied Water (pumps, heat exchangers for industrial/HVAC) is Xylem's margin anchor. Below 30% gross margin sustained would signal competitive pricing pressure or volume deleverage.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter weather-related demand variation, normal project timing delays, or temporary supply chain disruption in pump manufacturing.
