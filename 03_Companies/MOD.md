## 2. Thesis

1. **Data center cooling is now a structural growth engine, not a pilot program.** Modine's Performance Technologies segment — anchored by its data center cooling products including liquid cooling and air-side thermal systems — is capturing direct spend from hyperscalers and colocation operators facing acute thermal density challenges driven by GPU clusters. This is not a transient capex cycle; it is a multi-year infrastructure buildout with no credible demand ceiling visible at current AI adoption rates. [VERIFY: confirm segment revenue breakdown and specific data center revenue figures from most recent 10-K and earnings calls]

2. **The margin inflection from FY2022 to FY2025 is structural, not cyclical.** EBITDA margin nearly doubled from 8.3% to 13.9% over four years as data center cooling — a higher-margin product category than legacy HVAC or automotive thermal — scaled as a proportion of the mix. FCF went from ($29M) to $129M over the same period. If data center mix continues to grow, the next leg of margin expansion could be more dramatic than the market currently discounts.

3. **Balance sheet has meaningfully de-levered while growth CapEx is being deployed.** Net debt fell from $329M (FY2022) to $271M (FY2025) even as CapEx more than doubled — a sign that organic cash generation is outrunning growth investment. At roughly 0.75x net debt/EBITDA [VERIFY on exact current leverage ratio], Modine has significant capacity to accelerate investment, pursue tuck-in acquisitions in liquid cooling, or return capital, without balance sheet risk.

## 10. Bear Case

**Scenario:** Data center cooling market growth decelerates as rack density upgrades are pushed out. Modine's fastest-growing segment — data center thermal management — loses share to larger integrated competitors. Customer concentration creates revenue volatility.

**Implied Bear Price:** $150 (~49% downside from ~$291 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Data center revenue growth decelerates below 20% YoY for two consecutive quarters.** Modine's stock premium is based on 200%+ YoY data center growth. Any deceleration below 20% collapses the multiple from ~50x toward a low-20s industrial multiple.
2. **A single large data center customer represents more than 40% of revenue and reduces orders.** Modine's data center customer base is highly concentrated. Visibility into HPC/hyperscaler cooling orders is limited. A contract pause or competitive displacement would be material.
3. **HVAC segment margins fail to recover above 12% by YE2027.** Non-data-center HVAC is the earnings floor. If the core HVAC business doesn't recover, any data center slowdown would compound across the portfolio.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter weather impact on HVAC segment, temporary manufacturing capacity constraints, or normal lumpy order timing.
