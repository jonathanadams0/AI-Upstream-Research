# Hubbell (HUBB) — Q2 2026 Preview
**Rating:** Buy | **PT:** $545 | **Current:** $484.91

## Context

Hubbell is the most interesting setup in the July earnings cluster — it's our biggest Buy relative to consensus with 11% upside to the mean PT of $545, and the consensus is a Hold (7 Buy / 9 Hold / 1 Sell), which creates a potential catalyst dynamic if Q2 shows data center-driven acceleration.

## What Drives HUBB

Hubbell operates in two segments: Electrical Solutions (wiring devices, connectors, lighting) and Utility Solutions (distribution, transmission, substation, smart meters). The Utility Solutions segment — T&D infrastructure equipment — is the primary AI/data center beneficiary, as every new data center and grid upgrade requires distribution switchgear, high-voltage connectors, arresters, and smart meter installations.

The structural bull case: utility capital expenditure has entered a multi-year acceleration driven by both renewable integration and data center load growth. HUBB is a direct component supplier for this buildout.

## Key Watch Points

**1. Utility Solutions backlog** — the bellwether for forward revenue visibility. If backlog growth year-over-year is accelerating (vs. modestly growing), it signals the structural T&D order cycle is strengthening. A +20%+ backlog number would be constructive.

**2. Data center-specific commentary** — has Hubbell begun breaking out data center as a customer vertical? Any explicit sizing of the data center opportunity would be incrementally positive and could help close the gap to the analyst Buy community.

**3. Pricing power** — transformer shortages and supply-constrained T&D equipment should be enabling price increases. Watch for margin improvement in Utility Solutions.

**4. Tariff impact** — Hubbell sources some components internationally. Any Q2 quantification of tariff headwinds (copper, aluminum, steel) would help contextualize cost inflation vs. pricing offsets.

## Setup

At $485, the stock is 11% below consensus mean PT of $545 — the widest discount to consensus of any name in our coverage that has a Buy rating from us. This suggests the Hold-heavy analyst community is either behind the curve on the data center T&D opportunity or sees execution risk. A clean Q2 beat with constructive backlog commentary could catalyze upgrades from the Hold camp.

# Freeport-McMoRan (FCX) — Q2 2026 Preview
**Rating:** Hold | **PT:** $72 | **Current:** $70.67

## Context

FCX at $70.67 is essentially at the consensus mean PT of $70.88. At Hold with a $72 PT, we have minimal directional view — the copper structural thesis is real but already priced at consensus.

## What Drives FCX Q2

FCX is pure copper price leverage. Revenue and EPS move roughly 35-40 cents in adj. EPS for every $0.10/lb change in realized copper prices.

- Q2 2026 average copper price: LME copper has been trading in the $9,000-$10,000/tonne range ($4.00-4.50/lb), elevated by AI infrastructure demand and supply concerns
- Grasberg underground: The transition from the open-pit mine to underground operations at Grasberg (Indonesia) has been a multi-year process; underground production ramp is now well advanced and Q2 should show continued improvement in volume from Grasberg
- Americas operations: Morenci (Arizona) is the largest US copper mine; Cerro Verde (Peru) and El Abra (Chile) provide South American exposure

## Key Watch Points

**1. Copper price realization** — FCX often realizes a slight discount to LME on some sales. Q2 realized price vs. LME spot will determine EPS vs. consensus.

**2. Grasberg volumes** — underground ramp continues; each quarter showing higher volumes is positive for cost per pound (fixed cost absorption).

**3. Cost guidance** — any change to site unit cost guidance would move the earnings model. FCX unit costs are sensitive to fuel prices and labor (Indonesian wage pressures at Grasberg).

**4. Capital allocation** — FCX generates significant FCF at current copper prices. Watch for buyback acceleration or special dividend commentary, which would confirm management confidence in the elevated price environment.

## Setup

Hold at current levels. FCX would become a Buy below $60 (15% pullback, ~15x forward EBITDA) where the structural copper thesis provides meaningful upside vs. risk. The copper deficit story is real; the near-term setup at consensus is neutral.
