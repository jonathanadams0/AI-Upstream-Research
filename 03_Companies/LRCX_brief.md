# Lam Research (LRCX) — 30-Day Company Brief
**Date:** June 3, 2026 | **Rating:** Buy | **PT:** $380 | **Current:** $343.71

---

## Summary

Lam Research is in the middle of what CEO Tim Archer is calling a "renaissance" for wafer fab equipment, with the company explicitly guiding $140B+ in 2026 WFE and accelerating its own share-of-WFE gains from the low-30s% toward the high-30s%. The stock has outperformed WFE by a wide margin (+95% YTD vs. ~60% for the broader semi equipment group) and now trades above the consensus mean PT of $320 — but we maintain Buy at $380 because we believe consensus is too conservative on the HBM and NAND upgrade tailwinds that Lam has uniquely flagged. CEO: "Everybody in equipment's going to do well over the next several years. We're going to do even better."

## Key Developments (May – June 2026)

**May 20 — WFE SAM expansion above mid-30s%**
Lam disclosed that AI workloads are driving higher etch and deposition intensity, expanding their served available market to "slightly above the mid-30% range of WFE in 2026." This is above prior guidance and implies Lam is outpacing overall WFE growth. NAND: AI data center adoption of higher-layer-count QLC SSDs is accelerating 200-layer+ conversion activity — Lam now expects most of the $40B NAND conversion spending to occur before end of 2027 (accelerated). Source: [Nasdaq, May 20, 2026](https://app.bigdata.com/documents/E1FA551485182E947239CF518E47AEE4?cnum=2)

**May 27 — Bernstein Conference: HBM dominates DRAM wafer volume**
CEO Tim Archer at Bernstein: Lam is the dominant supplier of TSV (through-silicon via) processing for HBM stacking — essentially all major HBM manufacturers use Lam TSV tools. "We own the TSV. We do most of the TSV for everybody in the industry." As AI GPUs add more HBM (H100: 80GB HBM3; B200: 192GB HBM3E; next gen: higher still), Lam's content per rack grows structurally. Source: [Quartr Transcripts, May 27, 2026](https://app.bigdata.com/documents/C609B2175D1C06A749E83C79C8EFA07B?cnum=35)

**May 29 — CFRA raises PT to $356; WFE estimate raised to $155B**
CFRA raised its 2026 WFE estimate from $140B to $155B, citing strong results across the semi equipment space and growing hyperscaler capex forecasts. CFRA: "LRCX recently accelerated its $40B NAND upgrade spending forecast from 'several years' to YE CY 27... upgrades offer faster revenue realization." Raised FY27 EPS estimate to $8.25. Source: [MT Newswires, May 29, 2026](https://app.bigdata.com/documents/40D208ERA8D24325958E32B713E1A4C9B?cnum=1)

**May 31 — CEO Archer at Bernstein: WFE $140B "with bias to upside"**
Archer said AI has driven demand for higher performance across logic, memory, storage, and packaging simultaneously — "In AI right now, more is more." He sees sustained demand across compute (TSMC logic), HBM (SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron), NAND storage, and advanced packaging, each driven by the transition from training → inference → agentic → physical AI. Source: [Nasdaq, May 31, 2026](https://app.bigdata.com/documents/04241F04D8DBE07EFF31F173B5AF4AF2?cnum=1)

**June 2 — June Conference: "We're going to outperform WFE for the next several years"**
Archer: Lam's SAM is growing from low-30s% to high-30s% of WFE because of new products (Akara, Vantex, Mali, Halo) addressing process steps that weren't previously in Lam's portfolio. "We outperformed WFE last year. We're going to outperform it this year. We're going to outperform it for the next several years based on what I see." ## Why Buy Above Consensus PT

The consensus mean PT of $320.61 was largely set after Q3 results (March quarter). Since then:
1. CEO explicitly guided WFE to $140B+ with upside bias (consensus was $130-140B)
2. NAND upgrade spending accelerated ($40B now expected by end of 2027, not "several years")
3. CFRA raised its own WFE estimate to $155B
4. HBM content per Lam is growing — every new HBM generation requires more etch steps, more TSV processing
5. New products (Akara, Vantex, etc.) are expanding SAM from low-30s% to high-30s% of WFE

Our PT of $380 = 44x our FY2027 EPS estimate of ~$8.65 (vs. CFRA's $8.25 FY27 estimate). This is modestly above the current 44x forward P/E but we think justified by the accelerating SAM expansion narrative and NAND upgrade cadence pull-forward.

**The primary risk to the Buy is China export control escalation** — any further DUV restrictions that match EUV's treatment would cut ~25-30% of Lam's revenue. This is the main reason we're not at High-High conviction despite the business trajectory.
