**Q1 — NVIDIA partnership revenue/backlog: is the $500M deal generating actual activity?**
The Screener triggered on June 2 based on the NVIDIA announcement. But the note explicitly flags: "Backlog: not yet disclosed; monitor next earnings." This Q2 print is the first earnings with this deal on the books. Watch for: (a) any optical communications segment revenue growth call-out tied to AI data center demand, (b) any backlog or contracted order disclosure related to the NVIDIA JV, (c) management language distinguishing AI fiber demand from traditional telecom. If no quantifiable signal appears, the thesis lacks a near-term catalyst.

**Q2 — Optical communications vs. display glass: is the mix shifting?**
The bear thesis on GLW has always been display glass in secular decline (TV/laptop/smartphone). The NVIDIA deal is supposed to accelerate optical communications growth fast enough to rebalance the portfolio. Watch segment revenue split: if optical communications is growing ≥20% YoY while display is down 5–10%, the mix shift thesis is working. If both segments are flat or declining, the display headwind is not being offset.

**Q3 — Core EPS delivery vs. $0.73–0.77 guidance (and $0.75–0.76 consensus)**
Management guided core EPS of $0.73–$0.77 (+25% YoY) and core sales ~$4.6B (+14% YoY). Consensus clusters at $0.75–$0.76. This print is the baseline for whether the new AI optical story is also translating into earnings growth. A beat toward $0.80+ with raised guidance would be the signal to revisit the rating. An in-line or miss with downward revision would confirm the Hold.
