## What Management Guided for Q2

Directly from the Q1'26 earnings call (April 22):

**Electrification:** "Q2 revenues should be between $3.3B and $3.5B, a significant year-over-year increase. We also expect strong year-over-year EBITDA margin expansion from higher volume, productivity and favorable price with a margin rate modestly above Q1'26 levels." Q1 Electrification margin was 17.8%, so Q2 tracking toward 18%+.

**Power:** "Power is projected to deliver 16%–18% organic revenue growth and 17%–19% EBITDA margin" (FY guidance — Q2 should be in line with this trajectory).

**Gas turbines:** "Quarter-to-date [as of April 22], we have booked more power equipment orders in terms of value than we did in all of Q1'26." Q1 had $10B in Power orders — so April alone exceeded that pace.

**Pricing:** "Orders in the first half of '26 to be priced 10–20 points higher than Q4'25 orders on a dollar per kW basis." This is the margin expansion engine — new-price orders converting into revenue.

**Production capacity:** ~20 GW annualized output by Q3'26 — a manufacturing scale-up that supports backlog conversion.

**Wind:** "Higher second half onshore turbine shipments given recent orders profile." Q2 likely still a drag (low double-digit revenue decline FY), with modest EBITDA loss improvement vs. Q1's ($0.4B) loss.

**FCF:** "Positive free cash flow in Q2, given ongoing focus on aligning timing of inflows and outflows along with the impact of down payments." Q1 FCF was $4.8B (large due to Prolec M&A proceeds). Q2 FCF should be lower but positive — watch for contract liability growth as new orders convert to cash receipts.

## Key Questions for Q2

**1. Gas turbine backlog vs. 110 GW target**
- Q1 ended at 100 GW (backlog + slot reservations). Management guided ≥110 GW by YE2026.
- Q2 pace implies significant progress: CEO flagged April orders already exceeded all of Q1.
- **Bullish signal:** 105+ GW by Q2 end suggests on or ahead of track. Bears will watch for any deceleration.

**2. Electrification data center order pace**
- Q1 delivered $2.4B in single-quarter data center Electrification orders vs. all of FY2025.
- Q2 is the first quarter with full Prolec integration. Watch for $2B+ again or commentary on pipeline.
- Q1 Electrification orders were up 86% YoY organically.
- **Bear risk from May 27 CEO comments:** State-level pushback on data centers could slow new orders. But management explicitly separated new-order pace from existing backlog fulfillment.

**3. Wind segment trajectory**
- Q1 Wind EBITDA loss was ($0.4B). FY guidance calls for ~($400M) total Wind losses.
- If Q1 already burned ($0.4B), FY target is very back-end loaded or the guidance was already for most losses front-loaded. Watch for any improvement in onshore Wind services commentary.
- **Impairment risk:** Wind goodwill ~$3.3B. Any reserve action would be non-cash but sentiment-negative.

**4. Robotech acquisition close**
- Expected Q3 2026 close. May get a timeline update or regulatory status.

**5. Pricing disclosure on new orders**
- Management referenced "10–20 points higher" pricing on new orders vs. Q4'25 backlog. Any concrete dollar-per-kW disclosure would quantify the forward margin expansion trajectory.

**6. $200B backlog target by 2027**
- CNBC noted management now expects $200B total backlog by 2027, a year ahead of schedule. Q2 backlog update (was $163B at Q1) will show progress toward this.
