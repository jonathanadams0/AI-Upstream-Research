# Applied Digital (APLD)

> Applied Digital operates HPC (high-performance computing) data centers in North Dakota and Texas, purpose-built for GPU-intensive AI workloads. Unlike traditional data centers, APLD's facilities are designed for the 30-100 kW/rack densities of AI infrastructure. This is a smaller, independent alternative to Core Scientific — without the pending acquisition complexity.

**Sector:** [[Data-Center-Infra]] | **Rating:** Buy | **PT:** $60 | **Current:** ~$48 | **Upside:** +26%

## 2. Thesis

1. **Pure-play AI data center operator.** APLD builds and operates data centers specifically for AI/HPC workloads. Unlike EQIX or DLR which serve mixed enterprise customers, every MW of APLD capacity goes to AI compute.

2. **Location arbitrage.** North Dakota and Texas facilities benefit from low electricity costs (wind-heavy grids in ND, competitive market in TX) and available land for expansion.

3. **Discount to Core Scientific.** CORZ is being acquired by CoreWeave at a premium. APLD is a similar business without the CoreWeave anchor tenant, which is why it trades at a discount — but also why there's upside if APLD signs comparable contracts.

4. **Aschenbrenner position.** Situational Awareness held APLD as part of its "AI hosting" basket alongside CORZ and IREN, validating the thesis that miner-to-AI-host pivots are materially undervalued.

## 3. Bear Case

**Scenario:** HPC/AI hosting market becomes commoditized as Tier-1 hyperscalers undercut on price. Applied Digital's balance sheet refinancing encounters difficulty as interest rates remain elevated. GPU pricing falls materially, reducing the economics of AI inference hosting.

**Implied Bear Price:** $20 (~53% downside from ~$43 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Balance sheet refinancing (any debt maturing within 12 months) fails to close on favorable terms.** APLD is pre-profit and capital-intensive. Any disclosure that debt refinancing is delayed or priced at materially higher rates signals financial stress.
2. **Revenue per GPU per hour falls more than 25% from current levels.** The HPC hosting model is fundamentally a utilization and pricing business. If H100/H200 hosting rates collapse as supply increases, the economics of the business model deteriorate rapidly.
3. **GPU utilization drops below 70% for two consecutive quarters.** Current high utilization underpins the economics. Below 70% sustained means demand has softened or competition has increased beyond what current capacity can absorb.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single quarter of lower utilization from normal commissioning cycles, short-term GPU pricing volatility, or timing of large customer contract renewals.
