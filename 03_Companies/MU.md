## 2. Thesis (3 Bullets)

1. **HBM3E is Micron's inflection.** Micron is the third HBM supplier (after SK Hynix #1, Samsung #2) but the only one with a US manufacturing base. US government and US hyperscaler customers have strategic reasons to qualify Micron as a HBM source. HBM carries 3-5x the ASP of conventional DRAM.

2. **Idaho CHIPS Act investment cements US-only supply chain.** The $40B+ CHIPS Act investment in Idaho creates the first large-scale domestic HBM manufacturing capability. Federal subsidies reduce capital risk and lock in long-term demand from defense and critical infrastructure customers.

3. **NAND recovery adds to DRAM strength.** AI inference demand has created a new NAND floor; QLC SSDs for AI workloads are growing faster than the PC/server replacement cycle. Micron's 232-layer NAND is competitive globally.

## 3. Why Hold, Not Buy

At $1,070 (~$1.18T market cap), the HBM thesis and CHIPS Act tailwind are substantially priced in. The stock has already run dramatically. Hold until a meaningful pullback creates a better entry. Buy case: below $900. Source: Bigdata.com tearsheet, June 2026.

## 4. Key Risks

1. SK Hynix / Samsung competitive response — both are ramping HBM4
2. Memory cyclicality — even with AI floor, DRAM can overbuild
3. China ban retaliation — Micron had China sales restricted in 2023; could recur
4. Idaho fab execution — large capital projects carry schedule risk
