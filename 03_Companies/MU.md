## 2. Thesis (3 Bullets)

1. **HBM4 is the next leg, and Micron is ahead of schedule.** HBM4 12-high is ramping 2x faster than HBM3E 12-high did; Micron has already shipped >$1B in HBM4 revenue as of Q3 FY2026. HBM3E + HBM4 are fully booked through CY2027, with demand extending into 2028. The competitive concern (SK Hynix dominance) is fading as all three suppliers are now qualified and production-ramping.

2. **The revenue step-change is structural, not cyclical.** Q3 FY2026 revenue was $41.46B (+346% YoY), driven by data center ($25B+) and NAND ($9.9B, +361% YoY). Q4 guide of $50B at 86% gross margin signals this is not a beat-and-retreat quarter — it's a new floor. 16 Strategic Customer Agreements (~$22B) have been signed, locking future revenue with hyperscalers.

3. **Idaho CHIPS Act + customer lock-in defends the moat.** The $40B+ federal investment in Idaho creates the only large-scale domestic HBM manufacturing base. Combined with signed SCAs, Micron now has multi-year revenue visibility and a government backstop on capital risk — reducing the cyclicality risk that historically plagued the memory sector.

## 4. Key Risks

1. **SK Hynix / Samsung competitive response** — both ramping HBM4; risk is now more about market share than qualification (all three qualified). Diminished but not zero.
2. **Memory cyclicality** — even with AI floor and SCAs, DRAM can overbuild. The SCAs reduce but don't eliminate this risk.
3. **China restriction re-emergence** — Micron lost China sales in 2023; escalating US-China trade war could recur. Invalidation trigger: 8-K disclosing new restriction.
4. **Idaho fab execution** — large capital projects carry schedule risk; delays would shift HBM capacity timelines.
5. **Gross margin ceiling** — 86% guide is exceptional; a sustained margin decline would signal ASP erosion before volumes catch up.

None of the original three triggers has formally fired. However, three new structural developments not present at upgrade time materially alter the risk/reward.

### New Bears (Not in Original Thesis)

**1. SK Hynix Nasdaq ADR listing ($28–29B, debuting July 10)**
This is the most significant new structural development. SK Hynix is raising ~$29B on Nasdaq — the largest foreign company offering in US market history — and explicitly stated it seeks to be "valued in line with Micron." The implications are: (a) SK Hynix's cost of capital falls, accelerating its HBM4 capacity ramp; (b) US-listed investors now have direct access to the #1 HBM supplier, creating a valuation headwind for MU's premium; (c) the supply of HBM from SK Hynix ($29B of fresh capital to deploy) grows faster than the original thesis assumed. The original note treated SK Hynix as a competitive risk but assumed limited access to cheap US capital. That assumption is now wrong.

**2. Samsung Q2 2026 earnings — "sell the news" dynamic**
Samsung reported an 18-19x surge in Q2 operating profit (AI-driven memory demand) and the stock sold off. This is the market flagging that the memory bull thesis is well-priced at current multiples. Additionally, Samsung's HBM4 base die production (on 4nm process) is ramping aggressively, signaling it will compete directly with Micron's HBM4 rather than ceding share. The original thesis leaned on Samsung lagging in HBM qualification — that window may be closing.

**3. Michael Burry short (opened July 3 at $1,051.87)**
Burry's thesis: (a) MU is furthest above its 200-day MA since 1984; (b) South Korea's semiconductor capex surge (Samsung + SK Hynix combined) historically signals the beginning of a downturn, not a continuation; (c) HBM is "just another in a very long series" of cycle products, not a permanent moat. The Burry short is not a thesis change by itself, but it adds high-profile market attention to the bear case and his Korea capex argument directly addresses the question of whether supply will overshoot demand.

**4. Insider selling at highest rate since 2010**
>$100M in insider sales over 24 months, accelerating. CEO Mehrotra sold ~$46M on June 26. Not a single insider has been a net buyer since the upgrade. Elevated insider selling at an all-time-high stock price is a yellow flag on management's own assessment of near-term upside vs. downside.

### Re-underwritten Bear Case ($800)

The original $900 bear assumed: HBM demand intact, no new formal China restriction, Micron retaining ~30% HBM market share. Revised bear at $800 reflects:
- SK Hynix supply ramp accelerating (lower MU HBM share, ASP pressure in 2027)
- Samsung closing the HBM4 qualification gap
- Memory cycle risk re-emerging if hyperscaler capex moderates at all
- Bear scenario: Q4 GM beats but Q1 FY2027 guide disappoints → re-rating to 7x NTM EPS → $800 implies ~$114 NTM EPS × 7x

$800 is not a base case — it is a scenario where two of the three original triggers fire (GM decline + SK Hynix/Samsung ASP pressure) against the backdrop of a supply-driven memory down-cycle beginning in H1 FY2027.

### Re-underwritten Bull / PT ($1,200)

Original $1,500 PT used 12.5x NTM EPS on ~$120. Revised to 10x NTM EPS on $120 = $1,200. The multiple compression (12.5x → 10x) reflects: (a) SK Hynix now US-listed at presumably lower multiple = sector multiple compression; (b) insider selling reduces conviction on management's own NTM outlook; (c) cycle risk warrants a lower multiple than the post-Q3 euphoria implied. The earnings power ($120 NTM) is NOT being revised — Q4 guide is intact and confirmed as of last report.

### Rating Change: Buy → Hold

**Rationale:** At $938 with a $1,200 PT (+28%) and $800 bear (−15%), the risk/reward is approximately 1.9:1. That's below the 3:1 threshold required for a Buy in this vault. The fundamental thesis is not broken — HBM4 demand is real, SCAs lock revenue, Idaho provides a government backstop — but the competitive landscape has shifted enough to reduce forward confidence in the multiple, and the stock is too close to bear case to add. Hold until: (a) Q4 FY2026 report (~Sept 25) confirms the earnings trajectory; (b) SK Hynix listing impact on sector multiples can be assessed; (c) Apple-CXMT situation clarifies (rumor vs. actual order placement).

**Do not add:** Stock is at $938, only 17% above the new $800 bear. The original "don't add below $1,166" rule is superseded — at these prices, no new capital should be deployed regardless of intraday pullbacks.

**Re-upgrade path:** Q4 revenue ≥$49B + GM ≥85% + no formal China restriction disclosed + SK Hynix listing has not caused material MU multiple compression → reassess to Buy at Q4 report. If all conditions met and stock is still ≤$1,100, the reward/risk would be attractive again.
