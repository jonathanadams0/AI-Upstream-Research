## 2. Thesis (3 Bullets)

1. **ASML is the sole supplier of EUV lithography, and EUV is non-negotiable for every advanced node below 7nm — making ASML structurally immune to the competitive dynamics that affect most semiconductor companies.** TSMC, Samsung, and Intel cannot produce leading-edge logic chips without ASML EUV systems. No competitor is within a decade of replication. EUV development began in earnest in the 1990s and took approximately 30 years before commercial shipment (first production NXE system delivered ~2017). ASML has disclosed cumulative R&D investment in the tens of billions of euros across that period (note: ASML does not provide a single cumulative R&D figure; annual R&D is ~€4B in FY2025). This is not a moat — it is a wall.

2. **High-NA EUV is the next step-function that deepens and extends ASML's monopoly through the 2030s.** The transition from existing EUV (NXE series) to High-NA EUV (TWINSCAN EXE:5000 and EXE:5200) is required for sub-2nm nodes. Intel received the first High-NA EUV system in late 2023 (pre-production/R&D tool); TSMC and Samsung are qualified customers with initial deliveries ongoing in 2025. High-NA systems are priced at approximately €350–400M per system, versus ~€200M for current NXE:3600D systems — roughly 1.75–2x the per-unit ASP uplift. *(Verify exact ASP figures from 20-F or investor day materials.)*

3. **The backlog provides multi-year revenue visibility that is rare for a capital equipment company.** ASML closed FY2025 with a record €38.8B backlog (~3x trailing revenue at current run-rate), including €25.5B of EUV specifically. With a 2026 revenue guide of €34–39B ($37-43B), ASML has effectively pre-sold its next 12 months of production. The risk is not demand — it is ASML's ability to manufacture and deliver complex, custom optical systems at scale.

## 3. AI Exposure

**Classification: Direct**

### AI-Driven Demand Profile

ASML's AI exposure is structural and upstream: every AI chip — NVIDIA H100/H200/B200 GPUs, AMD MI300X, Google TPU v5, Trainium2, Inferentia — is manufactured using ASML lithography systems at TSMC (N3, N2) and Samsung (3nm GAA) fabs. ASML does not sell to AI companies directly; it sells to the foundries and IDMs, making its revenue a leading indicator of AI semiconductor capex rather than a lagging indicator of AI adoption.

**Key demand vectors:**
- **Logic / AI accelerators:** TSMC N3/N2 capacity additions for NVIDIA, Apple, AMD, and hyperscaler custom ASICs are the primary EUV demand driver. N2 requires more EUV layers per wafer than N3 (TSMC has disclosed N2 uses ~25% more EUV layers than N3 per wafer), driving per-wafer WFE intensity higher. Each capacity addition at TSMC requires a corresponding EUV tool order from ASML.
- **High-NA EUV for sub-2nm:** First High-NA EUV deliveries began in 2023 (Intel, R&D/pre-production), with commercial volume deliveries to TSMC and Samsung in 2025. Each system ~€350–400M ASP — 1.75–2x the NXE series. Required for sub-2nm node production (TSMC A16/A14, Intel 14A, Samsung 2nm).
- **HBM and advanced memory:** SK Hynix, Samsung, and Micron are deploying EUV in HBM3E DRAM production. HBM stacking requires tighter CD control (smaller features, tighter tolerances) that benefits from EUV vs. multi-patterning DUV. *(Verify EUV adoption rate in HBM vs. standard DRAM from memory manufacturer capex disclosures.)*

**Quantification:**
- EUV system revenue surged ~391% to €11.6B in FY2025 (48 system deliveries)
- Backlog: €38.8B total, €25.5B EUV-specific — represents ~15+ months of forward visibility at current production rates
- 2026 WFE market growth: ASML's FY2026 revenue guidance of €34–39B implies continued growth; Tokyo Electron management has cited 20%+ WFE growth expectations for 2026. ASML has not issued a specific WFE market size forecast but its guidance is consistent with strong AI-driven demand. *(Verify ASML's most recent WFE commentary from Q1 2026 earnings call.)*

**Durability: Multi-decade structural.** Semiconductor roadmaps through 2030+ require EUV at every leading-edge node. High-NA EUV extends the dependency to 2035+. No credible alternative pathway exists.

**Key risk to AI exposure thesis:** U.S./Dutch export controls on China sales. China was **~29% of FY2025 total revenue** (down from ~36% in FY2024 and ~49% peak in Q3 2023). EUV is already banned for export to China; DUV restrictions tightened in September 2023 (Netherlands government restrictions on ASML export licenses). Any further escalation (e.g., restrictions on older DUV nodes below 200nm, or service/maintenance restrictions) would further reduce China revenue. The remaining ~29% is at ongoing policy risk — it represents ~$10.7B of FY2025 revenue.

## 5. Industry & Competitive Position

**Market Position:** Sole global supplier of EUV lithography. In DUV, ASML competes with Nikon and Canon but holds dominant market share (~80-85% of DUV market) [VERIFY share estimates]. In metrology/inspection, ASML competes with KLA (KLAC) and Onto Innovation.

**Moat:**
1. **Technology monopoly:** EUV requires 13.5nm wavelength light generated by laser-excited tin plasma — a 30-year engineering achievement involving 5,000+ suppliers and approximately 100,000 components per system (per ASML corporate materials and published company descriptions). No competitor has produced a commercial EUV system. China's SMEE (Shanghai Micro Electronics Equipment) has stated ambitions but is estimated to be 10–15 years behind ASML's current EUV capability by industry observers.
2. **Customer lock-in:** Chip manufacturers invest billions in process development around specific ASML tool configurations. Switching costs are effectively infinite — a foundry cannot mid-generation swap its lithography supplier without destroying yield and process IP.
3. **IP portfolio:** ASML holds tens of thousands of patents covering optical design, control systems, light source technology, and Holistic Lithography software. The cross-licensing and patent portfolio create additional barriers to competition.
4. **Supply chain control:** ASML's key subsystems are either captive or structurally locked in: (a) light source via **Cymer** (acquired 2013 for ~$2.6B; based in San Diego); (b) optical columns via **Carl Zeiss SMT** — ASML owns ~24.9% stake in Carl Zeiss SMT Holding GmbH & Co. KG (the optics subsidiary of Carl Zeiss AG) under a long-term strategic partnership. Zeiss optics are purpose-built for ASML systems and not commercially available to any competitor.

**Key Competitors:**
- Nikon (DUV only, declining share)
- Canon (DUV mature nodes, niche)
- No EUV competitors

**Porter's Five Forces:**
- Rivalry: **Effectively zero in EUV.** Low-moderate in DUV.
- New entrants: **Negligible.** Prohibitive capital (~tens of billions), IP moat (~30 years of accumulated R&D), and supply chain barriers (Zeiss, Cymer). SMEE (China) has demonstrated immersion DUV capability but is estimated to be at least a decade behind in EUV.
- Substitutes: **None for EUV in leading-edge logic.** Canon's nanoimprint lithography (NIL) has been adopted by Kioxia for select NAND flash patterns — a niche use case — but NIL cannot achieve the overlay precision or throughput required for advanced logic (CMOS, FinFET, GAAFET). EUV remains the only path for sub-7nm logic at production scale.
- Buyer power: **Low.** TSMC, Samsung, and Intel have no alternative supplier for EUV. Backlog dynamics invert typical buyer leverage — customers are competing for delivery slots.
- Supplier power: **Moderate.** Zeiss (optical columns) is a critical single-source supplier and a long-term strategic partner. ASML holds ~24.9% equity stake in Carl Zeiss SMT Holding GmbH & Co. KG — an unusual structure that aligns incentives and provides supply chain visibility, but also concentrates optics risk. Cymer (light source, acquired 2013 for ~$2.6B) is fully owned. *(Verify any updated Zeiss ownership terms or governance arrangements from 20-F.)*

## 9. Risks

1. **China export restrictions (largest near-term risk):** China was ~29% of ASML's FY2025 revenue (~$10.7B), down from ~36% in FY2024 — restrictions are already biting. EUV is already banned for export; DUV restrictions tightened in 2023. Further escalation (e.g., restrictions on older DUV nodes) could remove another large portion of revenue with no near-term offset. *(Verify China revenue % from 20-F geographic breakdown.)*
2. **WFE cyclicality:** Despite the AI supercycle, semiconductor equipment spending is cyclical. If hyperscaler AI capex moderates or chip inventories build, foundry capacity additions could slow — reducing EUV delivery demand.
3. **High-NA execution risk:** High-NA EUV is a massively complex engineering system. Production ramp challenges, yield issues at customer fabs, or adoption delays would impact the next growth phase.
4. **Customer concentration:** TSMC is estimated to be ~35–40% of ASML's revenue (widely cited estimate; consistent with TSMC's ~30-35% share of global WFE). *(Verify from 20-F customer concentration disclosure.)* Any capacity slowdown at TSMC is a direct revenue impact.
5. **Geopolitical:** ASML is a Dutch company subject to U.S. and EU export control policy. Political pressure (U.S. pressure on Dutch government) has already resulted in EUV export restrictions. Risk of further restrictions or even retaliatory measures from China.
6. **Valuation:** At ~52x EV/EBITDA (EV ~$737B / EBITDA ~$14.1B) and ~68x P/E, the stock prices in a long runway of AI-driven WFE growth with minimal room for error. A multiple compression from cycle doubt could be painful even without fundamental deterioration. Stock is at its 52-week high (~$1,925) vs. a stale PT of $1,730.
