## 6. Financial Analysis

### Key Trends

**Revenue Growth:**
- 2022→2023: +$400M, +17.4% YoY
- 2023→2024: +$300M, +11.1% YoY
- 2024→2025: +$900M, **+30.0% YoY** — material acceleration

The 2025 acceleration is the dominant analytical question. A $900M single-year revenue step-up for a company that grew ~$350M/year in the prior two years is not organic in the normal sense. Likely explanations: (1) a significant acquisition closed in late 2024 or early 2025 [VERIFY — debt peaked at $2.2B in 2024, suggesting M&A activity, then declined to $1.6B suggesting deleveraging from FCF and/or divestitures], (2) a major hyperscaler program ramp, or (3) some combination. **This is the single most important figure to reconcile.**

**EBITDA and Margins:**
- 2022: $345M / 15.0% — likely reflects integration costs or mix headwinds post-separation from Pentair [VERIFY separation timeline]
- 2023: $506M / 18.7% — strong margin recovery, +370bps
- 2024: $578M / 19.3% — peak margin in the series, +60bps
- 2025: $678M / 17.4% — margin compression of -190bps despite revenue growth

The 2025 EBITDA margin compression despite significant revenue growth is a yellow flag. In a normal industrial company with operating leverage, 30% revenue growth should expand margins, not compress them. Possible explanations: M&A dilution (acquired business at lower margins), integration costs, competitive pricing to win data center business, or unfavorable mix shift. **Requires explanation before a PT can be set.**

**FCF:**
- 2022→2024: FCF conversion was 91-103% of EBITDA — excellent, indicative of asset-light-ish model with low CapEx intensity
- 2025: FCF drops to $372M vs. $678M EBITDA = **55% conversion** — significant deterioration

The FCF deterioration in 2025 is notable. CapEx increased only modestly ($74M→$93M, +$19M), so the gap is not capital intensity. Working capital build (inventory ahead of large programs, receivables from rapid revenue growth) or acquisition-related cash costs (deal fees, restructuring, earn-outs) are the most likely culprits [VERIFY]. If this is a temporary working capital build, FCF should normalize in 2026 and the stock screen is more attractive. If it reflects a structural change in the cash conversion cycle, the quality of earnings is lower.

**CapEx:**
Consistent, low-intensity CapEx program ($40M→$93M over 4 years, ~2-2.5% of revenue). This is a positive indicator for FCF generativity in a normal year.
