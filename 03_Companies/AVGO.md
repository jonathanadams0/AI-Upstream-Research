# Broadcom Inc. (AVGO)

> The invisible backbone of AI. Broadcom designs the custom AI ASICs for Google (TPU), Meta (MTIA), and Apple (Neural Engine), plus the networking chips (Tomahawk, Trident) inside nearly every enterprise and hyperscale data center switch. No other company has simultaneous dominant positions in both custom AI silicon and AI infrastructure networking.

## 2. Thesis (3 Bullets)

1. **Custom AI silicon is the multi-decade structural winner.** Every hyperscaler that builds a custom chip to reduce Nvidia dependency goes through Broadcom (Google) or Marvell (Amazon, Microsoft). Broadcom's XPU business (custom accelerator design) is growing faster than its core semiconductor business and carries higher margins. Google's TPU v5 enables Google to offer AI inference at 30-40% lower cost than Nvidia H100 — a significant competitive advantage.

2. **Networking is the irreplaceable moat.** Broadcom's Tomahawk and Trident switch ASICs are inside virtually every enterprise data center switch and most hyperscale switches. Every time a data center upgrades from 100G to 400G to 800G Ethernet, they buy new Broadcom silicon. This is recurring revenue tied to bandwidth expansion that has no ceiling.

3. **AI Ethernet vs. InfiniBand.** Broadcom is a primary beneficiary if AI clusters shift from Nvidia's proprietary InfiniBand to standard Ethernet (which is exactly what is happening). Broadcom's 800G Ethernet ASICs are in Arista and Cisco switches used in AI clusters — cutting Nvidia out of the networking revenue.

## 3. Investment View

Buy/$560. At $477, Broadcom trades at ~35x NTM earnings. Premium justified by: (1) AI custom silicon is early innings, (2) networking is recurring and growing, (3) VMware integration (acquired 2023) adds recurring software revenue. The combination of AI silicon + networking + enterprise software creates a unique earnings durability profile.

## 5. Bear Case

**Scenario:** Aschenbrenner's short thesis plays out: custom AI silicon market fragments as Google, Meta, and Apple bring more chip design in-house over the next 3-5 years, reducing Broadcom's role. AI networking semiconductor market slows as hyperscalers complete initial cluster builds. VMware integration absorbs management bandwidth.

**Implied Bear Price:** $350 (~15% downside from ~$414 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Google or Meta discloses a transition away from Broadcom for next-generation custom ASIC.** This is the Aschenbrenner bear thesis. Any earnings call disclosure from Google (TPU strategy) or Meta (MTIA strategy) indicating reduced Broadcom dependency for the next-generation chip would be the signal.
2. **AI semiconductor revenue guidance cut more than 15% in a single quarter.** Broadcom has guided AI revenue to $60-90B over the next 3 years. Any mid-year guidance reduction would validate the Aschenbrenner concern that the custom silicon opportunity is smaller than expected.
3. **VMware integration costs exceed $2B annually for more than two years.** The VMware acquisition thesis requires $8.5B+ in annual run-rate synergies. If integration costs remain elevated and synergies are delayed, the acquisition dilutes rather than accrets.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single quarter of enterprise software volatility, timing differences in chip revenue recognition, or macro-driven enterprise spending slowness.
