## 2. Thesis (3 Bullets)

1. **Copper is the indispensable bottleneck in the AI infrastructure buildout, and SCCO controls more of the ground than anyone else.** Every hyperscaler data center, every grid upgrade needed to power it, and every EV charging network amplifying that grid demand consumes copper at scale — and the market faces a structural supply deficit that SCCO's reserve base (estimated at 43+ million tonnes [VERIFY]) is uniquely positioned to address over the coming decades.

2. **SCCO's EBITDA margin of 59% in 2025 is not a commodity fluke — it reflects genuinely low-cost Peruvian and Mexican assets that widen the moat as copper prices rise.** With $7.9B in EBITDA on $13.4B of revenue and FCF generation sustaining $3.4B annually, the business is generating returns well above any reasonable cost of capital even before additional production volumes come online from major growth projects.

3. **The growth pipeline is real and permitted, not aspirational.** Unlike most copper developers, SCCO has projects with operating history (Cuajone, Toquepala, IMMSA) and advanced-stage expansion projects (Tía María, Los Chancas, El Arco [VERIFY]) that could meaningfully increase production capacity over the next 5–10 years — but permitting and community relations risk in Peru remains the central execution variable.

## 3. AI Exposure

**Classification: Indirect**

**AI-Driven Demand Mechanism:**
Copper is the essential conductor underpinning every layer of AI infrastructure. The demand linkage operates through three channels:
- *Data center construction and internal wiring:* Each hyperscale data center requires an estimated 15–30+ tonnes of copper per MW of capacity [VERIFY], used in busbars, cabling, transformers, and cooling systems.
- *Power grid transmission and distribution:* AI data centers require dedicated utility-scale grid connections; every mile of new transmission line is copper-intensive.
- *EV and energy storage co-investment:* The same policy and capital cycle driving AI power demand is simultaneously driving EV adoption, each EV requiring ~80–100kg of copper vs. ~25kg in an ICE vehicle [VERIFY].

**Estimated AI-Linked Revenue Contribution:**
Not directly quantifiable at the company level — copper is a fungible commodity and SCCO does not disaggregate end-market demand. However, Wood Mackenzie and CRU Group have estimated that data center-related copper demand could represent 1–2 million tonnes per year of incremental global demand by 2030 [VERIFY], against current global production of ~22 million tonnes [VERIFY]. SCCO's ~1 million tonne annual production would represent ~4–5% of that global supply [VERIFY].

**Durability Assessment: Structural.**
Unlike cyclical commodity upswings driven by construction or manufacturing, the AI and electrification drivers of copper demand are policy-backed, capex-committed (hyperscaler datacenter programs are multi-year), and supply-side constrained — the average time from copper discovery to production is 16+ years [VERIFY]. This is not a 12-month trade.

## 4. Business Overview

**Segments (approximate revenue split, 2025A — analyst to verify from 10-K segment disclosures):**
- **Peru Operations** (~55–60% of revenue [VERIFY]): Toquepala and Cuajone open-pit mines; among the largest and lowest-cost copper mines globally. Also produces molybdenum and silver as by-products.
- **Mexico Operations** (~35–40% of revenue [VERIFY]): Buenavista del Cobre (world's largest copper mine by reserves [VERIFY]), La Caridad, and IMMSA underground mines. Also produces zinc, lead, and coal.
- **Exploration / Other** (~low single digits [VERIFY])

**Geography:** Peru and Mexico are the sole operating jurisdictions. Both are Tier 1 mining countries but carry distinct political and community-relations risk profiles.

**Revenue Model:** Commodity revenue — realized copper price × volume sold. Highly leveraged to copper spot (SCCO does not systematically hedge copper prices [VERIFY]). By-product credits (molybdenum, silver, zinc) provide partial offsets. Revenue is lumpy to the extent copper prices are volatile; volume is relatively stable year to year.

**Key Customers:** Copper is sold into global commodity markets; no single customer concentration [VERIFY]. Primary smelter and trading counterparties are global commodity houses and Asian smelters [VERIFY].

**Parent:** Grupo México (through Americas Mining Corporation) owns ~88% of SCCO [VERIFY]. This creates governance considerations for minority shareholders — capital allocation decisions including dividends are heavily influenced by the controlling shareholder.

## 5. Industry & Competitive Position

**Market Position:** SCCO is the world's largest copper company by reserve base [VERIFY] and one of the top 5 by annual production volume [VERIFY]. It competes with Codelco (state-owned Chile), Freeport-McMoRan ([[FCX]]), BHP ([[BHP]]), Glencore ([[GLEN]]), and Rio Tinto ([[RIO]]).

**Moat Sources:**
- *Reserve life:* Decades of low-cost ore reserves locked in — the critical scarcity in copper is not capital, it is ore bodies. SCCO holds one of the most concentrated reserve positions on the planet [VERIFY].
- *Cost position:* SCCO consistently operates in the first quartile of the global cost curve [VERIFY], meaning it is cash-flow positive even in severe copper price downturns.
- *Vertical integration:* Owns mines, concentrators, smelters, and refineries in both Peru and Mexico, capturing margins across the value chain [VERIFY].
- *Existing infrastructure:* New greenfield copper projects require 15–20 years and $5B+ to develop; SCCO's existing operations cannot be replicated at cost.

**Porter's 5 Forces (Brief):**
- *Supplier power — Low:* Mining equipment and consumables are broadly available; energy is a cost factor but not a bottleneck.
- *Buyer power — Low to Moderate:* Copper is priced on LME; no single buyer can dictate terms, but Chinese smelter consolidation could incrementally pressure treatment/refining charges [VERIFY].
- *Substitution threat — Low:* Aluminum can substitute for copper in some applications but not at scale in high-density power and data wiring [VERIFY].
- *New entrant threat — Very Low:* Permitting timelines, capital requirements, and the scarcity of Tier 1 ore bodies make new entry nearly impossible on a relevant timescale.
- *Rivalry — Moderate:* Oligopolistic structure; SCCO, FCX, Codelco, and Glencore collectively control a large share of global primary supply [VERIFY].

**EBITDA:**
- Margins have *expanded* through the cycle: 52.0% (2022) → 50.5% (2023) → 56.1% (2024) → 59.0% (2025). The 2025 EBITDA margin of 59% on $7.9B is exceptional for any industrial company and reflects operating leverage on fixed-cost infrastructure.

**Free Cash Flow:**
