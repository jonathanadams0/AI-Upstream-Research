## 2. Thesis (3 bullets)

1. **Gas turbine oligopoly + sold-out orderbook = pricing power.** GEV is one of three global heavy-duty gas turbine OEMs (with Siemens Energy and Mitsubishi Heavy). 2026 and 2027 delivery slots are fully booked; orders extend to 2028-2029. New slot reservations are pricing 10-20 points above existing backlog — margin expansion is structurally locked in as old-price backlog converts.

2. **Electrification segment is the underappreciated grid play.** Transformers (via Prolec GE, now 100%-owned), HVDC, switchgear, and synchronous condensers all benefit from the same data center load growth driving turbine demand. Prolec adds ~$4B in backlog plus captive transformer capacity — strategically critical given the [[Transformer Supply Crunch]].

3. **Direct hyperscaler exposure is scaling fast.** >$2B of data-center-linked electrification orders in 2025 (3x 2024). Supplying turbines to Chevron/Engine No. 1 plants serving Microsoft AI workloads. Management discussing "volume agreements" with hyperscalers extending to 2035.

## 3. AI Exposure

  - Transformers (Prolec) — beneficiary of [[Transformer Supply Crunch]]

- **Quantification:**

  - Slot reservation pricing: +10-20 pts above current backlog

- **Durability:** Multi-cycle. Management has publicly stated turbine capacity is "sold out" through 2029 and is discussing volume agreements to 2035. This is not a one-quarter story — it's a structural supply-demand mismatch that takes years to resolve because new turbine manufacturing capacity has 3-5 year lead times.

- **Management commentary:** CEO Scott Strazik (Q4'25): "We increased our backlog to $150 billion, with better equipment margins, and are entering 2026 with significant momentum." CFO Ken Parks noted Prolec's ~$4B backlog provides real opportunities for the next three years.

## 4. Business Overview

### Segments (2025 Revenue: $38.1B)

- **Power** (~$19.8B rev, 52%): Gas turbines (largest installed base globally), nuclear services, hydro, steam. Q4'25 orders +52% organic. **The AI story primarily lives here.**
- **Wind** (~$9B rev): Onshore + offshore wind turbines. Offshore outlook is poor (Vineyard Wind litigation, industry headwinds). Drag on valuation.
- **Electrification** (~$9B rev): Grid solutions, HVDC, transformers (Prolec), power conversion, grid software. **The secondary AI story — growing faster than Power.**

### Geography
- Global installed base; US, Europe, Middle East are the biggest growth regions for data center-linked orders

### Key Customers
- Utilities (traditional)
- Hyperscalers directly (growing): Microsoft via Chevron/Engine No. 1 partnerships
- Oil majors and industrial: Chevron power plants for data centers

### Revenue Model
- Equipment sales (lumpy, backlog-driven)
- Long-tail services revenue on installed base (high-margin, recurring) — the "razor blade" model. Every installed turbine/transformer generates service revenue for decades.

## 5. Industry & Competitive Position

See sector primer: [[Power-Gen-Grid]]

- **Market position:**
  - Largest installed gas turbine fleet in the world (>7,000 units)
  - Heavy-duty gas turbine oligopoly: GEV + Siemens Energy + Mitsubishi Heavy
  - Grid equipment: meaningful share alongside Siemens Energy, Hitachi Energy, ABB

- **Moat:**
  - Scale (installed base = services annuity)
  - Technology (HA-class gas turbines at efficiency frontier)
  - Switching costs (utilities don't casually change turbine OEMs — 30+ year asset life)
  - Supply-side constraint (new entrants can't add capacity in <5 years)

- **Key competitors:** [[SMNEY]] (Siemens Energy), Mitsubishi Heavy Industries, Hitachi Energy, ABB (grid)

- **Porter's 5:**
  - Rivalry: **Low-Medium** — rational oligopoly in gas turbines, more competitive in wind and grid
  - New entrants: **Very low** — capital + technology + installed base barriers
  - Substitutes: **Medium long-term** — renewables + storage could erode baseload demand, but not in this decade
  - Buyer power: **Currently weak** — backlog is sold out, GEV has pricing power. This inverts if the cycle breaks.
  - Supplier power: **Medium-High** — specialty metals (GOES for transformers, superalloys for turbines) are constrained

## 9. Risks

1. **Execution/supply chain:** Sold-out backlog means any delivery slippage is punished severely. Turbine wait times already stretching to 2030; any quality or supply issue = multi-quarter setback.
2. **Valuation:** 56x P/E leaves no room for error. Sentiment reversal on AI capex (hyperscaler capex cut) could compress the multiple by 20-30% even without fundamental damage.
3. **Wind segment:** Offshore wind litigation (Vineyard) and structurally weak offshore economics could require impairments.
4. **Hyperscaler capex cycle:** Currently the bull case is secular. If AI capex proves cyclical, 2028-2029 backlog becomes at-risk.
5. **Geopolitical:** Currently benefiting from Japan-US investment deal (Hitachi nuclear JV). Reverse-scenario would be a headwind.
6. **Commodity/input costs:** GOES steel, superalloys, copper — input cost pressure could compress equipment margins even with pricing power.
7. **Regulatory/permitting:** New gas plant permitting is the binding constraint on customer project timelines — if customers can't build, orders get pushed.
