## Risks

**Downside:**

1. **Margin compression from labor scarcity.** MEP labor is structurally tight; DC projects are large, complex, margin-sensitive. A miss on execution (subcontractor disputes, cost overruns) would hit 8–10% margins hard. Historical trough margins (~6%) would destroy the earnings model.

2. **Backlog conversion risk.** RPO is a right-to-revenue metric; it is not guaranteed revenue. Projects cancel, defer, or reprice. A 10% RPO cancellation at lower margin mix would be both an EPS miss and a multiple compression event simultaneously.

3. **Concentration risk in hyperscaler capex.** If one or two major DC clients pause or reschedule, the network/comms RPO declines visibly. This is the same tail risk that ground FIX/STRL at peak — high RPO can reverse fast.

4. **Multiple already stretched.** At 32x fwd P/E and above consensus average PT, any earnings disappointment has no cushion. The market is pricing near-perfection; the stock can give back 20–30% on a one-quarter miss.

5. **Industrial services drag.** The ~7% of revenue at ~2% operating margins dilutes returns and provides no narrative value. Hyperscaler clients don't care about oil refinery turnarounds.

**Upside risks to Hold:**
- RPO exceeds $17B in Q2 2026 (further DC wins not yet visible)
- Oppenheimer's $1,100 PT is correct and represents early-adopter consensus
- FIX/STRL multiple compression rather than EME re-rating (mean reversion to sector P/E of 38x would imply EME at ~$1,280)
