Management called Q1 "the trough" — margins were hit by capacity ramp costs and commodity inflation (steel, copper, aluminum, silver) ahead of April price increases.

## What to Watch in Q2

**1. Margin recovery — the central question**
Management explicitly said Q1 is the margin trough and Q2 will show progress. The Boyd Thermal acquisition's capacity ramp costs should normalize, price increases implemented April 1 should begin flowing through, and supply chain commodity pressure should stabilize.

Q1 Electrical Americas margin: 25.6% — below the segment's 32% 2030 target. Q2 guidance implies 22.6-23.0% *blended* segment margin (across all segments including lower-margin non-electrical). Electrical Americas should recover toward 27-28%.

**2. Boyd Thermal integration and DC data center pipeline**
Boyd Thermal is Eaton's entry into white-space data center infrastructure — cold plates, CDUs, cooling systems. This acquisition (closed in 2026) gives Eaton a "grid-to-chip" solution unique in the sector. Any disclosure of Boyd-sourced data center wins or design partnership progress with hyperscalers would be a positive.

Boyd is tracking above its previously guided $1.7B annual sales pace — Q2 should show the first full-quarter contribution and any upward revision would be incremental.

**3. DC orders — will the +240% pace sustain?**
The +240% YoY DC order surge in Q1 is extraordinary. This was partly driven by large hyperscaler framework agreements; Q2 may or may not replicate that pace. Watch for whether orders normalize (acceptable) or decelerate sharply (bearish signal).

**4. Vehicle/eMobility spinoff timeline**
Eaton plans to spin off Vehicle and eMobility segments by end of Q1 2027. Any update on the transaction timeline, structure, or expected value realization will affect how the market prices the residual "pure-play" electrical business. Faster execution = better multiple for remaining portfolio.

**5. Solid-state transformer and higher-voltage DC**
Eaton is quoting on higher-voltage DC projects and expects orders in H2 2026 for later shipments. Any concrete Q2 disclosure of this next-generation product opportunity would be incrementally positive for the longer-term thesis.

## Investment View

At $421 with PT $420, Eaton is essentially at fair value on our current estimate. The electrical business is exceptional — DC orders +240%, backlog +44%, margins recovering, and Boyd Thermal adding a new product dimension. But stock has already priced in the near-term strength (+26% YTD) and trades at 24x EV/EBITDA vs. peers.

Q2 margins recovering toward 25%+ Electrical Americas would confirm the "trough" narrative and could drive modest upside. We'd become more constructive on ETN at $380-390 where the multiple becomes more compelling.
