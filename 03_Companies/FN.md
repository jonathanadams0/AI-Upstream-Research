# Fabrinet (FN)

> Fabrinet manufactures optical transceivers, networking equipment, and precision optical assemblies for companies including Coherent, Lumentum, Ciena, and Arista. It is the "picks and shovels" of the optical transceiver supply chain — a pure-play on transceiver volume growth without the technology risk of the component companies.

## 2. Thesis

1. **Manufacturing leverage on optical transceiver demand.** Every 800G and 1.6T transceiver that Coherent or Lumentum sells requires precision manufacturing. Fabrinet's Thailand facilities provide high-quality, cost-effective manufacturing for the entire optical component industry. As AI data center build-outs drive transceiver volumes, Fabrinet's order book grows proportionally.

2. **Customer diversification reduces single-company risk.** Fabrinet manufactures for Coherent, Lumentum, Ciena, Viavi, Arista, and others. No single customer is dominant. This diversification means Fabrinet captures growth across the entire optical supply chain rather than depending on one company winning.

3. **Capital-light model with growing margins.** Contract manufacturing is lower-margin than component design, but Fabrinet generates excellent FCF with minimal capital intensity. The margin structure supports 25-30x P/E as revenue scales.

## 3. Bear Case

**Scenario:** Key OEM customers (Coherent, Lumentum) lose market share in optical transceiver market, reducing manufacturing volume at Fabrinet's facilities. Industrial laser and automotive sensor segments fail to offset any optical communications softness. Customer concentration creates earnings volatility.

**Implied Bear Price:** $450 (~35% downside from ~$687 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Two or more top-3 OEM customers reduce Fabrinet manufacturing orders more than 20% in the same quarter.** Fabrinet is a contract manufacturer — its revenue follows its customers' end-market demand. Any concurrent weakness at Lumentum, Coherent, and a third OEM would be immediate and material.
2. **Optical communications revenue decelerates below 15% YoY for two consecutive quarters.** AI data center transceiver demand is Fabrinet's growth driver. Below 15% growth implies the hyperscaler buildout cycle is maturing faster than expected.
3. **Gross margin drops below 9%.** Fabrinet's contract manufacturing margins are thin (~11-12%) and reflect volume leverage. Any sustained pricing pressure or volume reduction that compresses gross margin below 9% signals structural deterioration.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter customer inventory drawdown, timing shift in large orders, or normal quarterly lumpiness in industrial laser volumes.
