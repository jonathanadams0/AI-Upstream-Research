## 2. Thesis (3 Bullets)

1. **The uranium business is structurally re-rating, and Energy Fuels holds the most important domestic processing infrastructure.** The White Mesa Mill in Utah is the only conventional uranium mill operating in the U.S. [VERIFY operational status]. As hyperscaler-backed nuclear power demand (Microsoft/Constellation, Google/Kairos, Amazon/X-energy) [VERIFY deal specifics] drives uranium contract prices higher, Energy Fuels is the domestic supplier of last resort — a position that commands a strategic premium independent of spot price.

2. **The REE processing pivot is genuinely differentiated but pre-revenue at meaningful scale.** Energy Fuels has invested in the capability to separate mixed rare earth carbonate into individual oxides — specifically neodymium-praseodymium (NdPr) used in permanent magnets for EV motors and wind turbines — at White Mesa [VERIFY current processing throughput and offtake agreements]. No other Western facility combines uranium and REE processing under one roof. If the U.S. government follows through on critical minerals supply chain mandates, this positions UUUU as a logical procurement target. The bet is on execution and policy follow-through, not current economics.

3. **The FY2025 debt load of $676M is a fundamental question mark that overrides the strategic narrative until explained.** With revenue of $66M, FCF of $(109)M, and cash of $87M, the company's balance sheet has transformed in a single year. Whether this reflects an acquisition (the Bahia mineral sands project in Brazil [VERIFY]), a streaming deal, or equipment financing will determine whether UUUU is a leveraged option on critical minerals or a value trap. The cash burn trajectory at current rates implies the balance sheet needs active management within 12–18 months.

## 10. Bear Case

**Scenario:** Uranium prices fall below $60/lb sustained as global supply recovers faster than demand. US nuclear capacity additions face congressional funding obstacles or NRC licensing delays. Energy Fuels fails to secure domestic uranium supply contracts at economic prices.

**Implied Bear Price:** $4 (~94% downside from ~$unreported current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Uranium spot price falls below $60/lb for two consecutive quarters.** Energy Fuels is a marginal cost producer. Below $60/lb spot, standby mines cannot be economically restarted and the company's strategic inventory becomes a liability rather than an asset.
2. **No new long-term supply contract signed with a US utility within 12 months.** Energy Fuels' thesis requires US utilities to sign domestic uranium contracts as an alternative to foreign supply. If the contracting cycle doesn't materialize, the company remains cash-flow negative indefinitely.
3. **US Congress fails to fund the Advanced Nuclear Fuel Authority or similar domestic uranium reserve program.** Federal support for domestic uranium is a meaningful demand backstop. Legislative failure would remove a key buyer from the market.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Short-term uranium price volatility, normal delays in mill restart timelines, or temporary reduction in rare earth processing volumes.
