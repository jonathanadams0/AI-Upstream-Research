## 2. Context

Oklo is developing the Aurora powerhouse — a liquid metal fast reactor using High-Assay Low-Enriched Uranium (HALEU) fuel. At 15 MW nameplate capacity, it's designed for remote industrial and military applications, and potentially for small data center co-location. Altman is both chairman and a vocal AI-nuclear advocate; OpenAI has been exploring Oklo power as a potential long-term solution.

**The bear case (why it pulled back 65% from highs):** Oklo has zero revenue, NRC licensing approval is years away, and the first Aurora is still in development. At $67 the company is still valued at $8B with no product. The stock was pricing in too much optionality.

**The bull case:** If Oklo licenses and commercializes by 2030-2031, it would be the first commercial small fast reactor in the US. The moat would be extraordinary. At $67, you're buying an option on that outcome.
