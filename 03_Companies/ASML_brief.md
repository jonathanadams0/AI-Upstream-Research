## Summary

## Key Developments (May – June 2026)

**EUV demand acceleration** — Customers increasing short- and medium-term demand. Advanced logic (TSMC N2/N1A for AI chips) and advanced DRAM (for HBM) are both pulling harder on EUV capacity. CEO: "We see end-market dynamics supporting a shift in product mix towards more demand for our advanced lithography products and an increase in litho intensity."

**High-NA progress** — ASML is targeting 18nm line/space for Logic and 28nm contact pitch for DRAM with High-NA EUV. High-NA can support at least 3 nodes in Logic and DRAM before a new generation is needed. Customers are beginning to test High-NA on product wafers — HVM transition is approaching.

**Export control buffer** — ASML built "bandwidth" into its €36-40B guidance specifically to accommodate potential adverse export control outcomes. The company does not require incremental China access to deliver at the low end of guidance. This framing reduces export control as a binary risk.

**Installed base management** — Service and upgrade revenue from existing EUV installed base is growing "significantly" in 2026. This recurring revenue layer is increasingly important as the EUV fleet expands.

## Why Hold vs. Buy

| Bull Case | Bear Case |
|---|---|
| AI chip demand → higher EUV intensity per wafer | Stock at 52-wk high, above consensus mean PT |
| Non-EUV DUV recovery is incremental upside | 3 Sell ratings among covering analysts |
| High-NA transition creates next multi-year upcycle | China export risk (DUV tightening) is a binary downside |
| Installed base services growing structurally | €40B 2030 target requires sustained execution |

At €1,726 (~$1,726 US listed), the stock trades essentially at the consensus mean PT of $1,694. The EUV monopoly is unassailable and the 2030 revenue range (€44-60B guidance) supports a multi-year bull case. But current price leaves little margin of safety. We'd be buyers below $1,600 (roughly 10% pullback), where the Hold becomes a Buy.
