## 2. Context

FuelCell Energy (FCEL) uses molten carbonate fuel cell technology — different from Bloom Energy's solid oxide approach:
- **Bloom (SOFC):** Higher efficiency, faster startup, runs on natural gas or hydrogen, good for grid-independent power
- **FuelCell (MCFC):** Lower temperature, better for large-scale base load, can capture CO₂ simultaneously

**The AI data center thesis:** Like Bloom, FCEL positions fuel cells as a solution to the grid interconnection queue problem. FCEL markets its technology specifically for "infrastructure-grade" data center power.

**Financial risk:** FCEL has historically been a pre-profitability company. The revenue ramp needed to sustain the current valuation is significant. Bloom's advantage is having Oracle and Nebius contracts that make the thesis concrete. FCEL needs similar hyperscaler validation.
