## 2. Thesis (3 Bullets)

1. **HBM is a structural, not cyclical, windfall for Lam.** High Bandwidth Memory — the on-package DRAM stacked atop every AI accelerator from Nvidia's H100/H200/B200 to AMD MI300X — requires dramatically more etch steps per wafer than conventional DRAM (multiple additional through-silicon via etch steps per HBM layer; exact step count varies by generation — verify from investor day or published process flows). Lam holds approximately 45–55% global etch market share (confirmed: CEO Tim Archer June 2026 conference; SAM growing from low-30s% to high-30s% of WFE), meaning every incremental HBM wafer start flows disproportionately through Lam's installed base and new tool orders.

2. **The memory capex upcycle has legs well beyond consensus.** FY2025's 23.5% revenue recovery is the beginning, not the peak: SK Hynix, Samsung, and Micron are all guiding to multi-year HBM capacity ramps while simultaneously needing to upgrade NAND architecture to 200+ layer configurations for AI storage use cases (CEO Archer confirmed $40B NAND upgrade cycle accelerated to end of 2027, Bernstein conference May 2026). Lam's customer concentration in memory means it is the most levered large-cap equipment name to this specific spending vector.

3. **FCF quality and balance sheet optionality are underappreciated.** At $5.4B FCF in FY2025 and a net cash position of $1.8B, Lam has the capacity to aggressively return capital (buybacks, dividends) while simultaneously funding R&D to defend its moat in next-generation deposition and etch — a combination that should compress the multiple discount the market has historically applied versus logic-exposed peers like AMAT and KLAC. FY2025 capital returns confirmed: $3.42B buybacks + $1.15B dividends = $4.57B total (Q4 FY2025 earnings release, 12M ended June 29, 2025) — representing ~85% of FY2025 FCF.

## 3. AI Exposure

**Classification: Direct**

### AI-Driven Demand Linkage
Lam's exposure to AI infrastructure is primarily through memory — specifically:

- **HBM (High Bandwidth Memory):** Every AI GPU/accelerator requires HBM stacks. HBM production is etch-intensive; Lam is the market leader in DRAM etch (~45–55% global etch share per CEO commentary Jun 2026). As AI accelerator shipments scale, HBM demand scales in lockstep, and each new HBM generation (HBM3, HBM3E, HBM4) requires more layers and more etch steps per wafer.
- **NAND for AI storage:** Large-scale AI inference deployments (hyperscaler data centers, edge AI) are driving sustained NAND demand for both training data storage and model serving. Lam is the leading provider of high-aspect-ratio etch for 3D NAND (CEO confirmed; $40B NAND upgrade cycle accelerated to end of 2027 per Bernstein conference May 2026).
- **Logic/Advanced Packaging (secondary):** Lam has growing exposure to advanced packaging etch used in chiplet and CoWoS configurations relevant to AI accelerators. Advanced packaging revenue crossed **$1B in calendar year 2024** and management guided to **triple to >$3B in calendar 2025** (Zacks/Lam IR, September 2025). Key product: SABRE 3D copper plating systems for 3D HBM stacking and through-silicon via (TSV) applications. Advanced packaging is explicitly listed in FY2025 10-K as a demand driver alongside gate-all-around transitions and 200+ layer NAND.

### AI-Linked Revenue Estimate
Not separately quantified in Lam's public disclosures. Analyst estimate pending — key input will be HBM wafer start forecasts × estimated Lam tool content per wafer start. As a rough frame: if HBM represents ~15–20% of DRAM wafer starts and Lam commands ~50% etch share in DRAM, the AI-linked etch revenue contribution is material and growing. *(Verify if any HBM-specific revenue disclosure was made in FY2025 10-K or earnings call.)*

### Durability Assessment
**Structural.** The HBM ramp is not a one-quarter inventory restocking cycle — it is a multi-year architectural shift in how memory is packaged for compute. Each new AI chip generation pulls forward more HBM volume at higher stack heights. NAND's role in AI storage is similarly durable. This is not 2021 crypto/consumer-PC demand; it is datacenter-led capex with 5-10 year visibility.

### Management Commentary
Confirmed: CEO Tim Archer (Bernstein conference, May 31, 2026): 'AI-driven demand reshapes the semiconductor industry.' Lam now expects WFE of ~$140B 'with a bias to the upside.' NAND and HBM are both cited as demand drivers.

## 5. Industry & Competitive Position

### Market Position
Lam Research is the global #1 player in its core product categories:
- **Plasma Etch:** ~45–55% global market share — dominant, with particular strength in memory/DRAM applications (consistent with CEO commentary on SAM share).
- **CVD/ALD Deposition:** Competes with Applied Materials ([[AMAT]]) and Tokyo Electron ([[TOELY]]); Lam strongest in certain CVD and ALD sub-categories (particularly selective CVD and certain ALD applications). *(Verify specific market share data from investor day materials.)*

### Moat Sources
1. **Process recipe lock-in:** Customers qualify Lam tools at the process level; switching costs are extraordinarily high once a fab has qualified a recipe for production. This is the deepest moat in the equipment industry.
2. **Installed base scale:** A massive global installed base generates services/spares revenue and creates a self-reinforcing data advantage for process optimization.
3. **Memory domain expertise:** Decades of focused development specifically for DRAM and NAND architectures creates application-specific know-how that generalist competitors cannot replicate quickly.
4. **Customer co-development relationships:** Lam engineers are embedded at major fabs, co-developing next-generation process modules. *(Standard industry practice; verify from investor day or annual report.)*

### Porter's Five Forces (Brief)
- **Supplier Power:** Moderate. Lam depends on specialized components (RF generators, ceramic parts, precision machined parts) but has multiple qualified vendors for most inputs. *(Verify key sole-source supplier risks from FY2025 10-K risk factors.)*
- **Buyer Power:** High concentration (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron collectively represent a large % of revenue; FY2025 10-K Note 19 names Samsung and TSMC as significant customers, each ≥10% of revenue; top-5 >65%) but switching costs are extreme — buyers have leverage on pricing but rarely switch tools mid-process node.
- **Threat of New Entry:** Very low. Capital requirements, IP moats, and customer qualification cycles make new entry in leading-edge etch/deposition essentially impossible on any near-term horizon.
- **Threat of Substitutes:** Low for current architectures. Emerging computing paradigms (photonics, neuromorphic) could eventually reduce silicon etch intensity — not a material near-term risk.
- **Industry Rivalry:** Concentrated oligopoly — AMAT, Lam, TEL control the majority of the WFE market. Competition is intense but rational; price wars are uncommon given switching costs.

## 9. Bear Case

**Scenario:** Wafer Fab Equipment (WFE) spending cycle enters a multi-year contraction as memory chip oversupply suppresses DRAM and NAND capex. HBM TSV etch demand plateaus as next-generation HBM designs require different equipment configurations. China export control escalation removes a key growth market.

**Implied Bear Price:** $200 (~40% downside from ~$335 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **WFE industry consensus spending estimate revised down more than 15% by SEMI or major analyst firms.** WFE is a cyclical industry. Any industry-level guidance cut at Gartner, SEMI, or VLSI Research signals a multi-quarter headwind that directly flows through to Lam's equipment orders.
2. **Memory customer (Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron) capex cuts in a single quarter exceed 20% YoY.** HBM TSV etch is Lam's highest-margin, fastest-growing product line. It's tied directly to memory manufacturer capex. A simultaneous capex reduction from two of the three major memory producers would be a thesis test.
3. **China revenue share drops below 20% AND alternative market growth doesn't offset.** China has been 30-40% of WFE revenue. Progressive export control tightening has compressed this. If China drops below 20% without commensurate growth in Korea/Taiwan/US advanced packaging, the net impact is negative.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single quarter of order timing lumpiness, temporary service revenue dip, or inventory correction at one memory customer.
