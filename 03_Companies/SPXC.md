## 2. Thesis

1. **Margin inflection is real and structural, not a one-year anomaly.** EBITDA margins expanded from 6.5% in 2022 to 20.6% in 2025 — a move consistent with a genuine business mix shift toward higher-value HVAC/cooling products and away from lower-margin legacy segments, not simply volume leverage. 2. **CapEx acceleration ($16M → $92M over three years) signals management conviction in forward demand.** This is not a company milking its asset base — it is investing ahead of what it believes is a durable demand cycle. The most credible driver is AI-adjacent data center cooling and commercial HVAC retrofit demand, both of which have multi-year runways [VERIFY specific project wins and customer mix].

## 10. Bear Case

**Scenario:** AI data center HVAC cooling market fails to develop at the scale implied by current orders. Industrial detection and measurement end-markets remain weak longer than expected. Core heating and cooling market faces competitive pricing pressure.

**Implied Bear Price:** $150 (~36% downside from ~$234 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **HVAC segment bookings decline more than 20% for two consecutive quarters.** SPX Technologies has been benefiting from elevated HVAC demand tied to data center cooling and industrial heat management. Below 20% bookings growth signals the data center tailwind is maturing.
2. **Detection and measurement segment revenue fails to recover above 5% YoY growth by YE2027.** The non-HVAC segment has been weak. If recovery doesn't materialize within 18 months, the diversification thesis breaks down and the stock reverts to a pure HVAC company multiple.
3. **Two large data center cooling project wins are delayed or cancelled in the same quarter.** SPXC's cooling towers and fluid cooling products have benefited from hyperscaler data center buildouts. Any public project cancellation at a named hyperscaler customer site is a leading indicator.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter order timing lumpiness, normal HVAC seasonality, or temporary supply chain delays on a large project.
