## 2. What Nebius Is

Nebius was spun off from Yandex NV (the Russian search/tech conglomerate) in 2024 as a standalone company, keeping Yandex's substantial international technology assets while the Russia-focused business was sold separately. The company builds:

- **GPU clusters:** Large-scale Nvidia GPU infrastructure for AI training and inference
- **Cloud platform:** Developer tools, storage, databases optimized for AI workloads
- **On-premise AI:** Enterprise AI infrastructure deployments

**Why European hyperscalers need Nebius:** US-based AI clouds (AWS, Azure, GCP) are subject to US data sovereignty and export control concerns. European enterprises and AI companies increasingly prefer European-domiciled AI infrastructure. Nebius is uniquely positioned as a full-stack, European AI cloud at scale.

## 6. Bear Case

**Scenario:** European AI cloud market fails to develop scale independent of US hyperscaler infrastructure. Nebius loses GPU allocation priority as Nvidia partnership proves insufficient against Microsoft Azure/AWS capacity advantages. Revenue growth decelerates below 50% as the market recognizes European AI cloud as a niche rather than a platform.

**Implied Bear Price:** $80 (~68% downside from ~$248 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Revenue growth decelerates below 50% YoY for two consecutive quarters.** NBIS is priced at 200%+ revenue growth (Q1 FY2026: +684% YoY). The stock's premium multiple requires sustained triple-digit growth. Any deceleration below 50% represents a severe multiple compression event at the current price.
2. **Nvidia publicly deprioritizes NBIS for GPU allocation in favor of larger hyperscalers.** The NBIS thesis depends on GPU access parity with AWS/Azure. Any public disclosure suggesting Nvidia is reducing NBIS's allocation queue priority relative to competitors would directly impair the competitive position.
3. **Operating losses widen beyond $300M quarterly without a credible path to profitability.** NBIS is pre-profit. The market is funding a growth story. If quarterly cash burn accelerates beyond $300M with no visible path to breakeven by 2027, the financing risk becomes a thesis issue.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter revenue shortfall vs. elevated expectations, normal GPU delivery timing, or short-term margin pressure from infrastructure buildout.
