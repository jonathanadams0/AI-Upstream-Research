**1. Does the FQ4 guide confirm pricing power into FY27, or is this the cycle top?**
Options market is pricing ~20% and commentary agrees the Q4 outlook matters more than the Q3 number (GuruFocus, Jun 9). Guidance was GM ~81% on already-record numbers. A FQ4 guide with GM flat-or-up plus 2027 supply commentary ("sold out") keeps the structural-change narrative (UBS: durable margins/ROE) intact. Sequential GM decline = cyclicality risk (#2 in note) reasserting.

**2. Where does Micron stand on HBM4 vs. SK Hynix and Samsung?**
Both competitors are ramping HBM4 (note risk #1). Watch: HBM4 qualification/sampling timeline at Nvidia and AMD, HBM share targets, 2027 HBM order-book extension, and whether long-term customer agreements (first deal spans 5 years, per UBS) are expanding. This is the thesis spine — Micron as third supplier but the only US-based one.

**3. Is >$25B capex discipline or overbuild — and what is the Idaho ramp status?**
FY26 capex was raised to >$25B on the Q2 call. Watch capex trajectory for FY27, Idaho fab timeline (note risk #4: schedule risk on 2026–2028 ramp), and any China commentary (risk #3: 2023-style restrictions could recur). Capex acceleration *into* a pricing peak is the classic memory-cycle sell signal; capex matched against sold-out HBM supply is not.
