# Intel Corporation (INTC)

> The world's most ambitious industrial turnaround. Under CEO Lip-Bu Tan (March 2025), Intel has delivered 6 consecutive earnings beats, secured $8.9B from the US government, attracted $5B from NVIDIA and $2B from SoftBank, and — on the day of this initiation (June 18, 2026) — had its foundry ambitions validated by a Trump-announced Apple chip deal using Intel's 18A process. At $121 and a ~$590B market cap, the stock has tripled in 12 months and is pricing in the full foundry thesis. Hold: turnaround is real, valuation reflects it.

## 1. Investment Thesis

**Hold (not Buy) because:**
1. Probability-weighted EV ~$106 (12% below current price); risk/reward is +82% bull / −50% bear — asymmetric but negative expected value from $121
2. Stock has tripled (+360% TTM) and is at >100x FY2026E Non-GAAP EPS and ~73x FY2027E — pricing in the full foundry thesis
3. FCF remains deeply negative (−$3.1B TTM), $45B debt, 18A yields industry-standard only in 2027

**Upgrade to Buy if:**
- Stock pulls back to $95–105 (same thesis, better entry)
- Apple deal formally confirmed in 8-K AND scales beyond 20M units
- Any hyperscaler (NVIDIA, Google, Meta, Amazon) confirms foundry order on 18A or 14A
- FCF turns positive on TTM basis

## 3. The Apple Deal (June 18, 2026 — Live Event)

Trump announced today that Apple will use Intel's 18A process for entry-level M-series chips:
- **Volume:** 15–20 million units/year (starting 2027)
- **Revenue impact:** ~$500M–$1B/year by 2028
- **Strategic significance:** First major external 18A customer; validates the foundry thesis
- **Caveats:** Neither Apple nor Intel formally confirmed; Apple uses TSMC for 80%+ of production; this is a second-source test, not TSMC displacement
- **Market reaction:** INTC +9% on the news

## 7. Key Risks

1. **18A yields miss 2027 target** — Most important near-term risk; if yields don't reach competitive levels, Apple can't scale and foundry thesis collapses
2. **AMD Epyc structural share gains** — Xeon 6 gains may be cyclical; Zen 5 "Turin" in production; CEO Tan admitted "we lost data center leadership, I'm trying to bring that back"
3. **FCF remains negative through 2027** — $45B debt + $12B+ annual capex; requires revenue growth to cover
4. **NVIDIA investment stays financial** — $5B is a hedge, not an order; foundry depends on conversion to actual wafer starts
