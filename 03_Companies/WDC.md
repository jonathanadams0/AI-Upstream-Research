# Western Digital (WDC)

> Post-Sandisk spinoff, Western Digital is a pure-play hard disk drive (HDD) manufacturer. The AI cold storage thesis — petabytes of training data requiring the cheapest-per-TB storage medium — keeps HDDs relevant longer than the secular decline narrative suggested.

**Sector:** [[Memory-Storage]] | **Rating:** Hold | **PT:** $560 | **Current:** ~$537 | **Upside:** +4%

---

## 2. Thesis

1. **AI cold storage demand is real.** Training datasets for frontier models exceed petabytes. At current NAND prices, storing a 1PB training corpus on SSDs costs ~$200,000; on HDDs it costs ~$20,000. HDDs remain essential for capacity-tier AI storage.

2. **HAMR drives extend HDD relevance.** Heat-Assisted Magnetic Recording (HAMR) enables 30-50+ TB per drive — roughly 3x the capacity of current technology. Seagate and WDC are both ramping HAMR, enabling higher areal density and lower cost per TB.

3. **Post-spinoff re-rating.** Freed from the NAND business, WDC can be valued purely on HDD free cash flow. HDD is a duopoly (WDC + Seagate) with rational pricing.

## 3. Hold Rationale

At $537 and $185B market cap, the AI cold storage thesis is priced. Hold/$560.
