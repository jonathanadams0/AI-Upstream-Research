# Iron Mountain (IRM)

> The most contrarian data center play in the coverage universe. Iron Mountain began as a document storage company (physical records, film archives, medical records) — and the market still prices it that way. Meanwhile, IRM has been quietly building a $4B+ data center business growing at 30%+ annually, leveraging its existing global real estate footprint to fast-track AI data center development.

**Sector:** [[Data-Center-Infra]] | **Rating:** Buy | **PT:** $145 | **Current:** ~$128 | **Upside:** +13%

---

## 2. Why IRM Is The Most Interesting Data Center Play

1. **Real estate re-purposing.** IRM owns hundreds of warehouse-grade facilities globally where it stores physical documents. These facilities have power infrastructure, loading docks, and established utility relationships — all of which fast-track data center conversion. Building a greenfield data center in the US takes 2-4 years; converting an existing IRM facility takes 12-18 months.

2. **Document storage is a cash cow funding the pivot.** IRM's legacy document storage business generates stable, high-margin recurring revenue. This funds the data center investment program without external financing stress — unlike speculative data center pure-plays.

3. **The market hasn't re-rated the pivot.** At $128 with ~$37B market cap, IRM trades at a discount to both pure-play data center REITs (EQIX, DLR) and to the blended value of its document storage + data center businesses. As data center becomes a larger percentage of revenue, the multiple should converge toward data center REIT comparables.

4. **Government and regulated sector customer base.** IRM's existing customers include US federal agencies, healthcare systems, and financial institutions — exactly the customers most likely to want private, dedicated AI data center capacity.

---

## 3. Investment View

Buy/$145 (+13% upside). The combination of an underappreciated data center growth business, legacy cash flow funding the buildout, and a discount to comparable REITs makes IRM the highest-conviction data center name in our coverage.

## 4. Bear Case

**Scenario:** Data center pre-leasing rate declines below 70% as new supply outpaces demand. Digital transformation revenue fails to grow fast enough to offset the structural decline in physical document storage. REIT sector de-rating from rising interest rates.

**Implied Bear Price:** $100 (~21% downside from ~$127 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Data center pre-lease rate falls below 70% on any new major campus.** IRM's data center expansion depends on pre-leasing to underwrite construction. Below 70% pre-lease indicates the market is softening and economics are weakening.
2. **Physical storage revenue declines more than 12% YoY for two consecutive quarters.** The thesis requires data center revenue growth to exceed physical storage decline. If physical storage falls faster than 12% — suggesting accelerated shredding/digitization — the crossover timeline shortens.
3. **REIT sector multiple compression reduces implied cap rates above 7%.** IRM is priced as a data center REIT, not a storage REIT. If cap rates for data center REITs decompress above 7% from rising rates, the entire sector de-rates and IRM follows regardless of operational performance.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter occupancy dip, normal physical storage volume seasonality, or temporary data center construction delays.
