## 2. Thesis (3 Bullets)

1. **Nuclear fleet being repriced from commodity to contracted.** 20-year PPAs with Meta (2,609 MW PJM, Jan 2026) and AWS (1,200 MW ERCOT, Sep 2025) have converted ~3,800 MW from merchant exposure to locked-in contracted revenue at prices well above historical merchant averages. Market still prices VST at merchant multiples (~9.7x EBITDA) vs. contracted/nuclear peers at 16x.

2. **ERCOT structural demand shift + Cogentrix = capacity scarcity value.** Data center load growth in ERCOT has reaccelerated to 3-5% annually (vs. ~1% historical). VST is the dominant ERCOT generator. Cogentrix acquisition (5,500 MW gas, H2 2026 close) expands the fleet by ~14% at mid-teens levered returns. Each MWh of firm capacity is becoming increasingly scarce and valuable as load growth outpaces new supply.

3. **Investment grade + FCFbG of $4.3B = deleveraging and re-rating flywheel.** Dual IG ratings (S&P 2025, Fitch May 2026) reduce cost of capital, eliminate restrictive covenants, and create a path to refinancing ~$19B at materially lower coupons. At $4.3B FCFbG midpoint, VST can deleverage $2–3B per year after dividends/buybacks — supporting a gradual re-rating as the balance sheet strengthens.

## 3. AI Exposure

**Classification:** Direct
**AI-linked contracted capacity:** ~3,800 MW (Meta PPA 2,609 MW + AWS PPA 1,200 MW), ~10% of total generation portfolio under direct hyperscaler contracts

- **Direct drivers:** 20-year nuclear PPAs with Meta (PJM: Perry, Davis-Besse, Beaver Valley) and AWS (ERCOT: Comanche Peak). Data center load growth driving ERCOT price floors upward. Cogentrix gas fleet as "bridge power" for data centers under construction.
- **Quantification:** Meta deal = 2,176 MW operating + 433 MW uprates (largest corporate-supported nuclear uprate in US history). AWS deal = 1,200 MW at Comanche Peak. Combined: ~3,800 MW contracted. Delivery begins late 2026, ramps through 2034.
- **Durability:** Nuclear power is the only 24/7 zero-carbon source available at scale today. AI requires firm dispatchable baseload — no substitute exists. 20-year contract terms with no technology substitution risk.
- **Management commentary (Q1'26 call):** "High levels of interest from customers on all different arrangements, including co-location with existing, new build, co-location with nuclear plants, renewables PPAs, bridge power solutions, and front-of-the-meter retail deals. Vistra is uniquely positioned with our large fleet of dispatchable assets and our leading retail, commercial, and regulatory capabilities to serve these customer needs."

## 4. Business Overview

### Segments (FY2026E)
- **Texas (ERCOT):** ~45% of EBITDA. Includes ~15,000 MW gas + coal + Comanche Peak nuclear (2,300 MW) + TXU Energy retail. Weather-sensitive but structurally supported by ERCOT demand growth.
- **East (PJM + others):** ~35% of EBITDA. Perry, Davis-Besse, Beaver Valley, Braidwood nuclear + gas. Meta PPA covers 2,609 MW starting 2026–27.
- **Retail:** Integrated across Texas and East. ~4.3M customers across 20 states (TXU Energy, Dynegy, Homefield, Ambit, others). Natural hedge vs. wholesale price volatility.
- **West / Sunset / Asset Closure:** Smaller. Legacy coal assets winding down on defined timeline.

### Geography
- Texas (ERCOT) = largest market; PJM/Mid-Atlantic = second; Midwest and West = smaller exposure

### Key Customers
- Meta Platforms (20-year nuclear PPA, 2,609 MW, PJM)
- AWS / Amazon (20-year nuclear PPA, 1,200 MW, ERCOT)
- ~4.3 million retail electricity customers (residential + C&I)

### Revenue Model
- Merchant power sales (spot + forward hedges)
- Capacity market payments (PJM RPM, ERCOT ancillary)
- Contracted PPA revenues (growing — Meta + AWS)
- Retail electricity margin

## 11. Bear Case

**Scenario:** Cogentrix acquisition fails to close or is materially repriced. Meta or AWS PPA faces early termination clause invocation. ERCOT market structure change caps merchant returns. No multiple re-rating from 9.7x as contracted book grows.

**Implied Bear Price:** ~$105 (≈ −36% from $163.73) — **re-underwritten 2026-06-23.** The prior $145 figure was a placeholder (only ~4% below the old entry) and is retired.

**Downside math:** Bear adj. EBITDA **$6.5B** — below the $6.8–7.6B 2026E guide low end, reflecting mild ERCOT weather *and* Cogentrix excluded/repriced. At **9x** EV/EBITDA (the current merchant multiple, no re-rating): EV ≈ $58.5B − net debt $19.2B − preferred $2.5B = equity ≈ $36.8B ÷ ~330M diluted shares ≈ **$112**. A deeper de-rate to **8x** (thesis breaks, market re-rates merchant IPPs down) gives ≈ **$92**. Midpoint ≈ **$105**; defensible range **$90–112**.

**Asymmetry:** at the $105 bear, upside-to-PT ($66/sh to $230) ÷ downside-to-bear ($59/sh) ≈ **1.1x** — *not* the 13.1x previously carried in [[Position Sizing Framework]] (that number used the too-tight $145 bear). VST is a legitimate Tier-2 hold, not the book's asymmetry standout; the genuine 2x names are [[CEG]] and [[NRG]]. Per the rebalancing rules, VST becomes an **upsize-to-Tier-1 candidate only near the bear** (within 10% ≈ **$115.50**) or on a PJM clear >$325 with Cogentrix closed (see [[Catalyst Playbooks]]).

**Tier scoring (4-factor matrix, re-run 2026-06-23 at $163.73):** Conviction High = 3; Asymmetry ~1.1x (1.0–2.0x band) = 2; Variant perception = 1 (consensus is *aligned* with our $230 PT — a timing/conviction edge, not a differentiated target, unlike CEG/NRG); Catalyst clarity = 3 (PJM ~weeks + Q2 Aug 7, both <90 days). **Total = 9 → Tier 2 (confirmed, no change).** Price sensitivity: at ~$115 (the upsize trigger) asymmetry ≈ 11x → 3 pts → total 10 → Tier 1, which is why the within-10%-of-bear add is justified. The other Tier-1 path is event-gated (PJM >$325 + Cogentrix close → higher EBITDA base and PT).

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Cogentrix fails to close.** Regulatory approval (FERC or state PUC) blocked or deal renegotiated at materially higher price. Management has guided H2 2026 close; any extension past Q1 2027 signals deal risk.
2. **Meta or AWS PPA renegotiated.** Both are 20-year contracts. Any disclosure of renegotiation, force majeure invocation, or load reduction would materially impair the contracted earnings narrative.
3. **Net debt/EBITDA does not track toward 2.3x by YE2027.** Management's own projection requires $10B+ cash generation through 2027. If capex overruns or EBITDA misses push that ratio above 3x, the IG credit thesis weakens.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter ERCOT weather miss, mild Texas winter, or GAAP net income volatility from mark-to-market.
