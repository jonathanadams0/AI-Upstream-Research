# Lumentum Holdings (LITE)

> The #6 best-performing stock in the S&P 500 YTD. Lumentum makes the laser chips and photonic components inside every major optical transceiver used in AI data centers. Where Coherent (COHR) makes complete transceiver modules, Lumentum makes the laser source inside them — a higher-margin, more defensible position in the optical supply chain.

## 2. Thesis

1. **Every optical transceiver needs a laser.** Lumentum is the dominant supplier of indium phosphide (InP) laser chips — the light source inside 400G/800G/1.6T optical transceivers. Coherent, II-VI, Fabrinet all buy Lumentum chips. As transceiver volumes for AI clusters scale, so does Lumentum's laser chip demand.

2. **Co-packaged optics is the next wave.** The industry is moving toward co-packaged optics (CPO) — where the laser and transceiver are integrated directly onto the switch chip, eliminating the pluggable module. Lumentum's silicon photonics and InP laser capabilities are essential for CPO. Every switch vendor (Broadcom, Marvell, Cisco) is working on CPO; Lumentum is a critical supplier for all of them.

3. **Telecom + AI = two tailwinds.** Lumentum also supplies lasers for telecom networks (5G backhaul, long-haul fiber). The telecom recovery plus AI interconnect creates a double demand cycle.

## 4. Bear Case

**Scenario:** AI optical transceiver demand cycle peaks earlier than expected as hyperscalers complete initial 400G/800G buildouts and new order cadence slows. Co-package optics transition displaces traditional pluggable transceivers faster than Lumentum can pivot its product mix. Stock down -12% in a single day signals sentiment fragility.

**Implied Bear Price:** $600 (~33% downside from ~$901 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **Revenue guidance cut more than 10% in a single quarter.** LITE's stock is priced for sustained 30%+ growth in AI optical. Any downward revision to forward guidance — especially if framed as hyperscaler order pause rather than timing — is a thesis-level event.
2. **Coherent Corp wins material market share in 400G/800G transceivers at two or more top-3 hyperscalers.** The optical transceiver market is a share battle between LITE, COHR, and smaller vendors. Any competitive displacement at a key account needs to be tracked through quarterly disclosures.
3. **Gross margin drops below 40% for two consecutive quarters.** Current ~43% gross margin reflects product mix premium for AI-optimized transceivers. Margin compression signals either pricing pressure or a mix shift toward lower-ASP products.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter customer inventory digestion, normal order timing lumpiness, or stock volatility from the broader semiconductor sector.
