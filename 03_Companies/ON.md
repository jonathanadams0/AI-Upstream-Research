## 2. Thesis

> **⚠️ Thesis status: DISRUPTED (not invalidated) — 2026-06-29.** The Synaptics acquisition ($7B all-stock, 1.35x exchange ratio, close mid-2027) is a strategic pivot to "physical AI platform" — ON's SiC business is intact but the company's identity is changing. Original bear triggers (EV volume, inventory correction, SiC displacement) did **not** fire. The disruption is capital allocation and strategic direction, not fundamentals. Rating downgraded to Hold pending deal close and synergy proof.

1. **SiC for AI data center UPS.** AI data centers need uninterruptible power supplies (UPS) rated for 120kW+ per rack. SiC transistors enable more efficient, more compact UPS designs. ON is a primary SiC device supplier.

2. **EV SiC is durable demand.** ON's SiC inverter chips are in multiple EV platforms. EV SiC is a long-cycle, design-win driven business with 5-7 year revenue visibility per win.

3. **Physical AI / edge AI optionality (new — post-Synaptics).** Synaptics' Astra processors (edge AI SoCs) + wireless connectivity + ON's power/sensing = full system solutions for industrial robotics, drones, and autonomous systems. The combined TAM is estimated at $243B by 2030 per management. This is speculative pre-close but is the strategic rationale for the deal.

## 3. Bear Case

**Scenario:** EV penetration growth stalls AND Synaptics integration disappoints simultaneously. The combination of an auto SiC destocking cycle and a failed "physical AI platform" pivot — with no synergy proof and management distracted — would de-rate the combined entity to a trough multiple on compressed earnings.

**Revised Bear Price: $65** (was $80 pre-deal) — the original $80 did not account for $7B M&A dilution + integration overhead. If EV SiC weakens AND Synaptics integration disappoints, trough combined-entity EPS could be ~$3.50–4.30/share at 15–18x = $52–77 range; midpoint $65.

**Change Rating to Sell If (original triggers — still valid):**
1. **EV unit volumes delivered by ON's top-5 automotive customers decline more than 20% YoY.** SiC revenue is tied to EV production volumes. Any sustained reduction in customer EV output (Tesla, BMW, Honda, Hyundai, Ford) is directly reflected in ON's SiC revenue.
2. **Automotive segment revenue guidance reduced more than 15% citing inventory correction.** ON experienced a similar correction in 2022-2023. Any management commentary specifically calling out automotive SiC inventory overbuild is the primary warning signal.
3. **Wolfspeed or STMicro win publicly announced SiC design contracts at two or more of ON's top-5 automotive customers.** Competitive displacement is the structural risk. Track RFQ outcomes at major OEMs through supplier day disclosures and industry reports.

**Change Rating to Sell If (new — M&A trigger):**
4. **Synaptics integration materially underperforms.** $200M synergy target missed by >50% within 24 months of close; SYNA Physical AI revenue fails to ramp; ON management calls out integration cost overruns on earnings calls. This adds a structural overlay to the original three triggers — if #4 fires alongside any of #1–3, the combined bear case deepens well below $65.

**Upgrade back to Buy If:**
- Deal closes on schedule (mid-2027) **and** first post-close quarter demonstrates synergy realization **and** PT rebuilds to ≥$120 with ≥1.5x asymmetry. OR: stock pulls back to $70–75 with confirmed SiC business intact (automotive revenue stable, no inventory correction commentary).

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter EV production lumpiness, normal auto program launch timing, industrial SiC softness unrelated to the EV thesis, or the Synaptics deal closing on schedule without yet showing synergies.
