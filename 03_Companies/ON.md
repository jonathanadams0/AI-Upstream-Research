# ON Semiconductor (ON)

> Silicon carbide (SiC) power devices for AI data center UPS systems, electric vehicles, and industrial power conversion. At $133, ON trades at ~20x forward earnings — a reasonable multiple for a SiC leader with growing AI data center exposure and dominant EV SiC positioning.

## 2. Thesis

1. **SiC for AI data center UPS.** AI data centers need uninterruptible power supplies (UPS) rated for 120kW+ per rack. SiC transistors enable more efficient, more compact UPS designs. ON is a primary SiC device supplier.

2. **EV SiC is durable demand.** ON's SiC inverter chips are in multiple EV platforms. EV SiC is a long-cycle, design-win driven business with 5-7 year revenue visibility per win.

3. **Reasonable valuation.** At 20x forward earnings, ON is not priced for perfection unlike some AI-adjacent semiconductors. Upside to $160 if SiC AI data center demand inflects.

## 3. Bear Case

**Scenario:** EV penetration growth stalls below 25% global market share through 2027, delaying the SiC power electronics content ramp that underpins the thesis. Automotive OEMs accelerate SiC inventory build followed by a sharp destocking cycle. Wolfspeed or STMicro win key design contracts at cost-competitive prices.

**Implied Bear Price:** $80 (~38% downside from ~$130 current)

**Change Rating to Hold/Sell If:**
1. **EV unit volumes delivered by ON's top-5 automotive customers decline more than 20% YoY.** SiC revenue is tied to EV production volumes. Any sustained reduction in customer EV output (Tesla, BMW, Honda, Hyundai, Ford) is directly reflected in ON's SiC revenue.
2. **Automotive segment revenue guidance reduced more than 15% citing inventory correction.** ON experienced a similar correction in 2022-2023. Any management commentary specifically calling out automotive SiC inventory overbuild is the primary warning signal.
3. **Wolfspeed or STMicro win publicly announced SiC design contracts at two or more of ON's top-5 automotive customers.** Competitive displacement is the structural risk. Track RFQ outcomes at major OEMs through supplier day disclosures and industry reports.

**What Would NOT Change the Rating:** Single-quarter EV production lumpiness at one customer, normal automotive program launch timing, or industrial SiC softness unrelated to the core EV thesis.
