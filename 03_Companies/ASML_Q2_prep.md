## What Management Guided for Q2 (from Q1 call, April 15)

**Revenue:** €8.4–€9.0B — midpoint €8.7B, slightly below analyst consensus of €9.1B at time of guidance. The Q2 range is wider than usual, reflecting mix uncertainty between EUV, immersion DUV, and installed base management (service/upgrades).

**Gross margin:** 51–52%, at the lower end of the FY range. This reflects product mix — Q2 skews more toward non-EUV (immersion DUV) which carries lower margins, and some high-NA development costs that don't benefit from scale.

**EUV shipment cadence:** ASML is executing on a plan for ≥60 Low-NA EUV systems in 2026. Q2 shipment count will be watched closely — any slippage vs. quarterly schedule would be a headwind to the FY revenue target.

**Non-EUV DUV growth:** Previously expected to be flat in 2026; now expected to grow. Customers are adding DUV (immersion) capacity alongside EUV, which is the incremental surprise in the FY2026 guidance raise. Q2 non-EUV performance will confirm whether this demand is sustained.

**CEO Q1 commentary (Christophe Fouquet):** "The semiconductor industry's growth outlook continues to solidify, driven by ongoing AI-related infrastructure investments. Demand for chips is outpacing supply. In response, our customers are accelerating their capacity expansion plans for 2026 and beyond, supported by long-term agreements with their customers." ## Key Questions for Q2

**1. EUV unit shipments — on track for ≥60 in 2026?**
ASML guided ≥60 Low-NA EUV in 2026, stepping up to ≥80 in 2027. A quarterly cadence of ~15 units/quarter is needed for ≥60. Any deviation (positive or negative) is market-moving. TSMC and Samsung are both accelerating EUV capacity, which supports shipment pull-forward.

**2. Order intake — sustained or normalizing?**
Q1 order intake was "very strong" per management. Watch for whether bookings remain elevated or begin to moderate. ASML's order book is long-cycle (12-24 months to delivery), so a strong order quarter provides future revenue visibility even if current revenue is in-line.

**3. Non-EUV DUV demand confirmation**
The Q1 surprise that non-EUV would *grow* rather than be flat was driven by customers adding immersion capacity alongside EUV. Q2 should confirm whether this is sustained demand or a one-quarter pull-forward.

**4. China exposure in Q2 mix**
ASML built "bandwidth" into FY guidance to accommodate export control outcomes. The CFO guided that the €36-40B range accommodates scenarios including further China DUV restrictions. Watch for any disclosure of China revenue as a % of mix in Q2 — any reduction would confirm the export control buffer is working as intended.

**5. High-NA maturation**
High-NA EUV is moving from R&D to product-wafer testing at customer fabs. ASML expects High-NA to support at least 3 Logic and DRAM nodes before requiring a successor. Any Q2 update on customer High-NA engagement timelines would be incremental for the long-term thesis.

**6. Installed base management growth**
Service revenue from the expanding EUV installed base is growing "significantly" in 2026. This is the most recurring and predictable part of ASML's model. Q2 should show continued progress — watch for installed base management as a % of total revenue.
