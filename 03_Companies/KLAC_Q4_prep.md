## What Management Guided for Q4 (from Q3 call, April 29)

KLA's Q3 FY2026 was strong: revenue $3.415B (+11% YoY), EPS $9.40 (above guidance midpoint). Q4 guidance is for the strongest quarter of the year at $3.575B revenue — sequential growth. Key details:

**Mix:** Foundry/logic ~82% of SPC systems revenue; memory ~18%. Within memory, DRAM ~84%, NAND ~16%. This is a very logic-heavy mix — any acceleration of DRAM/HBM spending would be additive.

**Advanced packaging:** Revenue expected to reach ~$1B in 2026 (growing from $635M in 2025). Q4 should show this running at ~$250M quarterly run-rate. KLA achieved #1 position in Process Control for Advanced Wafer-Level Packaging in 2025.

**Margin headwind:** Elevated DRAM chip costs for KLA's own image processing computers are creating ~100 bps GM headwind, expected to persist through at least calendar 2026. This is manageable but acknowledged.

**Capital returns:** >90% FCF targeted return. 17th consecutive annual dividend increase announced (Q3 call). $7B incremental buyback authorized.

**2030 targets:** 13-17% revenue CAGR to a WFE market of $215B ±$20B. KLA expects to outperform WFE throughout.

## Key Questions for Q4

**1. SPC intensity acceleration?**
KLA disclosed that design activity, rising memory complexity, custom silicon (hyperscaler chips), and advanced packaging are all driving higher process control intensity per wafer. Q4 mix toward higher foundry/logic should sustain the intensity trend. Watch for any SPC intensity commentary.

**2. Advanced packaging upside?**
CoWoS, SOIC, and emerging SOIC packaging are driving "meaningful revenue increases across more advanced systems." If AI GPU demand causes TSMC to accelerate CoWoS capacity addition, KLA benefits directly. Watch for $1B packaging revenue confirmation.

**3. DRAM/HBM uptick?**
Memory is only 18% of mix currently — low by historical standards. If SK Hynix, Samsung, or Micron pull forward HBM capacity additions (which multiple sources indicate), DRAM intensity and KLA's memory revenue should step up in H2 2026 and into 2027. Q4 commentary on memory customer capex will be closely watched.

**4. 2027 guidance framework?**
KLA typically provides initial next fiscal year color in the Q4 call. Given the investor day 2030 model (13-17% CAGR), Q4 management commentary on 2027 trajectory will be market-moving. Any upward revision to near-term expectations would be catalytic for a Hold → Buy re-evaluation.

## Why Hold at Current Price

The stock at $2,125 is 17% above the consensus mean PT of $1,819. Even accounting for conservative analyst modeling (as Lam's CEO noted, "everybody in equipment is going to do well"), KLA at 28x forward EV/EBITDA is pricing in the upside scenario already. The process control monopoly is exceptional and the 2030 financial model is compelling, but there is limited margin of safety at current levels.

**Hold becomes Buy below $1,800** (roughly 10% pullback), where the stock would be near consensus and the long-term compounding model is buying-quality.
